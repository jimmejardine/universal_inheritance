# Personal Conversations: Persuasion One Person at a Time

**Executive summary.** Every other file in this section addresses audiences; this one addresses the two conversations every supporter must eventually have in person — with their own child, who would forfeit an above-threshold inheritance, and with friends, who may quietly conclude you have lost your mind. The field-experiment literature says these conversations are not a sideshow: arguments delivered at strangers' doors move nothing, but a single non-judgemental, story-exchanging conversation durably shifts attitudes [5][6] — and inheritance-tax opinion is the *most* movable by a single fact of any tax ever tested [1].

## Findings

- The arithmetic a supporter's child actually faces: up to **£500,000 from each parent's side tax-free**, plus the **£50k endowment at 25**, plus citizenship of the country the policy builds. What is forfeited is only the excess above the threshold — the child keeps more than roughly 90% of heirs will ever see ([root README](../README.md), [02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)).
- The parental case is evidence-based, not sacrificial: large inheritances measurably push recipients out of the labour force (the "Carnegie Conjecture" finding [4]; [03-case-for/heirs-burden.md](../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md)), and the *expectation* of a fortune distorts careers, mortgages autonomy to a revocable will, and makes competence unprovable ([03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md](../03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md)). A parent can truthfully say: capping your inheritance is something I am doing *for* you.
- Buffett said it in one sentence — leave children "enough money so that they would feel they could do anything, but not so much that they could do nothing" [3] — and the threshold *is* that sentence as legislation ([00-capitalism/capitalist-supporters.md](../00-capitalism/capitalist-supporters.md)).
- The persuasion evidence favours conversation over broadcast: in Kalla & Broockman's three field experiments, canvassing conversations deploying *arguments alone had no effect*, while otherwise-identical conversations that added a non-judgemental exchange of personal narratives durably reduced hostile attitudes for at least four months [6]; a single ~10-minute perspective-taking conversation produced attitude change lasting at least three months and resistant to counterargument [5].
- Opinion on this specific tax is unusually soft: telling people that only 4.62% of UK deaths trigger any inheritance tax [2] is the kind of single fact that, in Kuziemko et al., more than doubled support for raising the estate tax — the largest information effect measured for any tax [1] ([06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)). One good conversation genuinely can move someone.
- The ordering doctrine ([README.md](README.md)) applies at the kitchen table exactly as on television: benefit before mechanism, £50k and the £500k allowance before any mention of "100%".
- All scripts and tactics below are **judgement — suggested language, not evidence**. The evidence citations mark what may honestly be *claimed inside* them.

## Part A — Telling your own child

### 1. The arithmetic first, always

Before any philosophy, the numbers — because the imagined loss is nearly always larger than the real one:

- They can still receive **up to £500,000 from you entirely tax-free** — on IFS-based figures, that leaves roughly 90% of heirs receiving everything they would ever have inherited, untouched ([root README](../README.md), [02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)).
- They receive the **£50,000 endowment at 25** like every other citizen ([02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md)) — and so does every child they ever have.
- They live in the country the policy builds: less dynastic capture, cheaper assets at the margin, competitors' untested heirs no longer handed empires ([01-problem/](../01-problem/README.md), [16-second-order/](../16-second-order/README.md)).
- What they forfeit is **only the excess above £500k per recipient** — the slice that converts provision into rank.

The honest one-line frame (judgement): *"You lose a fortune you didn't earn; you keep more than 90% of heirs will ever see."*

### 2. What the evidence lets a parent honestly say

This is the conversation where the repo's heir-harm evidence stops being rhetoric and becomes parenting. A parent can truthfully claim, with citations:

| Claim you can make to your child | Evidence behind it |
|---|---|
| "Very large inheritances measurably make people stop building" | Holtz-Eakin, Joulfaian & Rosen: recipients of ~$150k inheritances were roughly four times as likely to exit the labour force as recipients of under $25k [4] ([heirs-burden](../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md) §4) |
| "Even *expecting* the money warps a life — careers chosen in its shadow, adulthood spent on the leash of a revocable will, achievements you can never prove were yours" | [expectant-heirs.md](../03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md) §§1–3 — mechanism and clinical observation, honestly flagged there as weaker than the post-receipt record |
| "The richest people who ever lived agree" | Carnegie: enormous wealth "deadens the talents and energies of the son" ([heirs-burden](../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md) §1); Buffett: enough to do anything, not so much as to do nothing [3] |
| "The cap frees you *now*, not at probate" | The tax binds expectations at announcement — the career must carry the life, and the will stops being an instrument of control ([expectant-heirs.md](../03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md) §7) |

The crucial honesty rule: do not oversell. The anticipation literature is thin and says so in the source file; the claim is "the evidence points this way," not "science proves inheritance ruins you." Overclaiming to your own child costs you the one audience whose trust you cannot re-recruit.

### 3. Age-appropriate framings *(scripts — judgement, suggested language only)*

**Young child (roughly 6–11).** No policy, no numbers — just the value the policy encodes, planted early: things you make are yours; things handed to you are luck; luck is for sharing.

> *"In our family the rule is: what you build is yours, and I'll always make sure you have a real start. But nobody in this family gets to skip the building part — that's the fun part, and I'm not letting anyone take it from you."*

**Teenager.** Teenagers can handle the numbers and are the age group most alert to unfairness among their friends; recruit that instinct rather than fighting it.

> *"Here's what's actually on the table: you'd still get up to half a million from us tax-free, plus £50,000 at 25 that every single one of your friends gets too. The only thing that goes is the part that would have made you a different species from them. You've met trust-fund kids. Do you want to be the person people wonder about — 'would she have got that on her own?' — for the rest of your life?"*

**Adult child.** Adults deserve the full ledger: the arithmetic, the evidence, and the admission that you are asking them to give up something real.

> *"I want to be straight with you: if this passes, you'd inherit less than you otherwise would — the excess above £500k goes. I'm not pretending that's nothing. But look at what the evidence says big inheritances do to the people who get them — the careers not attempted, the question mark over everything you achieve — and look at what you keep: half a million tax-free, £50k at 25, and a country where your own kids compete on talent instead of on grandparents. I'd rather leave you a fair country and a provable life than a fortune and an asterisk."*

### 4. Not victims — founders

The identity on offer to a supporter's child is not "heir who lost out" but **first-generation founder of the new settlement** — the same move the campaign makes with wealthy defectors generally ([13-movement/messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md)). The models already exist:

- **Marlene Engelhorn**, the BASF heiress who handed €25m to a citizens' council in 2024 while demanding "tax me properly, don't make me rely on my own goodwill" — the archetype whose every attack line inverts [7] ([10-opposition/personal-attacks.md](../10-opposition/personal-attacks.md) §"The Engelhorn model").
- **Resource Generation**, which organises precisely the demographic in question — young people with wealth — around redistribution as self-liberation, not sacrifice [8] ([03-case-for/heirs-burden.md](../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md) §6).

A child of supporters who embraces the policy is one of the campaign's most credible possible messengers — the endowment-generation advocate who can say "yes, this costs my family the most, and I want it anyway" ([13-movement/recruitment.md](../13-movement/recruitment.md), [13-movement/messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md)). Being early to a just settlement is a legacy no above-threshold bequest can buy — the same legacy trade offered to billionaires in [messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md) §"Converting billionaires", available to your own children at a much lower price.

### 5. "Why should *our* family go first?" — the honest answer

This is the strongest objection your child can raise, and it deserves the campaign's real answer, not a deflection:

1. **Nobody is asked to disarm unilaterally.** Supporting the law is not the same as renouncing the money today. The movement's instrument is the **pledge-conditional commitment** — the statutory-pledge logic of [13-movement/messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md) §"Converting billionaires": endorse in law what applies to everyone simultaneously; commitments activate at law-change, when every rival dynasty's excess is redirected on the same day. Until then, your family's position is unchanged. As Carnegie's own answer runs: one family giving voluntarily changes nothing; a rule changes the game for everyone ([messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md), disclosure-first doctrine).
2. **Advocacy is the cheap, high-leverage act.** What the family "goes first" with is its voice and its credibility — the costly-signal value described in [messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md) — not its assets.
3. **Concede the residual.** If the law passes, your child genuinely receives less than heirs of equivalent families in non-adopting countries, and less than they would have under the status quo. That is real, it is the price of the position, and pretending otherwise insults their intelligence. The honest close (judgement): *"Yes — someone's family goes first, or nobody does. I'd rather it be us by choice than nobody by default."* The steelmanned version of what is lost lives in [04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md](../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md); a parent should have read it before this conversation.

## Part B — Raising it with friends, without sounding mad

### 6. Start from the shared complaint, and ask rather than pitch *(judgement)*

The evidence is unusually clear that *pitching* is the wrong format: in three field experiments, conversations that deployed arguments alone moved attitudes not at all, while the same conversations built around non-judgemental exchange of personal stories produced durable change [6]. A ~10-minute conversation done this way outperformed decades of ambient persuasion on a far more charged topic than tax [5]. Translated to the pub (judgement — the studies tested canvassing, not friendship):

- **Open from a complaint you already share**, not from the policy: house deposits, nepo babies, "our kids will be the first generation poorer than us," the colleague whose flat was bought by the Bank of Mum and Dad. These are [01-problem/](../01-problem/README.md) chapters wearing casual clothes.
- **Ask questions rather than assert** — the street-epistemology style: *"What do you think actually separates the kids who get ahead now — effort or parents?" … "How much of that would you say is earned?" … "If you could design it from scratch, would you let starting positions be inherited?"* Let the friend build the case; your policy arrives later as the answer to *their* diagnosis.
- **Trade stories, not statistics.** Your own expectant-heir story, or your reasons as a parent, does more work than any chart — consistent with both the narrative-exchange finding [6] and the story-first doctrine of [narratives.md](narratives.md).

### 7. The credential shield: the one-breath answer to "that's crazy"

When the "you've gone full communist" eyebrow arrives, the response is a pedigree, not a defence. One breath (judgement on phrasing; every item repo-verified):

> *"It's the oldest respectable idea in economics: Thomas Paine proposed exactly this — inheritance levy funding a universal grant — in 1797; John Stuart Mill said cap what any person may* receive*; Andrew Carnegie and Teddy Roosevelt made the capitalist case; Britain and America actually ran 77–80% top estate rates mid-century, during the best growth decades either ever had; Piketty, Atkinson and Zucman are the modern lineage; and the OECD itself endorsed lifetime-receipts taxation in 2021. I didn't invent it — I read up on it."*

Every clause has a file: [05-history/paine-1797.md](../05-history/paine-1797.md), [05-history/mill-1848.md](../05-history/mill-1848.md), [05-history/carnegie-1889.md](../05-history/carnegie-1889.md), [05-history/roosevelt-1906.md](../05-history/roosevelt-1906.md), [05-history/uk-estate-duty.md](../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md), [05-history/us-estate-tax.md](../05-history/us-estate-tax.md), [05-history/piketty.md](../05-history/piketty.md), [05-history/atkinson.md](../05-history/atkinson.md) (the Meade → Mirrlees → Atkinson → OECD chain [9]), [01-problem/wealth-concentration.md](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md) (Zucman).

### 9. Social-risk management *(judgement, except where cited)*

- **The "researching" stance beats the "converted" stance.** "I've been reading about this idea and I can't find the hole in it — find it for me" invites collaboration; "I believe X" invites classification. It is also simply true of this repo, whose case-against section exists for exactly this use ([04-case-against/](../04-case-against/README.md)).
- **Carry one fact, not twenty.** The polling evidence says a single incidence fact does extraordinary work: only 4.62% of UK deaths trigger any inheritance tax [2], and learning how few estates pay is the treatment that more than doubled support in Kuziemko et al. — the largest single-fact persuasion effect measured for any tax [1]. If a conversation delivers only that one number, it succeeded. (Caveat from the same literature: information effects decay over weeks; a conversation opens a door, a follow-up keeps it open [1] — [06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md) §5.)
- **Humour is the pressure valve.** Self-deprecation beats zeal: *"I know, I know — I've become the inheritance-tax guy at parties. Ask my kids, they're thrilled."* A person who can laugh at their position is heard as sane; a person who can't is heard as recruited.
- **Know when to drop it.** One unanswered question, one fact, one link — then change the subject *first*. Friendship is the channel; burning the channel to win one exchange is negative-value persuasion, and pushing past resistance is precisely the judgemental mode the field experiments found inert [6]. The idea's best ally is the second conversation, which only happens if the first one ended pleasantly.
- **Never perform the argument for an audience.** Persuasion at a dinner table with spectators triggers position-defence; the evidence-backed format is one-to-one and non-judgemental [5][6]. Raise it in the kitchen, not across the table.

### 10. What not to do

**Never lead with "100% inheritance tax."** This is the section's ordering doctrine ([README.md](README.md)) applied person-to-person, and it matters *more* in private, where there is no editor to cut to the benefit. The first numbers spoken are **£500,000 tax-free per child** and **£50,000 for every 25-year-old** — the offer ([root README](../README.md): "Lead with the universal inheritance. The 100% rate is how it is paid for"; [02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md)). The rate arrives later, as arithmetic: *"and it's funded by taxing only what anyone receives above the half-million."* Other landmines, all inherited from the mass-audience files and just as live at close range: don't say "death tax" back ([debate-toolkit.md](debate-toolkit.md) rule 6), don't attack parents-providing ([narratives.md](narratives.md) rule 3), don't use left-coded vocabulary on conservative friends ([audience-messaging.md](audience-messaging.md)), and don't overclaim a statistic you can't source — among friends, you *are* the citation.
