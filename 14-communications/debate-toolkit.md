# Debate Toolkit: The Live-Argument Field Manual

**Executive summary.** Live debate is won by structure, not spontaneity: a rehearsed 30-second answer, a 2-minute expansion, and a disciplined pivot for each of the ten standard attacks. This file is the spoken-word companion to [09-objections/](../09-objections/README.md), which holds the full written rebuttals, and to [inoculation.md](inoculation.md), which delivers the same content *before* the fight.

## Rules of engagement

1. **Concede the kernel of truth first.** Every strong attack contains one. "You're right that valuation is hard —" disarms; blanket denial reads as evasion and hands the attacker the reasonable-person position.
2. **Never defend "the state"; always defend "the kid with nothing."** The moment the argument becomes state-versus-family, it is lost. The argument is always kid-versus-dynasty. The state is merely the referee that moves £50k from the second to the first.
3. **Bring every abstract point back to the Two 25-Year-Olds** ([narratives.md](narratives.md)). Deadweight loss, Laffer curves, liquidity events — all of it resolves into: which of Amy and Ben does your position help?
4. **Refuse the "politics of envy" frame by out-celebrating them.** Agree, enthusiastically, that greatness should be celebrated — *living* greatness. Founders, builders, earners: celebrate them by name. What we decline to celebrate is inherited title. Never say the word "envy" ([naming-and-language.md](naming-and-language.md)).
5. **Endowment in the first sentence, every time.** If the clip is cut, the audience must have heard "£50k at 25" before anything else.
6. **Never repeat the attack's language.** Rebut the farm-auction *scenario*; never speak the phrase "death tax" (one sanctioned exception: the prebunking construction in [inoculation.md](inoculation.md)).

## The universal 30-second answer

> "Here's the whole policy: every 25-year-old in this country gets £50,000 — a deposit, a training fund, a real start. It's paid for by taxing only what any one person *receives* above half a million pounds in inherited windfalls across their whole life. Your house, your savings, your kids — untouched. What changes is that nobody gets to be an aristocrat, and everybody gets an inheritance."

## The universal 2-minute expansion

Add, in order: (1) the Two 25-Year-Olds story with names; (2) the threshold arithmetic — a £900k estate split two ways passes entirely tax-free ([02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)); (3) the carve-outs stated as clause one, not as afterthought ([02-proposal/carveouts.md](../02-proposal/carveouts.md)); (4) the lineage — Paine, Mill, Carnegie, Roosevelt ([05-history/](../05-history/README.md)) — "this isn't radical; forgetting it was is what's radical"; (5) close on the endowment again.

## The ten attacks: answer and pivot

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "It'll destroy family farms/businesses" | Liquidity at succession is a real design problem | Farms and trading businesses are protected by name, in statute, with rollover relief — clause one, published before anything else | "The question isn't the farm — it's whether the farmhand's daughter gets any start at all" | [09-objections/family.md](../09-objections/family.md), [04-case-against/family-business.md](../04-case-against/family-business.md) |
| 2 | "The rich will just leave" (capital flight) | Some mobile wealth will try | Land, housing, and UK businesses can't board a plane; exit charges and coordination close much of the rest — and we've mapped it | "Meanwhile Ben can't flee his rent. Whose mobility are we worried about?" | [09-objections/avoidance.md](../09-objections/avoidance.md), [08-capital-flight/evidence.md](../08-capital-flight/evidence.md) |
| 3 | "People will stop working/saving if they can't pass it on" | Bequest motives are real | You can pass on £500k per child untouched — more than 95% of parents ever will (to verify); above that, the evidence that estate taxes kill effort is thin | "Nobody builds their second company for the great-grandchildren; they build it because building is what they do" | [09-objections/economic.md](../09-objections/economic.md), [04-case-against/bequest-motive.md](../04-case-against/bequest-motive.md) |
| 4 | "It's double taxation" | The money was often taxed once as income | It's taxed once *per person* — the heir has paid nothing on it; we tax windfalls people receive, and this is the least-earned windfall there is | "Ben pays tax on every pound he earns. Why is the only untaxed income the kind you get for being born lucky?" | [09-objections/fairness.md](../09-objections/fairness.md) |
| 5 | "It's the politics of envy" | Aspiration deserves respect | We celebrate every builder and want more of them — that's the point: a market that rewards doing, not descent | "Envy is wanting what someone earned. This is about what nobody earned" | [09-objections/whataboutery.md](../09-objections/whataboutery.md), [03-case-for/ethical.md](../03-case-for/ethical.md) |
| 6 | "The state will waste it / can't be trusted" | State delivery failures are real | The endowment is the least-discretionary programme possible: one transfer, one age, no means test, no caseworker | "This isn't a programme; it's a birthright with a bank transfer" | [09-objections/practicality.md](../09-objections/practicality.md), [04-case-against/state-failure.md](../04-case-against/state-failure.md) |
| 7 | "25-year-olds will blow £50k" | Some will | Evidence from capital grants and lump-sum transfers says most don't (to verify); and design options exist — staged release, qualified uses | "Amy got £2m at 25 and nobody demanded she pass a maturity test" | [09-objections/spend-it-all.md](../09-objections/spend-it-all.md), [16-second-order/heirs-and-work.md](../16-second-order/heirs-and-work.md) |
| 8 | "They'll avoid it with trusts and gifts" | Avoidance is an arms race | Lifetime-cumulative, recipient-based design closes the gift loophole by construction — every receipt counts, whenever it arrives | "Avoidance is an argument for better plumbing, not for keeping aristocracy" | [09-objections/avoidance.md](../09-objections/avoidance.md), [07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md](../07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md) |
| 9 | "It's a slippery slope to taxing everything" | Thresholds have moved before | The threshold is indexed and the endowment creates a mass constituency *against* stealth expansion — 100% of voters get the benefit; the ratchet points the other way | "The slope runs downhill toward the voters, not away from them" | [09-objections/slippery-slope.md](../09-objections/slippery-slope.md) |
| 10 | "Parents have a right to provide for their children" | They do — it's the deepest instinct in the debate | Half a million pounds per child, tax-free, protects every normal act of provision; what ends is provision so large it becomes the child's permanent rank | "Every parent in this country *does* provide under this policy — including, for the first time, Ben's" | [09-objections/family.md](../09-objections/family.md), [04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md](../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md) |

## Handling formats

- **Hostile broadcast interview:** answer the question asked in one sentence, then deliver the 30-second answer. Do not chase the interviewer's hypotheticals past one round; return to Amy and Ben.
- **Panel debate:** claim the concrete early — be the first to use a named person and a number; opponents will then sound abstract by contrast.
- **Comment-section / social:** answer the top objection once, well, with a link into [09-objections/](../09-objections/README.md); never thread-fight. The website is the long answer ([channels.md](channels.md)).

## Training

Spokespeople rehearse the ten pivots to fluency before first exposure; the attack list mirrors [10-opposition/attack-playbook.md](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md) and should be updated in lockstep with it. New attacks graduate into this table only after a written rebuttal exists in [09-objections/](../09-objections/README.md).
