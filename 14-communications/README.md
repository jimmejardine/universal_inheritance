# 14 — Communications: How the Idea Is Named, Told, and Spread

**Executive summary.** [06-palatability/](../06-palatability/README.md) establishes *why* the idea can be made popular — the polling psychology, the framing levers, the coalition map. This section is the operational *how*: the words, stories, channels, and combat drills that carry the idea from a research repository into mainstream discourse.

## The doctrine in one line

> **Lead with the endowment — "every 25-year-old gets £50,000" — never with the tax. One concrete number, everywhere, forever.**

Every file in this section is an application of that line. The tax is the funding mechanism; the endowment is the product. Campaigns that lead with the mechanism lose ([case-studies](../06-palatability/case-studies.md)); campaigns that lead with the benefit can win. The number must be singular and stable: £50k in the UK, $60k in the US, at age 25 (see [02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md)). Message discipline around one number is worth more than a dozen clever arguments.

## Three operating principles

1. **Benefit before mechanism.** The endowment is concrete, individual, and visualisable; the tax is abstract and loss-framed. Order of presentation is not cosmetic — it determines which mental frame the audience loads first (see [naming-and-language.md](naming-and-language.md) on Lakoff).
2. **One number, repeated until embarrassing.** "£50k at 25" must become the idea's handle the way "$15 minimum wage" and "1%" became handles. Internal debate about the exact figure belongs in [02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md), never in public copy.
3. **Inoculate before you scale.** The opposition's attack lines are known and finite ([10-opposition/attack-playbook.md](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)). Audiences reached *before* those attacks, with weakened versions and rebuttals attached, are measurably more resistant ([inoculation.md](inoculation.md)). Reach without inoculation is reach handed to the opposition.
