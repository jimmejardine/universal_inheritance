# Outreach: The Named Creator and Journalist Map

**Executive summary.** [channels.md](channels.md) says *which kinds* of channel do which job; this file names the actual UK and US creators, podcasts, and journalists whose existing audiences and published record make them the highest-value first briefings — with the specific pitch, the repo file that is "their story," and the risk notes for each. Every characterisation below is drawn from the public record (verified July 2026, audience figures approximate and dated); **listing implies no endorsement by, contact with, or agreement from any person named** — this is a map of publicly stated positions and audiences, nothing more.

## Findings

- The UK's largest economics-creator audience — Gary Stevenson's 1.6m-subscriber Gary's Economics [1][2] — is already assembled around wealth inequality, but around a *different remedy* (an annual wealth tax); the engagement has to be built honestly on that difference, not blurred over it.
- The under-35 news audience is reachable through a small number of named outlets: TLDR News (~3.7m subscribers across six channels [3][4]), PoliticsJOE (~700k, parent JOE targets 18–35s [5]), Novara Media (~1.1m [6]), and podcast brands with mass 25–45 reach (The News Agents: 100m+ downloads, ~5m TikTok views/month [8]).
- Favourable-to-neutral broadsheet coverage of inheritance-tax reform already exists under respected bylines — Paul Johnson's Times columns (via IFS) [10][11] are the strongest verified examples — so the "licensing" function in [channels.md](channels.md) has named, warm entry points rather than cold ones.
- Two nominated entries could not be fully verified and are flagged rather than asserted: Martin Sandbu's specific FT column on "an inheritance for all" (FT is not crawlable; only his book's adjacent positions are confirmed [12][13]), and any Aaron Bastani content specifically on inheritance tax (his wealth-tax content is confirmed [7]; inheritance-specific content was not found).
- The correct sequencing is unchanged from [channels.md](channels.md): brief journalists and podcasts (respectability layer) in parallel with, not after, creator outreach — creators amplify what the reference layer makes citable.

## Ground rules for this file

1. **Public record only.** Every claim about a named person is sourced to published material; audience sizes are approximate and dated to mid-2026 unless stated.
2. **Judgement is labelled.** Where receptivity is inferred beyond the record, the inference is marked "(judgement)."
3. **No endorsement implied.** Nobody listed has endorsed, been approached about, or is affiliated with this project. Several would likely oppose parts of it; that is noted where the record shows it.
4. **The pitch is always a file.** Per [audience-messaging.md](audience-messaging.md), the journalist segment's asset is depth on demand — every outreach ends with a stable URL into this repo.

## UK — economics and politics video/social

### Gary Stevenson / Gary's Economics — the priority conversation

**Verified record.** Former Citibank trader (joined 2008 via LSE), now the UK's most-followed economics YouTuber: Gary's Economics, launched 2020, ~1.6m subscribers (mid-2026) [1][2]. Author of the bestselling memoir *The Trading Game* (Penguin, March 2024) [2]. Presented the Channel 4 documentary *How to Get Filthy Rich* (July 2026), arguing for a wealth tax on the super-rich [2]. His signature policy is an **annual wealth tax — publicly stated as ~2% on assets above £10m** [1]. His March 2025 Diary of a CEO debate with Daniel Priestley on wealth inequality went viral and spawned an ecosystem of response content [9].

**Where his position differs from this project — and how to engage it honestly.** Stevenson advocates a recurring tax on *stocks of wealth held by the living*; this project taxes *transfers at death* above £500k per recipient and recycles the proceeds into a universal endowment ([02-proposal/mechanism.md](../02-proposal/mechanism.md); the repo's explicit position that this is not a wealth tax is part of its identity). The honest engagement: do not pitch Universal Inheritance as "his idea, refined." Pitch it as the *complementary* instrument — his diagnosis (wealth concentration compounding while wages stagnate) is substantially this repo's [01-problem/](../01-problem/README.md) section; the difference is remedy design, and the comparison (annual valuation of the living vs. once-per-lifetime settlement at transfer; avoidance surface; exit incentives — [08-capital-flight/](../08-capital-flight/README.md)) is exactly the three-hour-format debate his channel runs well. A respectful on-record disagreement with the biggest audience in the space is worth more than a blurred endorsement (judgement).

**Format fit:** long-form direct-to-camera and multi-hour debate. **The pitch:** "You've built the case that wealth inequality is the story; here's the transfer-tax version of the fix, fully costed and objection-mapped — debate it": [02-proposal/revenue-model.md](../02-proposal/revenue-model.md) and [00-capitalism/markets-vs-dynasties.md](../00-capitalism/markets-vs-dynasties.md). **Risk notes:** his audience skews anti-establishment; an approach that reads as institutional co-option will be read out loud, on camera. Expect and welcome public pushback on the "why not also tax living wealth?" line — the answer is in [02-proposal/tax-interactions.md](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md).

### TLDR News

**Verified record.** UK-founded explainer network, six channels (UK/EU/Global/Daily/Podcasts/Business), ~3.7m combined subscribers; main UK channel ~918k; ~157m views in 2025–26 [3][4]. Nonpartisan-explainer house style; revenue majority from YouTube advertising, diversifying into a print magazine [3].

**Format fit:** 10–15-minute neutral explainers — the natural home of "what is this policy and would it work?" **The pitch:** the explainer brief writes itself from [00-executive-summary.md](../00-executive-summary.md) plus the incidence table in [02-proposal/revenue-model.md](../02-proposal/revenue-model.md): "only 4.62% of UK deaths pay any inheritance tax — this proposal would cut it for most of them and give every 25-year-old £50k." **Risk notes:** editorial neutrality means the video will platform the strongest objections; supply [09-objections/](../09-objections/README.md) so the steelman is answered in the same video. Receptivity: format-driven rather than ideological — high likelihood of *coverage*, zero likelihood of *advocacy* (judgement).

### PoliticsJOE

**Verified record.** Political arm of JOE Media, ~698k YouTube subscribers; podcast episodes typically 20–70k views; parent brand explicitly targets 18–35-year-olds [5]. Format mix: vox pops, interviews, satirical shorts, podcast.

**Format fit:** street interviews and shareable clips. **The pitch:** the vox-pop format is the Kuziemko experiment run as content — ask people what share of deaths pay inheritance tax, then show them the 4.62% figure on camera ([06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)); their reaction *is* the video. **Risk notes:** irreverent house tone can flatten nuance; keep the ask to the one number and the offer frame ([naming-and-language.md](naming-and-language.md)). Receptivity: strong fit with their generational-unfairness output (judgement).

### Novara Media / Aaron Bastani

**Verified record.** Left media organisation, ~1.1m YouTube subscribers (from 800k in July 2024) [6]. Co-founder Aaron Bastani's long-form interview output includes wealth-taxation economics — e.g. "We Need a Wealth Tax on Billionaires" with Gabriel Zucman [7]. **Not verified:** any Bastani or Novara content specifically advocating inheritance-tax reform or abolition; treat his inheritance-specific position as unknown.

**Format fit:** long-form interview (Downstream-style) and news commentary. **The pitch:** the interview subject is the repo's most left-legible file pair — [01-problem/wealth-concentration.md](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md) and [03-case-for/piketty-r-vs-g.md](../03-case-for/piketty-r-vs-g.md) — with the framing warning from [audience-messaging.md](audience-messaging.md): the progressive-base landmine is letting "abolish inheritance" maximalism become the public face. **Risk notes:** association codes the policy left in swing audiences; per [channels.md](channels.md) sequencing, centre-right validators should be on record *first*. Receptivity: high for the diagnosis; the pro-capitalist framing of [00-capitalism/](../00-capitalism/README.md) may attract friendly fire (judgement).

## UK — podcasts with 25–45 reach

### The Rest Is Money (Robert Peston & Steph McGovern)

**Verified record.** Goalhanger-network business/economics podcast hosted by ITV political editor Robert Peston and broadcaster Steph McGovern; weekly news-explainer format [14]. **Not verified:** a specific inheritance-tax episode (episode archives were not searchable to that depth); the show's tax-policy coverage generally is confirmed by its published feed [14].

**Format fit:** accessible two-hander explainer; hosts push back in real time. **The pitch:** "the most interesting live idea in tax policy" framing from the journalist row of [audience-messaging.md](audience-messaging.md), anchored on the revenue arithmetic ([02-proposal/revenue-model.md](../02-proposal/revenue-model.md)). **Risk notes:** broadcast-affiliated hosts will require balance; send the farm carve-out ([02-proposal/carveouts.md](../02-proposal/carveouts.md)) before they ask.

### The News Agents (Emily Maitlis, Jon Sopel, Lewis Goodall)

**Verified record.** Global's daily news podcast; 100m+ downloads, 24m monthly digital audience, ~5m TikTok views/month [8]. Hosts are ex-BBC (Newsnight; North America editor).

**Format fit:** daily news analysis plus clipped short-form — one strong interview yields a month of TikTok cuts. **The pitch:** newsjack a live IHT news moment (Budget speculation, farm-relief rows) with the counterintuitive offer-first angle: "the radical version is a tax cut for 90%+ of heirs" ([00-executive-summary.md](../00-executive-summary.md)). **Risk notes:** daily format means one pass, no depth — the spokesperson must land the two numbers (£500k tax-free, £50k at 25) in the first answer ([naming-and-language.md](naming-and-language.md) discipline rules).

### Pod Save the UK (Nish Kumar & Coco Khan)

**Verified record.** Crooked Media-format UK politics podcast; has covered exactly this terrain — an episode with then-IFS director Paul Johnson on how the rich avoid inheritance tax, clipped to TikTok [15].

**Format fit:** progressive comedy-politics; younger-skewing. **The pitch:** the avoidance story — "the current tax catches the mass affluent and misses the truly dynastic; here's the design that closes it" ([02-proposal/avoidance.md](../02-proposal/avoidance.md), [09-objections/avoidance.md](../09-objections/avoidance.md)). **Risk notes:** as with Novara — base-energising, not swing-persuading; schedule accordingly (judgement).

## US — economics video

### More Perfect Union

**Verified record.** Progressive non-profit video outlet founded 2021 by Faiz Shakir; 1.3m+ YouTube subscribers as of January 2025, with 2026 trackers showing ~2.6–3.1m; described by the Washington Post (February 2026) as one of the fastest-growing left-wing pages on YouTube, notable for avoiding personality-driven outrage formats [16].

**Format fit:** worker-centred mini-documentaries. **The pitch:** the US great-wealth-transfer story — $124tn transferring through 2048, $105tn of it to heirs (Cerulli) [17] ([01-problem/great-wealth-transfer.md](../01-problem/great-wealth-transfer.md)) — told through one heir and one worker, i.e. the Two 25-Year-Olds narrative ([narratives.md](narratives.md)) in their documentary grammar. **Risk notes:** US retail number is $60k/$750k per [README.md](README.md); do not export the £ figures. Their labour-first frame may foreground the tax over the endowment — brief the doctrine explicitly.

### Economics Explained

**Verified record.** Australian economics channel, ~2.8–2.9m subscribers (2025–26) [18]; documentary-style explainers on national economies and policy ideas; broadly market-friendly, centrist register.

**Format fit / judgement on fit:** the channel's "here's a policy idea — would it actually work?" format suits a neutral treatment, and its centrist, pro-market audience is precisely the demographic the [00-capitalism/](../00-capitalism/README.md) framing was written for; but the same audience will punish anything smelling of confiscation, so the brief must lead with the markets-vs-dynasties file, not the rate (judgement). **The pitch:** [00-capitalism/markets-vs-dynasties.md](../00-capitalism/markets-vs-dynasties.md) plus the UBS heirs-vs-founders crossover ($150.8bn inherited vs $140.7bn created by new self-made billionaires, 2023) [19]. **Risk notes:** an unconvinced verdict from a neutral explainer is a permanent citation for the opposition; only brief when the objection tree ([09-objections/](../09-objections/README.md)) is complete on economics.
