# Campaign Briefs: Nine Concrete Social-Media Concepts

**Executive summary.** [channels.md](channels.md) sets the channel strategy and [narratives.md](narratives.md) the story rules; this file turns them into nine executable campaign briefs, each built on one verified fact from this repository and one audience segment from [audience-messaging.md](audience-messaging.md). Every campaign concept below is creative judgement — untested until message-tested per [06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md) — but every fact each concept carries is verified and sourced.

## Findings

- The single most campaign-ready fact in the repo is the incidence-information effect: telling people how few estates pay the tax roughly tripled support (17%→~53%) in the landmark Kuziemko et al. experiment, with a standalone estate-tax information slide alone more than doubling it [2] — meaning the *content itself* (revealing "only 4.62% of deaths pay" [1]) is the persuasion mechanism, not just its packaging.
- Every brief obeys the doctrine ([README.md](README.md)): offer first — £500k tax-free per recipient, £50k for everyone at 25 — mechanism second; and the [narratives.md](narratives.md) rules: named people, system-villain, concede reality.
- The platform evidence is already in the repo and supports a video-first, creator-led plan: 37% of UK 16–24s use YouTube for news (Ofcom 2025) [11], and across 48 markets 44% of 18–24s and 38% of 25–34s name social/video networks as their main news source (Reuters Institute DNR 2025) [12] ([channels.md](channels.md), [10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md](../10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md)).
- Two briefs carry elevated legal/ethical risk and are gated accordingly: Dynasty Watch (public figures and public records only, pre-publication legal review) and heir-defector testimony (consent and verification doctrine from [13-movement/recruitment.md](../13-movement/recruitment.md)).
- Distribution is creator-led, not owned-channel-led ([outreach-creators.md](outreach-creators.md)); the creator kit specified at the end of this file is the standard hand-off artefact for every brief.

## Brief format

Each brief: **Concept · Platform · Format · Hook · Audience** (segment per [audience-messaging.md](audience-messaging.md)) · **The verified fact it carries** · **Success metric** · **Risk / inoculation note** ([inoculation.md](inoculation.md)).

## 1. "Your £50k" — the endowment calculator (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** An interactive calculator/simulator on the manifesto site, fed by short-form video: enter your age (or your kids'/grandkids' ages) and see when the £50k arrives and what it does — deposit, training, business stake — against real first-time-buyer maths. Creators film themselves and guests running it.
- **Platform:** Web tool + TikTok/Reels/Shorts clips; YouTube integrations.
- **Format:** 15–45s screen-capture reactions; embeddable widget.
- **Hook:** "Type in your age. That's the date you'd get £50,000."
- **Audience:** Young renters (primary); older homeowners via the grandchild variant ("enter four grandkids — that's £200k into your family").
- **Verified fact:** The £50k-at-25 design ([02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md)) against the average UK first-time-buyer deposit of £61,090 in 2024 (Halifax) [3] ([01-problem/housing.md](../01-problem/housing.md)).
- **Success metric:** Calculator completions and share-rate per completion; % of users reaching the "how it's paid for" page (voluntary depth is the consideration-stage signal per [channels.md](channels.md)).
- **Risk / inoculation:** The comments will say "inflationary" and "they'll blow it" — pre-load the follow-up clips from [09-objections/spend-it-all.md](../09-objections/spend-it-all.md) and [16-second-order/asset-prices.md](../16-second-order/asset-prices.md) before launch, per [inoculation.md](inoculation.md).

## 2. "Who Actually Pays?" — the myth-buster series (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** A guess-then-reveal series: street interviews and creator pieces asking "what percentage of deaths do you think pay inheritance tax?" — then the reveal: **4.62%** (2022–23). The Kuziemko result says the reveal itself moves opinion; the format industrialises the experiment.
- **Platform:** TikTok + PoliticsJOE-style vox pops ([outreach-creators.md](outreach-creators.md)); YouTube for the long version.
- **Format:** 30–60s guess/reveal; episodic ("myth #1: everyone pays…").
- **Hook:** "Everyone thinks they'll pay inheritance tax. Guess what percentage of deaths actually do."
- **Audience:** All segments — this is the universal misperception; older homeowners are the highest-value converts ([06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)).
- **Verified fact:** 4.62% of UK deaths in 2022–23 resulted in any IHT charge (HMRC) [1] ([02-proposal/revenue-model.md](../02-proposal/revenue-model.md)); information about how few estates pay roughly tripled support for the tax, 17%→~53%, in Kuziemko, Norton, Saez & Stantcheva (2015, AER) [2] ([06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)).
- **Success metric:** Pre/post polling lift in a matched panel (a UK replication of the Kuziemko treatment is already an open item in [06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)); comment-section guess distribution as free misperception data.
- **Risk / inoculation:** Durability — information effects decay ([06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md) finds 58% of the effect surviving one month); the series must be evergreen and repeated, not a one-off stunt.

## 3. "£500k Tax-Free — Did You Know?" — the offer-first frame (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** The core doctrine as a campaign: most people have never heard the actual offer. Deadpan format — "Under the most radical inheritance proposal in Britain, here's what happens to your family: [walks through a normal family: nothing, nothing, nothing, +£50k each]."
- **Platform:** Short-form everywhere; paid social to older demographics on Facebook.
- **Format:** 30s explainer; carousel for static feeds ("Your house → passes free. Your savings → pass free. Each child → £500k allowance. Each grandchild → +£50k").
- **Hook:** "The 'confiscatory' inheritance policy, explained with your actual family."
- **Audience:** Older homeowners (decisive swing segment); the Family Security narrative ([narratives.md](narratives.md)) is the script.
- **Verified fact:** Over 90% of heirs would pay nothing — IFS projections put even the top tenth of inheritors born in the 1980s at just under £500k received [4] ([00-executive-summary.md](../00-executive-summary.md), [02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)).
- **Success metric:** Aided recall of "£500k tax-free" in tracking polls; shift on the "would this policy affect your family?" misperception question.
- **Risk / inoculation:** "That's how it starts — thresholds always fall" is the strongest comeback; the rebuttal lives in [09-objections/slippery-slope.md](../09-objections/slippery-slope.md) and must ship in the same content cycle.

## 4. "The Nepo Economy" — piggybacking nepo-baby discourse (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** Extend the culturally saturated "nepo baby" frame from film casting to the whole economy: episodes on inherited billboard-chart wealth — the year heirs out-earned founders. The existing discourse does the framing work; the campaign supplies the data layer.
- **Platform:** TikTok/Reels native; YouTube essay for depth (an [outreach-creators.md](outreach-creators.md) brief for Gary's Economics-adjacent and More Perfect Union formats).
- **Format:** 60s "nepo economy" episodes; recurring scoreboard graphic.
- **Hook:** "2023 was the first year new billionaires inherited more money than they built."
- **Audience:** Young renters; progressives (base); the meritocracy wound ([01-problem/meritocracy-legitimacy.md](../01-problem/meritocracy-legitimacy.md)).
- **Verified fact:** UBS *Billionaire Ambitions Report 2023*: for the first time in the report's tracking, new billionaires acquired more wealth by inheritance ($150.8bn) than the year's new self-made billionaires created ($140.7bn) [5] ([01-problem/wealth-concentration.md](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md), [00-capitalism/markets-vs-dynasties.md](../00-capitalism/markets-vs-dynasties.md)).
- **Success metric:** Organic adoption of "nepo economy" as a phrase by non-campaign accounts (the ownability test from [naming-and-language.md](naming-and-language.md)).
- **Risk / inoculation:** Envy-coding — the [narratives.md](narratives.md) rule (villain is the system, never the person) is load-bearing here; celebrate every founder named, target only the conversion of achievement into dynasty. No mockery of named heirs' persons, ever.

## 5. "The Waiting Room" — expectant-heir stories (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** The counterintuitive flank: inheritance harms *the people waiting for it*. Anonymised, dramatised first-person stories — careers not chosen, lives on the parental leash, sibling caveat wars — from the documented record in [03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md](../03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md). Ends on the liberation frame: "your start will be your own, and so will your life."
- **Platform:** YouTube (5–10 min story format); podcast segments; Reddit-native text versions (r/inheritance-adjacent communities are full of primary material — sourcing only from consenting/public accounts).
- **Format:** Narrated case stories with actor voiceover; disclaimered as composite where composite.
- **Hook:** "The fortune arrives at 60. The damage starts at 25."
- **Audience:** Journalists/commentariat (novelty value) and young renters (the "even winners lose" reframe); conservatives via the family-firm succession angle ([04-case-against/family-business.md](../04-case-against/family-business.md)).
- **Verified fact:** Probate caveats in England & Wales exceeded 11,300 in both 2024 and 2025 — up 79% in fifteen years — and contested High Court probate claims hit a record 1,217 in 2025 (law-firm-compiled MoJ data) [6] ([03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md](../03-case-for/expectant-heirs.md)).
- **Success metric:** Long-form completion rate; pickup as a feature angle by print journalists (this is a story-desk pitch as much as a social format).
- **Risk / inoculation:** Sneering at heirs violates the story rules; the tone is tragic, not gleeful. Composite stories must be labelled — one fabricated-testimony accusation poisons the format ([13-movement/recruitment.md](../13-movement/recruitment.md) verification doctrine).

## 6. "Dynasty Watch" — public-record dynastic-wealth tracking (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** A recurring, scrupulously sourced series tracking dynastic succession among public figures from public records: the Murdoch trust litigation and settlement, the Redstone decade, media and corporate successions — narrated as "the case for capping inheritance, serialised by the inheritors" ([10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md](../10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md)).
- **Platform:** YouTube + newsletter; clipped highlights to short-form.
- **Format:** 8–12 min episodes pegged to news events (court filings, settlements, rich-list publications).
- **Hook:** "Control of what you watch, read and rent was just decided by a probate court in Nevada."
- **Audience:** Journalists/commentariat; conservatives (unaccountable unearned power is a right-legible wrong — [16-second-order/dynasties-and-power.md](../16-second-order/dynasties-and-power.md)).
- **Verified fact:** A Nevada probate commissioner ruled Rupert and Lachlan Murdoch acted in "bad faith" in the trust-amendment attempt (December 2024) [7]; the September 2025 settlement dissolved the trust, entrenched Lachlan's control to 2050, and bought out three siblings for roughly $1.1bn each [8] ([10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md](../10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md)).
- **Success metric:** Citation of episodes by mainstream coverage; subscriber retention across news pegs.
- **Risk / inoculation:** **Highest legal risk in this file.** Public figures and public records only; every claim court-filed or on-the-record; pre-publication legal review of every episode; no net-worth speculation beyond published estimates; no coverage of private individuals or minors. The line between accountability journalism and harassment must be policed by counsel, not by editorial enthusiasm.

## 7. Heir-Defector Testimony — "I'm the heir, tax me" (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** First-person testimony from people set to inherit (or who have inherited) large sums and publicly support the policy — the single most attack-resistant messenger type in the playbook ([13-movement/heir-defectors.md](../13-movement/heir-defectors.md), [13-movement/messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md); the Gates Sr./Responsible Wealth precedent in [10-opposition/attack-playbook.md](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)). Format: direct-to-camera, full disclosure of their stake first.
- **Platform:** YouTube premiere + podcast circuit + broadcast offers (the Ofcom due-impartiality balance slot — [10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md](../10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md)).
- **Format:** 3–5 min testimonies; longer podcast conversations; a compilation film.
- **Hook:** "I stand to inherit millions. Here's why I want to be taxed."
- **Audience:** Conservatives and business owners ("this is not envy — the winners want it too"); journalists (irresistible profile subject).
- **Verified fact:** The organised precedent exists and is live: Patriotic Millionaires — wealthy members publicly campaigning for higher taxes on themselves [9] ([13-movement/funding.md](../13-movement/funding.md)); Gary Stevenson, the UK's largest economics creator, is a listed member of the adjacent Millionaires for Humanity network [10].
- **Success metric:** Number of recruited, verified, consenting testifiers (the binding constraint); earned-media pickup per testimony.
- **Risk / inoculation:** Hypocrisy-hunting is guaranteed ("give it away now, then") — the response is scripted in [10-opposition/personal-attacks.md](../10-opposition/personal-attacks.md) (disclosure-first, "one family opting out changes nothing; a rule changes everything"). Recruitment, consent, and verification doctrine per [13-movement/recruitment.md](../13-movement/recruitment.md): never burn a source, verify every biography before broadcast.

## 8. "The £100 Trillion Countdown" — the great-wealth-transfer clock (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** A live counter — the largest wealth transfer in history is happening now — as recurring content: what $124tn means, who gets it, and the two futures (Schumpeterian churn vs inherited stagnation, [09-objections/self-correcting.md](../09-objections/self-correcting.md)). The visual is a ticking counter; the argument is the salience window in [11-geographies/crisis-windows.md](../11-geographies/crisis-windows.md).
- **Platform:** A counter embed on the manifesto site; short-form explainers; a More Perfect Union-format US documentary pitch ([outreach-creators.md](outreach-creators.md)).
- **Format:** Data-visual shorts; annual "state of the transfer" flagship video.
- **Hook:** "While you watched this video, another £X million was inherited."
- **Audience:** Broadcast/general public (National Renewal narrative); US audiences with the $-denominated version.
- **Verified fact:** Cerulli projects $124tn transferring in the US through 2048 — $105tn of it to heirs [13] ([01-problem/great-wealth-transfer.md](../01-problem/great-wealth-transfer.md)).
- **Success metric:** Embed adoption by third-party sites and creators; recall of "the great wealth transfer" linked to the policy in tracking polls.
- **Risk / inoculation:** Number discipline — UK and US figures must never be mixed in one asset ([README.md](README.md): one number per market); the counter's methodology page must be linked from every asset or the figure becomes attackable.

## 9. "Trained On Us, Inherited By Them" — the AI-dividend campaign (creative judgement)

- **Concept:** The Common Inheritance narrative ([narratives.md](narratives.md) §5 — Paine's ground-rent updated for AI) as executable video: models trained on humanity's publicly curated commons are concentrating returns to capital and minting the next dynasty class; the £50k endowment is the public's dividend on its own data, and the inheritance cap stops the first AI trillionaires becoming a permanent aristocracy. Episodes peg to AI-wealth news moments (funding rounds, rich-list updates) and land the Paine update in one line.
- **Platform:** TikTok/Reels/Shorts organic + paid social ([13-movement/paid-acquisition.md](../13-movement/paid-acquisition.md)); YouTube pre-roll against AI-topic adjacency; podcast reads.
- **Format:** 30–60s explainer over training-data visuals ("everything you ever posted openly"); a 4-minute YouTube version that carries the honest-limits caveat in full.
- **Hook (judgement):** "The robots learned from all of us. The money went to a few of them. The inheritance goes to their kids — unless."
- **Audience:** Tech and AI-exposed workers (the new segment in [narratives.md](narratives.md)'s selection table); young renters; broadcast/general public via the National Renewal register.
- **Verified facts:** IMF SDN/2024/001 — capital-income and wealth inequality increase in *all* AI adoption scenarios [14] ([13-movement/closing-window.md](../13-movement/closing-window.md)); the 2023 crossover — new billionaires inherited more ($150.8bn) than the year's new self-made billionaires created ($140.7bn) (UBS) [5]; top-0.0001% fortunes compounding at 7.5%/yr since 1987 (Zucman) [15] ([01-problem/wealth-concentration.md](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md)).
- **Success metric:** Cost per completed view and donation conversion on the flywheel CTA against [13-movement/paid-acquisition.md](../13-movement/paid-acquisition.md) benchmarks; unaided association of "AI wealth" with the endowment in tracking polls.
- **Risk / inoculation:** Two failure modes. (a) Anti-tech or anti-founder coding — the campaign is neither: today's AI wealth is founder wealth and the policy never taxes the founder, only the windfall's transfer to heirs; the villain-is-the-system rule ([narratives.md](narratives.md)) is load-bearing, no named founder is ever the villain, and the honest-limits caveat ships inside every long-form asset per [inoculation.md](inoculation.md). (b) "Training data isn't land" rebuttals — pre-empt in the pinned fact sheet with the Paine primary source ([05-history/paine-1797.md](../05-history/paine-1797.md)) and Acemoglu's capital-vs-labour-income finding [16], and keep the claim modest: a dividend on the commons, not a property claim on model weights.
- **Flywheel CTA (standard end-card):** "£2–£200 keeps this ad running — it was paid for by people who saw one" ([13-movement/paid-acquisition.md](../13-movement/paid-acquisition.md)).

## Platform-evidence note

The video-first, creator-led weighting of these briefs rests on the repo's verified consumption data, reused from [channels.md](channels.md) and [10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md](../10-opposition/elite-media-capture.md): 37% of UK 16–24s use YouTube for news (Ofcom, News Consumption in the UK 2025) [11]; across 48 markets, 44% of 18–24s and 38% of 25–34s name social media and video networks as their main news source (Reuters Institute Digital News Report 2025) [12]. The corollary from [inoculation.md](inoculation.md) stands: none of these campaigns should scale before its objection content is live.
