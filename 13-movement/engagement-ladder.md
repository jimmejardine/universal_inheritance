# The Engagement Ladder: What a Sympathiser Can Do Today

**Executive summary.** This file is the individual supporter's action menu — twelve rungs from zero-cost (read, share) to full commitment (found a chapter), each with a concrete first step, a realistic time price, and the next ask built in. It is the supporter-side mirror of [recruitment.md](recruitment.md): that file tells the movement how to move people inward; this one tells a sympathetic person, today, with whatever hour or skill or platform they have, exactly where to stand on the ladder and how to climb one rung.

## Key points

- **Every rung is real work.** The lowest rungs are not consolation prizes: at the current stage of [theory-of-change.md](theory-of-change.md) (stage 1–2, corpus and credibility), a well-placed share or one persuaded dinner guest is genuinely among the highest-value acts available to anyone.
- **The ladder is climbed one rung at a time.** Mobilisation research distinguishes *mobilising* (many people, small transactional asks) from *organising* (fewer people, transformative asks that build capacity); the strongest civic associations blend both, and people accept escalating asks, not leaps [1]. Ask a reader to found a chapter and you lose the reader; ask them to host a dinner and you may gain a founder.
- **Match the rung to the person.** The supporter segments profiled in [../06-palatability/coalition.md](../06-palatability/coalition.md) enter at different rungs and cap out at different rungs (§3). A retired solicitor's best rung is not a student's.
- **Every action ends with a next ask** — the discipline from [recruitment.md](recruitment.md): attendance that is terminal converts nobody.
- Except where cited, this file is organising doctrine — labelled judgement, consistent with the movement precedents in [precedents.md](precedents.md), not established fact.

## 1. The ladder

Time prices are honest estimates (judgement). Rungs 0–4 are *mobilising* asks — private, low-cost, no exposure. Rungs 5–11 are *organising* asks — they build capacity in the supporter and structure around them [1].

| # | Rung | Cost | First step |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Read the manifesto; share one file | 1–3 hours, once | Read [../00-executive-summary.md](../00-executive-summary.md); send the one file that answers the objection your most sceptical friend actually holds |
| 1 | Have one good conversation | 1 hour | Use the scripts in [../14-communications/personal-conversations.md](../14-communications/personal-conversations.md) — one honest conversation with someone who trusts you outperforms broadcast |
| 2 | Join the mailing list | 2 minutes | The email address is the movement's only durable connection to you; everything else in this table is unreachable without it ([recruitment.md](recruitment.md) ring 4) |
| 3 | Small recurring donation | £3–10/month | Recurring beats one-off at any amount: it is the independence budget of [funding.md](funding.md), and the count of donors matters more than the sum. A one-off £2–£200 flywheel gift ([paid-acquisition.md](paid-acquisition.md)) is an honourable entry variant of this rung — expect the recurring upsell within days |
| 4 | Write to your MP | 30 minutes | Template ask in §2 — keyed to the stepping-stones, never to the flagship |
| 5 | Host a dinner or reading group | One evening, repeatable | Six guests, one chapter of the corpus, one honest argument; the movement's conversion unit is the table, not the timeline (judgement) |
| 6 | Start or join a university / workplace society | 2–4 hours/month | Student politics and workplace networks are where [recruitment.md](recruitment.md) sources ring-3 advocates; a society constitution and one termly speaker is a functioning cell |
| 7 | Volunteer a skill | 2–10 hours/month | Skills menu in §4 — the corpus needs economists, lawyers, designers, and translators more than it needs marchers at this stage |
| 8 | Organise locally | 5–15 hours/month | Convert rungs 5–6 into a standing local group: regular venue, named convener, event calendar, and the snowflake discipline of developing your own successors ([recruitment.md](recruitment.md)) |
| 9 | Go public as a messenger | Reputation cost | Op-eds, podcasts, testimony under your own name — coordinated with [messengers.md](messengers.md) so the right biography carries the right message. Heirs and the wealthy are the highest-value public voices of all: see [heir-defectors.md](heir-defectors.md) |
| 10 | Found a chapter | 10+ hours/month, years | A chapter is a local group with governance, funding hygiene, and succession — the forms and guardrails are in [organisational-forms.md](organisational-forms.md) and [funding.md](funding.md) |
| 11 | Join the core | Career-scale | Own a section of this corpus as its named maintainer — by invitation, on the strength of work already visible ([recruitment.md](recruitment.md) ring 1) |

Two rules of the ladder (judgement):

1. **Never skip more than one rung in an ask.** The research finding behind this is Han's: high-engagement associations succeed by *transforming* members through graduated work, not by harvesting one-off transactions [1].
2. **Descending is fine.** A founder who burns out to rung 2 is retained; a founder who is guilted into staying quits the movement entirely. Design every rung to be honourable to stand on indefinitely.

## 2. Rung 4 in full: the letter to your MP

The single most common supporter question is "what do I actually say?" The answer is disciplined by [../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md): **never ask an MP to endorse the flagship** (100% above £500k). Ministers carry steps, not maps. The template ask is the stepping-stone that fits the MP's party:

> I'm a constituent. I'm writing about inheritance taxation — not to ask you to support any radical scheme, but to ask you to support **[the step]**, because **[the true cover story]**.

| Your MP | The step to ask for | The one-sentence case |
|---|---|---|
| Right-of-centre | Statutory threshold indexation (step 4); relief hygiene (step 1) | "Take ordinary families out of this tax permanently, and stop reliefs meant for working farms being used for tax planning" |
| Left-of-centre | Recipient basis (step 2); pilot endowment (step 5) | "Tax the windfall, not the life's work — Ireland has done it for decades — and trial a capital grant at 25" |
| Centrist / third party | Gift register (step 3); recipient basis (step 2) | "Close the gifts loophole with simple record-keeping and make the receiver, not the estate, the taxpayer" |

Close every letter with a question that requires a reply ("Will you raise this with the Treasury team?") — a reply creates a thread; a thread creates a record; records aggregate into the backbench signal that [../14-communications/overton-strategies.md](../14-communications/overton-strategies.md) rung 5 needs (judgement).

## 5. Supporter FAQ

The questions sympathisers actually ask before acting, answered in two sentences each.

**Q1. Is this real? Is anyone serious about it?**
It is currently a research corpus with a 15–25 year theory of change and falsifiable milestones — which is exactly what the neoliberals and UBI movements looked like at the same stage ([theory-of-change.md](theory-of-change.md), [README.md](README.md)). Seriousness at this stage *is* the depth of the written work; read three files and judge for yourself.

**Q2. Who's behind it? Who funds it?**
Nobody yet: no organisation, no funder, no membership list — the honest starting position stated in [README.md](README.md), with the funding-transparency rules it will be held to written in advance in [funding.md](funding.md). That vacancy is the invitation: early supporters are not joining something, they are constituting it.

**Q3. Isn't this politically impossible?**
Inheritance-tax opinion is the *most movable* opinion in the experimental literature — a single information treatment roughly tripled support for raising the US estate tax ([../06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)) — and the window analysis says the constraint is time, not feasibility ([closing-window.md](closing-window.md)). "Impossible" was said, at the same distance, of every policy in the precedents file ([precedents.md](precedents.md)).

**Q4. What about my kids? I want to leave them something.**
You can: £500,000 *per child*, tax-free, is more than nine in ten heirs will ever receive, and every child and grandchild collects a £50k endowment at 25 regardless ([../06-palatability/mass-affluent.md](../06-palatability/mass-affluent.md)). Do the division-by-recipients arithmetic before deciding you're a loser — the conversation script in [../14-communications/personal-conversations.md](../14-communications/personal-conversations.md) starts exactly there.

**Q5. I only have an hour a month. Is that worth anything?**
Yes — rungs 2–4 fit inside it: stay on the list, keep the small donation running, and spend the hour on one MP letter or one good conversation. A thousand people doing only this is the sympathiser ring that makes every higher rung fundable and credible ([recruitment.md](recruitment.md) ring 4).

**Q6. I'm wealthy — am I the enemy?**
No: the design taxes the *receipt* of dynastic windfalls, not success, and most "wealthy" families are arithmetic winners once the estate is divided per recipient ([../06-palatability/mass-affluent.md](../06-palatability/mass-affluent.md)). If you or your children stand to inherit above the threshold and support this anyway, you are the movement's single most credible messenger — see [heir-defectors.md](heir-defectors.md).

**Q7. Will I be attacked for supporting this?**
Rungs 0–8 are private or local; only rung 9+ carries public exposure, and it is taken deliberately, with the disclosure-first and support protocols in [../10-opposition/personal-attacks.md](../10-opposition/personal-attacks.md). Nobody should arrive at rung 9 by accident.

**Q8. Why not just give my time or money to an existing party or charity?**
Because no existing organisation owns this demand — that gap is stage 2 of [theory-of-change.md](theory-of-change.md), and until it is filled, generic donations fund adjacent causes, not this one. If a party or think-tank later adopts a recognisable stepping-stone, supporting them there *is* supporting this ([../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md)).

## 6. The one-rung ask

Whatever rung you are on, the doctrine of this file compresses to one sentence: **do your rung well, and take one step up when asked.** The movement's job ([recruitment.md](recruitment.md)) is to make sure you are asked.
