# Coalitions: Adjacent Movements and the Terms of Alliance

**Executive summary.** The movement's natural allies range from housing campaigners and tax-justice networks to faith traditions and even deficit hawks — but every alliance has a divergence point, and coalition doctrine is about knowing each partner's real objective, what we can offer them, and where the alliance will strain before it does. The binding demand across the coalition should be the minimum shared sentence — "large inheritances should fund a universal endowment" — with the 100% design retained as our position, not the coalition's.

## Principles before partners

1. **Coalitions trade in interests, not affections.** Each row below is an exchange, not a friendship; the alliance holds while the exchange holds.
2. **The shared demand must be smaller than our full position.** Partners sign the minimum sentence; the manifesto's full design ([02-proposal/](../02-proposal/README.md)) remains ours alone. Forcing partners to endorse "100%" would shrink the coalition to zero — see purism as a movement-killer in [precedents.md](precedents.md).
3. **Guard the single issue.** Per [organisational-forms.md](organisational-forms.md), we join partners' coalitions as a named single-issue org; we do not absorb their causes into our platform.

## Coalition-management doctrine

1. **Bilateral before multilateral.** Build two-party working relationships (shared research, co-signed letters, platform slots at each other's events) before attempting any grand alliance; multilateral coalitions formed early spend their energy on internal governance.
2. **Write the divergence down at the start.** Each alliance begins with a private memo of where we will split (the table's final column). Ruptures that were predicted in writing are manageable; surprises become public feuds ([10-opposition/media-narratives.md](../10-opposition/media-narratives.md) will amplify any ally-on-ally fire).
3. **Never spend a partner's credibility without consent.** Partners' names appear on nothing they have not cleared. One burned ally poisons the reference network all future alliances draw on.
4. **Reciprocity ledger.** Track asks and gives per partner. Movements that only ask get quietly dropped; a deliberate practice of showing up for partners' fights (where compatible with single-issue discipline) is what makes ours attended.
5. **The swing-audience test for joint appearances.** Some partners help with the base but hurt with the decisive swing audiences (older homeowners, small-business owners — [06-palatability/README.md](../06-palatability/README.md)). Alliance is not the same as co-branding: work with everyone useful, share stages selectively, matching messenger to audience per [messengers.md](messengers.md).
6. **Expect and pre-plan the wealth-tax question.** The most persistent internal coalition debate will be inheritance tax vs annual wealth tax. Doctrine: they are complements, not rivals, but our organisation argues inheritance-first on administrability and political-narrative grounds ([02-proposal/mechanism.md](../02-proposal/mechanism.md), [09-objections/whataboutery.md](../09-objections/whataboutery.md)); we do not attack wealth-tax campaigns in public.

## Sequencing

Phase-1 alliances (research-stage): tax-justice networks and academic inequality centres — they trade in the currency we already have (analysis). Phase-3 alliances (campaign-stage): housing, intergenerational, faith, and union partners — they trade in mobilisation, which we can only reciprocate once chapters exist ([organisational-forms.md](organisational-forms.md), [recruitment.md](recruitment.md)). Approaching mobilisation partners before we can reciprocate spends credibility we cannot yet replace.
