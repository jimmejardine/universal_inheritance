# Recruitment: The Concentric-Circle Model

**Executive summary.** The movement recruits in five concentric rings — core, contributors, advocates, sympathisers, voters — each with its own sourcing pool, ask, conversion mechanism, and retention package. The highest-leverage recruits are not believers but *defectors*: tax lawyers, wealth managers, and inheritance-industry professionals whose insider knowledge and biographical credibility cannot be bought.

## The concentric-circle model

People do not join movements; they accept escalating asks. Each ring below is defined by the size of the ask it has accepted. The organising task is to move people inward one ring at a time — the "ladder of engagement" from community-organising practice, and the leadership-development logic of Marshall Ganz's "snowflake" model, in which every member is developed into a recruiter of further members rather than remaining a consumer of the campaign (to verify attribution and canonical description).

| Ring | Who | Approx. target size at maturity | The ask |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Core | Researchers, writers, strategists | 5–15 | Own a section of the corpus; multi-year commitment |
| 2. Contributors | Economists, tax lawyers, communicators, designers | 30–100 | Lend expertise and name to specific outputs |
| 3. Advocates | Activists, local organisers, chapter leads | 500–5,000 | Recruit, host, show up, deliver the message locally |
| 4. Sympathisers | Newsletter subscribers, followers, small donors | 50k–500k | Attention, small money, occasional signal (petition, share) |
| 5. Voters | The persuadable public | Millions | A vote, when the time comes |

## Ring-by-ring doctrine

### Ring 1 — Core

- **Where found:** economics and public-policy postgraduates; policy staff between jobs; writers already publishing on inequality; the authors of this corpus.
- **The ask:** own a section (e.g. [07-implementation/](../07-implementation/README.md)) as its named maintainer; commit to years, not months.
- **Conversion mechanism:** direct personal invitation after they have engaged with the corpus. Core members are never recruited by broadcast — they are courted individually on the strength of their existing work.
- **What they need:** intellectual ownership, co-authorship credit, a stipend as soon as funding allows ([funding.md](funding.md)), and genuine influence over doctrine. Core people leave when they become executors of someone else's fixed vision — see founder-capture governance in [organisational-forms.md](organisational-forms.md).

### Ring 2 — Contributors

- **Where found:** university economics and law departments; tax-professional bodies (CIOT, STEP in the UK; ABA tax section in the US — to verify openness); journalists on wealth beats; comms professionals sympathetic to inequality causes.
- **The ask:** one bounded, prestigious task — peer-review a chapter, model a revenue estimate, draft an avoidance memo, design a message test.
- **Conversion mechanism:** the bounded ask itself. Experts rarely "join movements" but readily accept a well-specified request that respects their time and yields a citable output.
- **What they need:** professional cover (association with a credible institution, not a fringe campaign — see [organisational-forms.md](organisational-forms.md)), named credit or guaranteed anonymity as they prefer, and a research agenda worth their career time. For academic economists specifically, the offer is a **new research programme**: inheritance flows, endowment effects ([16-second-order/](../16-second-order/README.md)), and behavioural responses are under-studied and publishable — the movement supplies questions, data access, and audience.

### Ring 3 — Advocates

- **Where found:** existing activist networks in adjacent movements ([coalitions.md](coalitions.md)) — housing/YIMBY groups, tax-justice campaigns, intergenerational-fairness organisations, student politics, union youth wings.
- **The ask:** run a local group, table an event, deliver the debate-toolkit arguments ([14-communications/debate-toolkit.md](../14-communications/debate-toolkit.md)), and — per snowflake doctrine — recruit and develop their own team.
- **Conversion mechanism:** the ladder: subscriber → attends one event → takes one small task → is asked to lead. The critical organising discipline is that every event ends with a specific next ask; movements that let attendance be terminal convert nobody.
- **What they need:** tools (slide decks, one-pagers, rebuttal cards — owned by [14-communications/](../14-communications/README.md)), status (titles, recognition, pathways toward rings 1–2), and community (the social bond is what retains volunteers when the news cycle moves on).

### Ring 4 — Sympathisers

- **Where found:** readers of the eventual long-form manifesto; audiences of aligned podcasts and newsletters; viral moments around dynastic-wealth news stories.
- **The ask:** an email address, then a small donation, then a share, then an event RSVP.
- **Conversion mechanism:** content with a capture step. Every essay, thread, and interview must end at a subscription point; a viral moment with no capture infrastructure is wasted ([theory-of-change.md](theory-of-change.md), stage 3 doctrine).
- **What they need:** a regular, high-quality product (newsletter as flagship), visible momentum, and a clear picture of what their small contribution buys.

### Ring 5 — Voters

Not recruited directly; reached through rings 3–4, coalition partners, and eventually parties ([12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md](../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md)). The movement's job for this ring is to make the endowment offer legible: "you, or your children, receive £50,000 at 25."

## The defector doctrine

The most valuable recruits are people whose current position argues *against* their joining. Their testimony is unfakeable and pre-rebuts the opposition's strongest frames ([10-opposition/attack-playbook.md](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)).

| Defector type | What they bring | Where found | Tailored ask |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tax lawyers, estate planners | Where the avoidance bodies are buried: trusts, gifting patterns, valuation games ([07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md](../07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md)) | STEP/CIOT membership, private-client practices, recent retirees with loosened client obligations | Anonymous technical review first; public authorship later if willing |
| Wealth managers, private bankers | How dynastic wealth actually behaves — flight realism for [08-capital-flight/evidence.md](../08-capital-flight/evidence.md) | Family offices, private banks; disillusioned mid-career leavers | Background briefings; "what would actually make your ex-clients stay" |
| Academic economists | Credibility, modelling, the inside track | Public-economics groups; early-career researchers needing a distinctive agenda | A funded, publishable research programme |
| Inheritance-industry professionals (probate, insurance) | Operational realism for [07-implementation/administration.md](../07-implementation/administration.md) | Professional bodies, trade press | Consultation on administrability |
| Wealthy would-be inheritors and founders | The messenger asset — treated fully in [messengers.md](messengers.md) | Patriotic Millionaires and adjacent networks | Public testimony, funding, doors |

Doctrine for handling defectors: (1) **anonymity first** — most will help privately years before speaking publicly; build the graduated path. (2) **Never burn a source** for a news cycle. (3) **Verify** — a fake or exaggerated defector story is a gift to [10-opposition/media-narratives.md](../10-opposition/media-narratives.md).

## Metrics

Track ring transitions, not headline totals: subscribers who attend (4→3), attendees who take a task (3 internal), contributors converted to core (2→1). A large ring 4 with no inward flow is an audience, not a movement — the Warren-wealth-tax failure mode described in [precedents.md](precedents.md).
