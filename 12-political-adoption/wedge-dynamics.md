# Wedge Dynamics: How the Policy Splits Opposing Coalitions

**Executive summary.** Modern electoral coalitions on both left and right are alliances of asset-rich and asset-poor voters, and a recipient-based inheritance tax with a universal endowment cuts across both alliances along the asset line rather than the party line. This file maps where each coalition splits, the design choices that sharpen the wedge against opponents while blunting it for adopters, and the signature manoeuvre — forcing opponents to defend billionaire heirs on television.

## The structural insight

Left–right coalitions are organised around work, culture, and identity; this policy is organised around *unearned receipts*. Because the two axes are not aligned, every major coalition contains both winners and losers from the policy — which makes it wedge material by construction. Whoever adopts first gets to choose which coalition's internal contradiction becomes the story.

| Coalition | Asset-rich wing | Asset-poor wing | Where the policy cuts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Right (Conservative/GOP-type) | Donors, landowners, wealthy retirees, financial and property interests | Renting and small-savings social conservatives; younger right-leaning workers; "left-behind" towns | The endowment and the 0%-below-£500k tax cut appeal directly to the asset-poor wing; only the donor wing pays |
| Left (Labour/Democrat-type) | Urban professionals with appreciating parental homes; public-sector upper-middle class expecting six-figure inheritances | Renters, the young, minorities, low-wealth workers | The tax threatens the professional wing's expectations; the endowment serves the base |
| Centrist/liberal | Affluent graduates | Younger urban renters | Same split in miniature ([centrists-and-liberals.md](centrists-and-liberals.md)) |

The asymmetry that decides strategy: **the right's split is between its donors and its voters; the left's split is between two blocs of its voters.** A donors-versus-voters split is electorally worse — donors have no votes to withdraw, only money — but organisationally worse for adoption, since donors sit inside the party machine ([right-parties.md](right-parties.md)). A voters-versus-voters split is electorally more dangerous but designable away, since the exposed left-wing bloc is mostly below the threshold ([left-parties.md](left-parties.md)).

## Wedge plays, by attacker

### Left party attacking a right party

The play: force right politicians to publicly defend the least sympathetic beneficiaries of the status quo. The mechanics:

1. **Table the divisible question.** A bill or amendment on a narrow, maximally asymmetric target — e.g. capping relief claims on estates above £10m, or closing a trust loophole ([stepping-stones.md](stepping-stones.md), items 1 and 3). The right must whip against it or split.
2. **The television manoeuvre.** The question for every broadcast appearance: *"Why should someone who inherits £20 million pay a lower effective rate than a nurse pays on her wages?"* There is no rehearsed answer that survives contact, because the honest answer — "our donors" — is unsayable, and every alternative ("aspiration", "double taxation") has a pre-built rebuttal ([../09-objections/fairness.md](../09-objections/fairness.md), [../14-communications/debate-toolkit.md](../14-communications/debate-toolkit.md)). The goal is not to win the argument but to make defending billionaire heirs the right's *recurring televised posture*.
3. **Offer the endowment to the right's asset-poor wing directly.** Constituency-level campaigning in post-industrial and coastal right-held seats: "their donors' grandchildren get the estates; your grandchildren get nothing — unless."

### Right party attacking a left party

The mirror play, less obvious but real:

1. **Adopt the tax-cut half first.** "0% below £500k" outbids the left's caution on IHT for ordinary families ([right-parties.md](right-parties.md)) and forces left professionals to argue against a tax cut their own base wants.
2. **Expose the left's quiet inheritors.** The attack line: "they preach equality while their frontbench waits on Hampstead house money." Effective precisely because the left's professional wing *is* inheritance-exposed; a left party that has not pre-committed to the recipient design has no clean answer.
3. **Claim the endowment as property-owning democracy.** A right party that moves first converts the left's signature-in-waiting into a conservative institution, permanently ([right-parties.md](right-parties.md)).

## Design choices that tune the wedge

The same policy can be specified to maximise the split in the opposing coalition and minimise it in one's own. The levers:

| Design lever | Wedge-sharpening setting | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Threshold level | High (£500k per recipient) | Shrinks the adopter's exposed wing to near-zero; isolates opponents as defenders of seven-figure receipts only ([../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)) |
| Recipient basis | Always | Converts "your dad's estate" into "the heir's windfall"; the attack surface moves from the sympathetic dead to the unsympathetic living ([../06-palatability/framing.md](../06-palatability/framing.md)) |
| Hypothecation to the endowment | Explicit and statutory | Every attack on the tax becomes an attack on the endowment: "which 25-year-olds lose their £50,000 under your plan?" |
| Farm/business carve-outs | Announced in the same sentence | Removes the opponents' only sympathetic televised cases ([../02-proposal/carveouts.md](../02-proposal/carveouts.md), [../04-case-against/family-business.md](../04-case-against/family-business.md)) |
| First target selection | Reliefs and trusts used by the top 0.1% ([stepping-stones.md](stepping-stones.md)) | Maximises the donor-versus-voter distance in the opposing coalition |
| Pace | Stepwise | Each step re-poses the unanswerable question at a new margin; opponents must fight every step on the worst possible ground |

Note the symmetry warning: every lever works in reverse against a sloppy adopter. A low threshold, estate-based language, no carve-outs, and general-revenue destination is the same policy specified to wedge the *adopter's* coalition — which is roughly how existing IHT is specified, and why it polls as it does ([../06-palatability/README.md](../06-palatability/README.md)).

## Managing the wedge against yourself

Whichever party adopts must expect the mirror attack. Standing defences:

- **Make members and voters compute their own number.** The single most effective internal de-wedging tool is a personal-exposure calculator: "Will you receive more than £500,000 from all gifts and inheritances in your lifetime?" For ~90%+ of any party's base the answer is no (to verify), and a computed "no" inoculates better than any argument.
- **Pre-recruit the exposed as validators.** A visible handful of people *above* the threshold endorsing the policy ("my kids will be fine with £500k each; the country will not be fine without this") collapses the martyrdom narrative ([../13-movement/messengers.md](../13-movement/messengers.md), the Carnegie lineage in [../05-history/carnegie-1889.md](../05-history/carnegie-1889.md)).
- **Never defend the tax; always prosecute the alternative.** The debate stance is offensive: opponents are asked to justify the current arrangement — taxing wages at 20–45% while seven-figure windfalls pass at effective rates near zero via reliefs (to verify effective-rate studies; [../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md)) — not the adopter asked to justify the change.

## The "force them to defend billionaire heirs" manoeuvre, formalised

The manoeuvre generalises: **choose the margin of debate such that the opponent's least defensible client is the only one at stake.** Every stepping-stone in [stepping-stones.md](stepping-stones.md) is selected on this criterion — each isolates a target (relief-abusing estates, offshore trusts, unlimited lifetime gifts) whose televised defence costs opponents more than the measure itself gains. Ten such fights, each individually lost by the defenders of the status quo, move the Overton window further than one apocalyptic fight over "100%" ([../14-communications/overton-strategies.md](../14-communications/overton-strategies.md)); and by the tenth, the full scheme is the obvious conclusion of arguments the public has already accepted.

## Bottom line

The policy's wedge value is a party-strategic asset independent of its merits, and it is symmetric: it will be used by whoever picks it up first, against whoever hesitates. That symmetry is itself the movement's strongest card when briefing any party — the realistic choice on offer is not "adopt or ignore" but "wedge or be wedged" ([../13-movement/theory-of-change.md](../13-movement/theory-of-change.md)).
