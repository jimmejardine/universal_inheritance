# Target Politicians: A Map of the Receptive and the Reachable

**Executive summary.** This file maps, from the verifiable public record only, the politicians and office-holders — UK first, then Ireland, the US, and the EU — whose votes, bills, speeches, books, or manifesto roles put them closest to the recipient-based receipts tax and universal endowment ([../02-proposal/README.md](../02-proposal/README.md)), and pairs the map with an approach doctrine: which ask, at which rung, for which target. **Listing here implies no endorsement by any named person of this project or its proposal**; every characterisation is drawn from cited public acts, and any inference beyond the record is explicitly labelled *(judgement)*.

## Key findings

- A cross-party **APPG on Inheritance & Intergenerational Fairness** existed (2019–, chaired by Conservative John Stevenson, secretariat STEP) and recommended in January 2020 the wholesale **replacement of IHT** — with a donor-based flat-rate gift tax (10%, possibly 20% above £2m), not a lifetime receipts tax, an important correction to campaign folklore [1][2][3]. None of its four registered officers remains in the Commons after 2024, so its institutional memory now sits with STEP, not Parliament.
- The two most consequential UK sympathisers by record both sit on the Labour benches: **Torsten Bell**, who ran the Resolution Foundation when its Intergenerational Commission proposed the £10,000 citizen's inheritance funded by a **lifetime receipts tax** (£125k lifetime allowance, 20%/30%) and is now a Treasury minister [4][5][6], and **Liam Byrne**, who chairs the Business and Trade Committee and published a book-length attack on "inheritocracy" in 2024 [7][8].
- The **Liberal Democrats are the only UK party to have adopted, at conference (September 2018), the abolition of IHT in favour of a levy on lifetime receipts** with a £250,000 tax-free lifetime allowance — the closest any UK party policy has come to this proposal's architecture [9].
- In the US, the **tax-funds-endowment pairing already exists in draft law**: the Booker–Pressley American Opportunity Accounts Act is "fully paid for" by restoring the estate tax to 2009 levels [10][11], and the Sanders–Warren–Gomez For the 99.5 Percent Act supplies the graduated top-rate architecture (45–65% above $3.5m) [12][13].
- In the EU, the February 2025 French National Assembly vote for the 2% "Zucman" wealth floor (116–39, sponsored by **Éva Sas** and the Écologiste group) and the April 2024 G20 ministers' push (**Haddad, Schulze, Montero, Cuerpo, Godongwana**) identify the coordination-minded bloc — though these are wealth-floor records, not inheritance-tax records, and the French momentum reversed in late 2025 [14][15][16][17].

## How to read this map

Three disciplines govern every entry. **(1) Record only.** Each named person is characterised solely by cited public acts — votes, bills, reports, books, manifesto positions, Hansard. **(2) Judgement is labelled.** Where receptivity is inferred beyond the record, the inference carries *(judgement)*. **(3) No endorsement.** Nobody listed has endorsed this project, the 100% rate, or the £500k threshold; most named politicians would, on their current record, endorse only a fraction of it. The map's purpose is to sequence approaches ([policy-entrepreneurs.md](policy-entrepreneurs.md)), not to claim allies.

## United Kingdom

### The APPG on Inheritance & Intergenerational Fairness — the precedent to rebuild

The All-Party Parliamentary Group on Inheritance and Intergenerational Fairness was registered with the purpose of examining IHT's impact and intergenerational fairness, with STEP (the Society of Trust and Estate Practitioners) as its public enquiry point [1]. Its January 2020 report recommended abolishing the current IHT regime — 40% rate plus relief thicket — and replacing it with a **flat-rate gift tax on lifetime and death transfers** (suggested 10%, possibly 20% above £2m), abolishing all reliefs except spouse and charity, with a £30,000 annual gifts allowance [2][3]. Two campaign-relevant facts:

- **It is not a lifetime receipts tax.** The APPG's design taxes the *transfer*, not the recipient's cumulative receipts — donor-based, unlike Ireland's CAT or this proposal. What the report proves is narrower but still valuable: a cross-party parliamentary group, with a professional-body secretariat, concluded that IHT should be **replaced root-and-branch rather than patched** — the conceptual door through which recipient-based designs walk ([stepping-stones.md](stepping-stones.md), step 2).
- **Its parliamentary capital is gone.** The registered officers were John Stevenson (Con, chair), Sir George Howarth (Lab), Holly Lynch (Lab), and Dr Matthew Offord (Con) [1]. All four left the Commons at or by the 2024 election — Stevenson's Carlisle seat was abolished and he was not returned; Howarth, Lynch, and Offord stood down [18][19][20]. The group is listed as defunct. The durable asset is STEP's secretariat experience; the obvious movement play is a re-founded APPG staffed from the 2024 intake *(judgement)*.

### The 2024 intake and how to find the rest

The systematic play is a **Hansard and maiden-speech trawl of the 2024–26 intake** for intergenerational-fairness, housing-deposit, and "start in life" language, cross-referenced against majorities and committee seats — the young-MP cohort whose careers will span the Great Wealth Transfer is structurally the most receptive audience *(judgement)*, but no further individual should be named here without a specific citable record; that trawl is an open research task for this file. Torsten Bell is the verified exemplar of the type. A re-founded APPG needs two frontbench-adjacent Labour members, one Conservative moderniser, one Lib Dem, and a STEP-successor secretariat *(judgement)*.

## Devolved administrations and Ireland

- **Scotland/Wales/NI:** inheritance taxation is a **reserved matter** (Scotland Act 1998, Sch. 5) — Holyrood cannot legislate any of this, and the repo's position is that it should stay reserved (the Australian sub-national cascade lesson) — see [../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md](../15-transition/legislative-path-uk.md). Consequence: Scottish and Welsh targets are their **Westminster** MPs, not devolved politicians.

## The approach doctrine

The ask-ladder, keyed to [stepping-stones.md](stepping-stones.md) and the pipeline in [policy-entrepreneurs.md](policy-entrepreneurs.md) — each rung deniable, each cheap for the politician, each banked before the next:

| Rung | Ask | Right-sized target (from the tables above) |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Private briefing | Two-pager: costings, attack rebuttals, draft announcement | Byrne; Willetts; Bowles; Booker/Pressley staff |
| 2. Parliamentary question | PQ/Lords oral on receipts-basis, gift-ledger data, or endowment costings | Bowles (Lords); any 2024-intake sympathiser identified by the Hansard trawl |
| 3. Debate | Westminster Hall / Lords QSD on intergenerational wealth transfer | Willetts + Bowles cross-bench pairing *(judgement)* |
| 4. PMB / ten-minute rule | A Receipts Register bill (stepping-stone 3) — plumbing, not rates | A backbencher recruited at rung 2; the bill text is the artefact |
| 5. Committee inquiry | Treasury Committee inquiry into wealth-transfer taxation post-APR/BPR | Hillier's committee; Byrne's committee for the endowment/ownership side |
| 6. Manifesto | Party adoption of steps 1–3 + pilot endowment | Lib Dems (revive 2018 policy); Greens (specify their 2024 line); Labour (fiscal-event window) |

**What each buyer needs** ([party-incentives.md](party-incentives.md)): Labour needs the funding-credibility and monument story ([left-parties.md](left-parties.md)); the Conservatives need the "0% below £500k" tax-cut framing and a donor-constraint workaround ([right-parties.md](right-parties.md)); the Lib Dems and Greens need a signature and have the least distance to travel ([centrists-and-liberals.md](centrists-and-liberals.md)).

**Staffer-level entry points** *(judgement)*: select-committee specialist advisers ahead of inquiry scoping; opposition-team researchers in pre-manifesto years; STEP's technical committee as the once-proven secretariat channel; Treasurer's-office staff in US states with live baby-bond programmes. The SpAd logic — products, not arguments — is set out in [policy-entrepreneurs.md](policy-entrepreneurs.md).

## Honest limits

- **Records go stale.** Portfolios, committee chairs, and party lines cited here are as verified in early-to-mid 2026; every entry must be re-verified immediately before any approach.
- **Nobody listed endorses this proposal.** The nearest records (RF's LRT, Lib Dem 2018 policy, Booker's pairing) are all at far lower rates and smaller endowments; the gap between "receptive to the architecture" and "supports 100% above £500k" is the whole persuasion task.
- **The APPG folklore correction matters.** Repeating "an APPG recommended a lifetime receipts tax" would be falsifiable and costly; it recommended a donor-based flat-rate gift tax [2][3]. Precision is the movement's credibility.
- **The EU entries are adjacency, not alignment** — wealth-floor votes and letters, not inheritance-tax advocacy.
- The systematic gaps: the 2024-intake Hansard trawl; current Lib Dem policy status; Sinn Féin/SocDem positioning in the next Irish coalition round; whether the 99.5 Percent Act and AOAA are re-introduced in the current Congress.
