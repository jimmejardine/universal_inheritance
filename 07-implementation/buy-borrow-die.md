# Buy, Borrow, Die: Debt as the Last Untaxed Transfer

**Executive summary.** "Buy, borrow, die" is the marquee strategy by which the largest US fortunes escape both income tax (never sell, so never realise) and estate tax (step-up erases the gain, leverage shrinks the estate) — and any receipts-tax design that stays silent on debt invites its heirs and mutations. This file verifies the classic strategy at documented scale, shows why it collapses into either honest consumption or a disguised transfer under a recipient-based tax with no step-up, pins the debt-netting rule the design has so far lacked, works the startup variants, and prices — honestly — what it costs to police debt against a 100% incentive.

## Findings

- **The classic strategy is real, named, and industrial-scale.** The term was coined by USC tax professor Edward McCaffery in the mid-1990s [1]; securities-backed loans stood at ~$138bn outstanding in the US (2024:Q1, Federal Reserve estimate), beside ~$180bn of margin loans [3]; regulators flagged the sales boom a decade ago [2]; insider share-pledging is so routine the SEC requires a proxy disclosure line for it (Reg S-K Item 403(b)) [5]; and the marquee case is public record — Musk had ~236m of his ~715m Tesla shares pledged as loan collateral in 2024 filings, and the original 2022 Twitter financing included a record $12.5bn margin loan against Tesla stock, later dropped [6][7].
- **BBD defeats taxes that trigger on realisation and death. This tax triggers on neither** — it triggers on the heir's *receipt* at market value, with basis uplift "earned by inclusion, never granted by death" ([`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §1). Without step-up, the borrow leg is deferral at commercial interest, not escape.
- **The pinned rule (DESIGN DECISION, §2):** receipts are measured **net of genuine, arm's-length, actually-discharged debts** — but connected-party and contrived debts are **non-deductible**, on the architecture of the UK's FA 2013 IHT debt-deduction restrictions (IHTA 1984 ss.162A/175A) [8].
- **The founder who borrows against his own stake and spends the proceeds is complying, not avoiding.** Dying indebted to a real bank after consuming the money is die-with-zero, which the design welcomes ([`../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md`](../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md) §3).
- **The startup variants split cleanly:** borrowing against your own equity is your own consumption (untaxed by design); options and RSUs from your own employment are employment income (ITEPA ERS / IRC §83) [10][11]; but cheap options, founder shares, or carry granted to *heirs* are receipts at grant, detected through the employer feed the UK already runs (ERS annual returns) [10] and priced with §409A-grade appraisal discipline [12].
- **Detection is consumption-shaped:** lender feeds into the mesh, estate-debt verification at probate, imputed interest against published official rates (HMRC ORI / IRS AFR) [13][14], a lifestyle/net-worth audit input, and the generalised collateral cross-check — the taxpayer's own loan documents testify.
- **Honest limits at full strength (§6):** nothing here has run at a 100% incentive; the derivatives frontier is unresolved; pure-DeFi lending has no intermediary to report; and the netting rule creates a debt-pricing game that only the official rate anchors.

## 1. The classic strategy, verified

**Mechanics.** Buy (or found) an appreciating asset. Never sell: unrealised gains are not income, so no income tax falls due. Borrow against the asset to fund life — loan proceeds are not income either, because they are offset by an obligation to repay; securities-backed lines of credit (SBLOCs) advance 50–95% of portfolio value at rates far below the capital-gains tax that a sale would trigger [2]. Die: in the US, §1014 steps the heirs' basis up to date-of-death value, erasing every dollar of deferred gain — a subsidy the JCT prices at $72.5bn of forgone revenue in 2026 alone (repo-verified — [`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §1) — while the outstanding debt *reduces* the taxable estate. The estate repays the lender by selling stepped-up stock tax-free; the heirs keep the rest. Income tax never touched the gain; estate tax touched a leveraged residue above an eight-figure exemption. The name is Edward McCaffery's, coined at USC in the mid-1990s to teach students how the system already worked, and mainstreamed when ProPublica's 2021 IRS-data reporting cited his work [1]. The UK runs a quieter version on the same logic: the CGT death uplift (TCGA 1992 s.62) forgives lifetime gains at death, so "borrow against the portfolio, die, uplift" works with a 40% IHT toll rather than a repeal-sized exemption ([`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §1).

**Scale.** This is not an exotic structure but a product line. FINRA and the SEC issued a joint investor alert in 2015 as brokerages pushed SBLOCs — one large firm reported a 70% increase in its securities-based lending business over 2012–14, another over 50% [2]. A Federal Reserve note estimates US securities-based loans at **$138bn outstanding in 2024:Q1**, alongside ~$180bn of margin loans — roughly $318bn of borrowing against portfolios [3]. At the private-bank tier, Bloomberg's reporting of Q2 2021 earnings put Morgan Stanley's securities-backed and tailored lending book near $76bn (up 43% year-on-year) and Bank of America's at $67bn (up more than 20%) [4].

**The insider tier is disclosed by rule.** SEC Regulation S-K Item 403(b) requires companies to disclose, in the beneficial-ownership table of the proxy, shares that directors and named executive officers have **pledged as security** [5] — pledging by controlling shareholders is common enough to have its own line in US securities law. The documented marquee case: Tesla's filings showed Musk with roughly **236 million of his ~715 million shares pledged** as collateral for personal indebtedness as of August 2024 (down slightly from 238.4m that March), against a board policy capping pledged borrowing at the lesser of $3.5bn or 25% of the pledged stock's value [6]. And in April 2022 the original Twitter acquisition financing included a **record $12.5bn margin loan secured against Tesla shares**, personally guaranteed — subsequently dropped from the package when Musk raised equity co-investors instead [6][7]. The strategy's fingerprints are on the largest acquisition financing ever attempted by an individual.

**Why it works.** Both taxes it defeats are *event* taxes with the wrong events. Income tax waits for realisation, which the borrower never supplies; estate tax waits for death, at which point step-up has already destroyed the income-tax claim and the debt deduction has shrunk the estate-tax base. Derivative monetisation runs the same race one lane over — prepaid variable forwards deliver most of a stake's cash value up front while deferring the gain (Rev. Rul. 2003-7; *Estate of McKelvey*) — verified in [`../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md`](../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md) §3 and not re-cited here.

## 2. Why BBD breaks under the receipts design

Three pillars, the second of which is new and binding.

**(a) Death-escape is irrelevant, because death was never the taxable event.** The tax point is the **heir's receipt at market value**, whenever it occurs ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §4). Whether the founder realised gains in life has no bearing on the heir's ledger: the full date-of-receipt value posts against the £500k lifetime threshold and is taxed at 100% above it. There is no step-up to harvest — the recipient's market-value basis exists *only because* the value entered the receipts base: "basis uplift is earned by inclusion, never granted by death" ([`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §1; the step-up-repeal precondition for any US variant is [`../05-history/us-estate-tax.md`](../05-history/us-estate-tax.md)'s standing lesson). And the borrow leg loses its second engine too: when the estate or heir sells assets to repay the lender, the accrued gain is realised and taxed on ordinary CGT principles — deferral ends at the grave instead of being forgiven there. Borrowing against an appreciated stake becomes what it always pretended to be: a financing choice, paying a bank interest to delay a tax.

**(b) DESIGN DECISION — the debt-netting rule.** The receipt definition in [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §1 has until now been silent on liabilities. The rule is pinned here:

> A taxable receipt is measured **net of liabilities genuinely attached to what is received** — a bequest of a mortgaged house is a receipt of the equity — subject to three conditions. **(i) Arm's-length origin:** the debt was owed to an unconnected commercial creditor, on market terms, for value actually advanced. **(ii) Actual discharge:** the deduction is allowed only to the extent the liability is actually discharged out of the estate or assumed with full recourse by the recipient — a debt that is never repaid was never a debt. **(iii) Connected-party non-deduction:** liabilities owed to connected persons, or to trusts, companies, foundations or partnerships within the family's perimeter, are **not deductible** from the receipts measure; and where such a debt *is* repaid, the repayment is itself a taxable receipt by the creditor to the extent the creditor sits inside the dynasty's perimeter (look-through).

The precedent is direct and current law: **Finance Act 2013** inserted IHTA 1984 ss.162A–162C and 175A precisely because IHT debt deductions were being manufactured — s.162A sets liabilities used to acquire excluded property against that property rather than the taxable estate, and s.175A **disallows any deduction for a debt not actually discharged from the estate** unless there is a real commercial reason, policed through HMRC's IHTM28000 series [8][9]. This design takes the same architecture and strengthens it for a 100% marginal rate: current UK law asks whether the debt is real and repaid; this design additionally asks *to whom* — because at 100%, a deductible debt owed to one's own family trust is a transfer wearing a liability's clothes. Dying owing £2bn to Morgan Stanley after spending the proceeds reduces what heirs receive, and correctly so. Dying owing £2bn to the family's own Jersey vehicle reduces nothing.

**(c) Die-with-zero is the intended exit, not a loophole.** The founder who borrows £2bn against his stake and spends it on living has **pre-consumed his estate**. At death the stake passes, the arm's-length debt is discharged from it (netting rule satisfied), and the heirs receive — and are taxed on — the genuinely smaller residue. This is not leakage: lifetime consumption is the one deliberately untaxed use of a fortune ([`../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md`](../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md) §3), and a fortune converted into decades of spending has, from the proposal's standpoint, complied. The design's target is transmission, and the money was not transmitted.

**The punchline.** Take away step-up and the income-tax escape dies with the borrower; take away deductibility of dynasty-debt and the estate-shrinking leg only works when the debt is real, external, and repaid. What remains of "buy, borrow, die" is a choice between **honest consumption** (welcomed) and **a disguised transfer** (a receipt, however documented). The strategy does not survive; it decomposes into the two things it was always a blend of.

## 3. The startup variant

Concentrated private equity is where the next generation of BBD would be engineered, so it gets its own treatment.

**(a) What is *not* a receipts-tax event — and deliberately so.** A founder borrowing against her own (vested or expected) equity is monetising her own property for her own consumption: estate-reducing, welcome, and untaxed by this design for the same reason as §2(c). The market exists and is growing — specialist lenders and private banks advance against pre-IPO stock and unexercised options, non-recourse structures included, precisely because founders are paper-rich and cash-poor [15][4]. Equally outside this tax: options and RSUs earned from one's **own employment**. Those are employment income on existing rules — in the UK, employment-related securities under ITEPA 2003, reported through the ERS regime [10]; in the US, §83 taxes the property at vest (or at grant, under an 83(b) election) at fair market value [11]. The receipts tax engages only on **gratuitous transfers between persons** ([`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §3); what you earn is the income tax's business.

**(b) The variants that *are* transfers — each with its catch.**

- **Cheap options, founder shares, and carry granted to heirs.** The founder has the company issue the 22-year-old daughter founder-class shares at par, or options struck at a token price, or a carried-interest allocation in the family fund — economically a gift of an appreciating claim, dressed as capitalisation. The rule: the grant is a **receipt at grant, valued at the market value of the right granted** ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §1(vi) — below-market transfer of the shortfall), with growth after grant belonging to the daughter as her own property. Two pieces of existing machinery make this administrable. *Valuation:* pricing an option or growth share on private stock is exactly what the US §409A appraisal industry already does at scale — independent appraisal, safe-harbour presumption, refreshed annually or on material events [12]. *Detection:* the UK already operates an **employer feed** — companies must register employment-related-securities arrangements and file an annual ERS return (by 6 July, nil returns required, automatic penalties) covering acquisitions by employees and directors [10]; extending the return to flag grants to *connected persons of controlling shareholders* on non-commercial terms turns the existing form into the detection surface. The recharacterisation boundary runs on substance: granted qua employee on terms an unconnected hire could get → employment income (Part 7A and ERS rules — [`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §3); granted qua child on terms no unconnected person could get → ledger receipt. This owns the gap flagged in [`../16-second-order/corporations.md`](../16-second-order/corporations.md) (honest limits) and [nepotism-and-networks.md](nepotism-and-networks.md) §1's salary test gets its equity twin.
- **Parent-guaranteed and parent-collateralised heir credit lines.** The bank lends the heir £5m at prime because the parent guarantees it or pledges her portfolio as collateral — the heir gets the balance sheet's benefit without any asset moving. **DESIGN DECISION:** a guarantee or collateral pledge by a connected person is (i) an **annual imputed receipt** of the guarantee-fee shortfall — the market price of the credit support minus anything actually paid for it — while it remains outstanding, on the same architecture as the soft-loan and use-of-assets imputation in [boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md) §3; and (ii) a **receipt in full** of any amount the guarantor or her estate actually pays out, or of any collateral actually applied, at the date it happens. Ordinary parental co-signing of a first mortgage sits below the materiality floor by design ([boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md) §3); standing £50m of collateral behind an heir's lifestyle line does not.
- **Derivative delivery of stake economics to an heir.** A prepaid variable forward, total-return swap, or collar-and-loan package that delivers the value of the founder's stake to the heir's account without moving title is a **receipt of the value delivered** — the substance-over-form rule now stated in [`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §3A, with the mechanics and the McKelvey litigation record in [`../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md`](../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md) §3.

## 4. The mutation catalogue

Each entry is **labelled speculative** — these are the designs an adviser would try next, anticipated rather than observed — and each is answered with its honest enforcement cost.

| Mutation (speculative) | The design's answer | Honest cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Rolling never-mature family loans** — parent "lends" £2m, refinances or capitalises interest forever, forgives nothing | **Design refinement (labelled):** refinancing, rolling over, or capitalising interest does **not** restart [boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md) §3's 10-year dormancy clock — the presumption of forgiveness runs from first advance unless interest is **actually paid** at ≥ the official rate; any shortfall is an annual receipt | Tracking real payment flows, not paperwork; audit must follow bank records |
| **Borrowing against expectancy** — bank lends heir today against the anticipated bequest | **DESIGN DECISION (labelled):** the receipts-tax charge has **statutory priority at the estate** — it ranks ahead of the heir's assignees and unsecured lenders, and probate cannot be granted until the Estate Information Return is lodged ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §5) — so a lender against expectancy can only ever reach the post-tax residue. | **Private annuities and instalment sales to heirs** — asset sold to heir for a lifetime annuity or soft instalment note | Below-market transfer rules price the gift element as a receipt ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §1(vi)); actuarial scrutiny of annuity pricing under [valuation.md](valuation.md); the instalment note is then a debt inside the netting rule — connected, hence non-deductible at the seller's death if unpaid | Actuarial valuation disputes; mortality gaming at the margin |
| **Insurance wrappers around debt structures** | Full look-through per [`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §3 — payout is a receipt regardless of wrapper | Already priced there |

## 5. Detection as consumption: the consolidated architecture

The common structure of every §4 answer: debt-based avoidance cannot stay abstract — it must eventually **fund a life** — so detection concentrates where borrowing meets living.

1. **Lender feeds join the mesh.** Regulated banks, brokers and lenders report — above a floor — securities-backed and other collateralised credit lines, pledges of assets, and guarantees **where a connected person is the borrower, guarantor, or collateral provider** ([gifts-and-timing.md](gifts-and-timing.md) §5, now amended). The precedent that pledge-reporting is workable is already on the statute book: US proxy statements have disclosed insider share pledges under Item 403(b) for years [5], and SBLOC books are line-items in bank earnings [3][4]. Ordinary mortgages and consumer credit stay out of scope unless a connected party stands behind them.
2. **Estate-debt verification at probate.** The executor claiming a deduction under the §2 netting rule evidences **arm's-length origin** (loan documentation, value advanced, market terms) and **actual discharge**; connected-party debts are disallowed on the face of the Estate Information Return. This is the FA 2013 / IHTM28000 compliance pattern [8][9], run at the probate gate the design already has.
3. **Imputed interest is codified against a published rate.** The annual soft-loan and guarantee-shortfall receipts (§3, [boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md) §3) reference the **HMRC official rate of interest** (3.75% from April 2025, reviewed quarterly) [13] and the **IRS Applicable Federal Rates** (published monthly by revenue ruling) [14] — both long-standing published benchmarks, so the imputation needs no bespoke rate-setting.
4. **The lifestyle input is named.** The audit risk model ([enforcement.md](enforcement.md) §2) consumes a net-worth/consumption reconciliation: **consumption materially exceeding declared income plus declared receipts plus documented arm's-length borrowing** is a flag. This extends [gifts-and-timing.md](gifts-and-timing.md) §4's unexplained-accretions presumption from assets to living standards — the point of BBD-style structures is to convert hidden capital into visible life, and the visible life is the evidence.
5. **The collateral cross-check, generalised.** The section doctrine — "the taxpayer's own documents testify" ([README.md](README.md)) — extends from art insurance schedules to **all pledged assets**: a loan file in which the family certifies to its bank that the stake is worth £2bn is discoverable evidence against a ledger that says £200m. Every pledge above the reporting floor imports the lender's valuation into the audit trail.
6. **The honest-cost paragraph.** This architecture is the **hardest enforcement mode the design runs**: substance-over-form contests against a 100% incentive, fought contract by contract against the best-advised taxpayers — the concession already made for derivatives in [`../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md`](../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md) §4 applies to debt in full. The cautionary precedent is domestic: the UK's pre-owned-assets charge — the closest current-law analogue to annual benefit imputation — is widely regarded as under-enforced ([boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md), research gaps), a warning that imputation regimes decay unless the audit tier is funded and insulated ([administration.md](administration.md) §4). And proportionality binds: legitimate credit access for heirs — the graduate's mortgage, the small-business loan with a parental co-sign — must survive, which is what the floors, the materiality thresholds, and the connected-party scoping exist to protect.

## 6. Honest limits

- **Nothing here has operated at a 100% incentive.** FA 2013's debt restrictions, §7872 imputation, Item 403(b) disclosure and the ERS feed all run at incentives of 40% or less; the pinned rules are extrapolations of working machinery, not observations of it.
- **The derivatives frontier remains unresolved.** The receipt-of-economics rule is stated, not demonstrated; no jurisdiction has recharacterised monetisation structures against confiscatory rates, and even the SEC leaves most cash-settled derivatives outside beneficial ownership ([`../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md`](../08-capital-flight/composition-and-concealment.md) §4).
- **The DeFi gap is real.** Where no intermediary exists, the lender-feed architecture reports nothing; enforcement falls back to the fiat boundary and the accretions presumption, which is slower and coarser.
- **The netting rule spawns a debt-pricing game.** Once genuine debts net and contrived ones do not, the frontier moves to *pricing*: quasi-connected lenders, above-market interest to a friendly counterparty, fee structures that shuttle value. The official rate anchors imputation but not every term of every loan; this will be litigated, and the outcomes are not knowable in advance.
- **Enforcement cost is budgeted, not assumed away.** The lifestyle audits, probate debt verification, and lender-feed reconciliation land in the audit tier costed in [administration.md](administration.md) and [enforcement.md](enforcement.md) §2; if that budget is starved — the US estate-tax history — this file's answers degrade first, because they are the most labour-intensive.
