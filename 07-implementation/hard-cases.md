# Hard Cases

**Executive summary.** Transfer taxes are repealed over sympathetic anecdotes, not aggregate statistics — the grieving widow, the fourth-generation farm, the disabled son — so every hard case must have a specific, rehearsed answer before opponents choose the anecdote ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md), [`../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md`](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)). This file catalogues the genuinely hard cases and states the proposal's answer to each, distinguishing cases the design already solves, cases solved with a specific instrument, and cases where the honest answer is "yes, that person pays, and here is why that is right."

## 1. The working farm

The canonical attack case in every jurisdiction. The full design is in [`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §6: liability crystallises but payment defers up to 30 years at gilts + 1% while the heir actively farms; claw-back on sale or conversion; working-farm test excludes hobby and absentee arrangements. The rehearsed answer: **no working farm is ever sold to pay this tax within the farmer's working life** — the deferral outlasts a career. What the design refuses is the current APR outcome, where farmland functions as an inheritance-tax shelter that inflates land prices *against* working farmers [1]. The heir who wants to cash out rather than farm pays like any other heir — selling a farm you don't intend to work is not a hardship case.

## 2. The heir living in the family home

The 45-year-old who lived with and cared for a parent in a house now worth £900k. Design ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §3): a genuine occupier (primary residence 2 of the preceding 5 years) receives **deferral until sale or cessation of occupation**, interest-free during qualifying occupation. They stay in the home indefinitely; the tax is a registered charge collected from eventual proceeds. The contrast case — the heir with their own home who inherits the childhood house and wants to keep it *and* its full value — pays above their headroom, and the answer is honest: keeping a second house worth more than the lifetime threshold is exactly the transfer the tax exists to catch. The carer case has a sympathetic edge the regime should meet with the deferral, not with exemption, because exemption recreates the residence-relief distortions the OTS documented in the RNRB [2].

## 3. The disabled dependant

Fully solved by design and must be stated loudly, because opponents will lead with it: transfers into a regulated care trust for a qualifying disabled dependant are **exempt without limit** ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §2). The parents of a disabled child can secure their entire fortune to that child's lifetime care. The boundary polices pass-through to non-disabled remaindermen, not the care itself.

## 4. The heir who worked unpaid in the family firm for 20 years

The genuinely novel hard case. A daughter works two decades in the family business at below-market pay on the shared understanding that the firm will be hers; under a naïve receipts tax, the firm arrives as a pure windfall receipt and is taxed above her headroom — even though part of it is, economically, *deferred wages she already earned*. The proposal introduces a **sweat-equity credit**:

- Where a recipient can evidence sustained employment in the transferred business at demonstrably below-market compensation, the shortfall (market-rate band for the role, per the same benchmarks used against the *inflated*-salary dodge in [nepotism-and-networks.md](nepotism-and-networks.md) §1, minus actual pay, accumulated with interest) is treated as **consideration given**, reducing the gift element of the receipt.
- Evidence: payroll records against the arm's-length band; the claim is capped at the value of the business received and audited like any valuation claim ([valuation.md](valuation.md)).
- Legal grounding: this is not a concession but consistency — the tax base is *gratuitous* receipts, and value given for value is not gratuitous. Proprietary-estoppel case law (*Guest v Guest*, the farm-succession line of cases) already recognises that promised-inheritance labour creates real claims [3].
- Symmetry is the integrity check: the same market-rate benchmark that credits underpaid heirs charges overpaid ones. One instrument, two directions.

Residual difficulty: reconstructing twenty years of counterfactual wages is genuinely hard, and the credit will be litigated. Accepted — a bounded, evidenced valuation dispute is better than either taxing earned value or opening an unevidenced "she was promised it" exemption.

## 5. Sudden death with no planning

Under current UK IHT, sudden death is a tax catastrophe: the seven-year clock is unstarted, no reliefs are arranged, and the unlucky pay multiples of what the advised pay. **Under lifetime cumulation this asymmetry disappears** — the ledger's arithmetic is identical whether death was planned for thirty years or arrived unannounced ([gifts-and-timing.md](gifts-and-timing.md) §1). What sudden death still strains is machinery, not liability: missing records, unlocated assets, distressed families facing deadlines. Answers are administrative — the 6/12-month filing windows with hardship extensions, executor clearance, instalment options for illiquid receipts, and hardship deferral pending appeal ([administration.md](administration.md)). Planning-neutrality is a headline virtue to communicate: this is the first transfer-tax design under which dying suddenly costs your family nothing extra.

## 6. Blended families

Second marriages, stepchildren, half-siblings. The recipient-based design is structurally neutral in ways estate taxes are not: each recipient has their own threshold regardless of the family tree's shape, so a parent of a blended family can benefit many children without the aggregation penalties an estate-side tax creates, and stepchildren need no special "lineal descendant" status (the RNRB's descendant definitions illustrate the classification maze avoided [2]). The spousal relief follows legal marriage/civil partnership; serial spousal chains ("widow laundering" — marrying to pass wealth tax-free stepwise) are closed by construction rather than by policing marriages: spousal relief is a *deferral with a tracked charge* ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §1), so above-threshold corpus carries a deferred-charge tag down the chain and the *ultimate* non-spouse recipient's ledger is charged whenever wealth exits the marriage chain — however many remarriages intervene, and without the state ever inquiring into why anyone married. The dynastic escalations — two houses merging estates by alliance marriage, or relaying each other's tagged fortunes through reciprocal "widow swaps" — are priced out explicitly in [`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §1 ("The dynastic alliance, priced honestly." and "The widow swap, priced honestly."): merged descendant pools shrink joint headroom, and tagged corpus beyond the first marital hop meets the trust-equivalent periodic charge. What the tax deliberately does not do is referee fairness between first-family and second-family claimants — that is succession law's job (Inheritance (Provision for Family and Dependants) Act 1975 in the UK [4]), and the Register's estate transparency incidentally makes such civil claims better-informed.

## 7. The family holiday home shared by 12 cousins

The Cornish cottage or Adirondack cabin held informally across a sprawling family. Each cousin's fractional receipt is small — twelve-way division of even a £1.2m property is £100k each, likely within everyone's headroom — so **in most real instances the tax is zero** and the case is easier than it sounds; fractional division is the taxpayer's friend under a per-recipient design. Where a share does cross someone's threshold: fractional interests are valued with the capped discounts of [valuation.md](valuation.md) §5, the 10-year instalment option for illiquid personal property applies ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §7), and sustained exclusive occupation by one branch is an imputation matter under [boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md) §3 — shared holiday use never reaches the materiality floor. The genuine cost is co-ordination (twelve returns, one appraisal — the regime accepts a single shared appraisal filed against all twelve ledgers). The anti-abuse edge: *deliberate* fragmentation of a controlled asset among relatives to manufacture headroom and discounts meets the related-recipient aggregation rule in [valuation.md](valuation.md) §5.

## 8. Two procedural hard cases

**The minor heir.** A child orphaned at 10 inherits £2m. Liability above the threshold is unchanged — a windfall to a child is still a windfall — but machinery adapts: the estate settles the tax before distribution (executor retention, [administration.md](administration.md) §3), the child's below-threshold portion is held on a bare trust until majority, and the support exemption ([boundary-problem.md](boundary-problem.md) §2) means guardians' spending on the child's maintenance and education never touches the ledger. The orphan keeps the full £500k threshold plus the endowment at 25 — by construction better treatment than most heirs, which is the right answer to the worst anecdote.

**Deeds of variation and disclaimers.** Under current UK IHT, beneficiaries can rewrite a will within two years by deed of variation, redirecting inheritance as if the deceased had done it — a well-known planning tool [5]. Under a recipient-based ledger the device loses its magic and gains a fair use: a variation is simply a *fresh transfer from the original beneficiary to the new one*, posting to the new recipient's ledger at the varied amount and consuming the original beneficiary's de-minimis or headroom only if they had constructively received. The clean rule: disclaimers made before any benefit is taken route the receipt directly to the next taker (no double ledger entry); variations after benefit are onward gifts. Redirecting inheritance to spread it across low-headroom relatives thus works exactly once per genuine recipient — which is not avoidance, it is the per-recipient design operating as intended.

## The honest residue

Three patterns recur across the catalogue. First, most "hard cases" are hard under the *current* estate-side tax and easier here: recipient-based thresholds, planning-neutrality, and deferral instruments absorb the sympathetic anecdotes. Second, the instruments that remain are payment-side (deferral, instalments, state equity), never liability-side — the moment hardship is answered with exemption, the exemption becomes the avoidance vehicle, which is the whole history of BPR/APR [1]. Third, some people the public will initially read as hard cases are, on inspection, the target: the absentee second-home heir, the cash-out farm seller, the hobby-farm purchaser. Part of the communications task ([`../06-palatability/framing.md`](../06-palatability/framing.md), [`../14-communications/inoculation.md`](../14-communications/inoculation.md)) is drawing that line *before* opponents blur it.

Each answer above should ship as a one-page public explainer before the legislation is introduced — the anecdotes will be chosen by opponents, but the answers can be pre-positioned ([`../14-communications/inoculation.md`](../14-communications/inoculation.md)).

## Research gaps

- Sweat-equity credit: survey of proprietary-estoppel awards as a benchmark for claim magnitudes; whether a statutory formula beats case-by-case assessment.
- Frequency data for each case class (how many estates actually contain a shared holiday home crossing thresholds?) to prioritise guidance drafting.
- Comparative treatment of blended families under Ireland's CAT group thresholds — the closest live analogue.
