# The Boundary Problem: Receipt vs Support

**Executive summary.** The deepest line-drawing question in a lifetime-receipts tax is not trusts or offshore accounts but the family dinner table: when does ordinary parental support become a taxable capital receipt? This file proposes a four-part workable line — an annual de-minimis allowance, an age-based support exemption, market-rate imputation for sustained use of assets, and graduated disclosure thresholds — and argues that the irreducible fuzziness at the margin is no worse than boundaries existing tax law already polices every day.

## The problem stated honestly

A 100% marginal rate above the lifetime threshold means that, for a recipient with exhausted headroom, *everything* that counts as a receipt is confiscated at the margin. If "receipt" is drawn too widely, the tax criminalises Sunday lunch; too narrowly, and the wealthy simply relabel capital transfers as "support" — school fees at 40, a rent-free Mayfair flat, an interest-free "loan" never repaid. The boundary must be drawn somewhere, it will be arbitrary at the margin, and the design question is where the arbitrariness does least damage.

## The spectrum

| Case | Intuition | Treatment under this proposal |
|---|---|---|
| Meals, housing, care of a minor child | Obviously support | Exempt (age-based exemption, §2) |
| University fees and maintenance, age 18–25 | Support | Exempt within the education carve-out (§2) |
| A wedding costing £15,000 | Consumption gift | Within annual de-minimis for most families (§1); excess counts |
| A £60,000 house deposit at 28 | Capital transfer | Taxable receipt against lifetime threshold |
| Interest-free loan of £200,000 | Hidden transfer of the interest | Imputed benefit counts annually (§3); principal counts if forgiven |
| Loan quietly never repaid | Transfer | Deemed receipt on forgiveness or after statutory dormancy (§3) |
| Rent-free occupation of parent's second flat, 5 years | Sustained transfer of rental value | Market-rate imputation above grace period (§3) |
| Family holiday home used 3 weeks a year | Ordinary family life | Below imputation materiality floor (§3) |
| House sold to child at 40% below market | Transfer of the shortfall | Gift element is a receipt ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §1(vi)) |

## 1. Annual de-minimis gift allowance

Per [`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §1: **£3,000 / $18,000 per donor–recipient pair per year** passes free of both tax and ledger entry. This is the pressure-release valve — birthday money, Christmas, a modest wedding contribution, help with a rental deposit all pass without paperwork. Everything above it in a year is a ledger entry, though for most people a costless one (it merely consumes threshold headroom). The allowance is deliberately *not* cumulative or transferable across years: banking unused allowances would recreate the planning game the lifetime design exists to kill.

**Why per-pair, not per-recipient:** a per-recipient cap would let one parent's gift crowd out a grandparent's; a per-donor cap would invite gift-splitting through relatives. Per-pair is how the US annual exclusion (§2503(b)) already works and it has proven administrable for decades [1].

## 2. Age-based support exemption

Maintenance of a person the donor is legally or naturally responsible for is not a capital receipt. Specifically exempt, without limit and without ledger entry:

- All support (housing, food, care, clothing, ordinary travel) of a **minor child or a dependant** in the donor's household or paid for their benefit.
- **Education and reasonable maintenance to age 25**, including school and university fees, aligning the cut-off with the endowment age in [`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md). School fees are deliberately *not* taxed as receipts — the reasons are developed in [human-capital.md](human-capital.md).
- Support of a **disabled dependant of any age** through the regulated care-trust route ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §2).

The precedent is direct: UK IHT already exempts "dispositions for family maintenance" (IHTA 1984 s.11) and the US excludes direct payment of tuition and medical costs (§2503(e)) [2]. Both boundaries have operated for decades without collapsing into either absurdity.

**The cliff at 25.** After 25, routine subsidy of an adult child's lifestyle — paying their rent, clearing their credit card — falls into the de-minimis allowance and, above it, the ledger. This is a real behavioural line and it is meant to be: sustained subsidy of adult children *is* inheritance in instalments, and the age-25 cut is the same judgment the endowment design already makes about when adulthood's capital account opens.

## 3. Use of assets and soft loans: market-rate imputation

The relabelling frontier. Rules:

- **Sustained use of a donor's asset** (rent-free or below-market flat, standing use of a holiday home, exclusive use of a vehicle) is an annual deemed receipt of the market-rate shortfall, once use exceeds a grace period of **183 days/year for dwellings** and subject to a **materiality floor** (imputed benefit below the de-minimis allowance simply consumes it). Casual family hospitality never reaches the floor; living in the parental buy-to-let for a decade does. Precedent: the UK pre-owned assets income-tax charge (FA 2004 Sch 15) and benefit-in-kind imputation for employer housing both already price the use of assets annually [3].
- **Interest-free and soft loans:** the foregone interest at an official rate (analogue of the HMRC official rate / IRS applicable federal rate, as in US §7872 below-market-loan rules [4]) is an annual deemed receipt. The principal is untouched while the loan is genuine.
- **Loan forgiveness** is a receipt of the forgiven amount at that date ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §1(v)). A loan with no repayments and no enforcement for **10 years** is rebuttably presumed forgiven — the dormancy rule that stops "loans" being inheritance with a stapled IOU.
- **Below-market sales:** shortfall to market value is a receipt, valued under [valuation.md](valuation.md).

## 4. Disclosure thresholds

The ledger must not drown in noise. Three tiers:

| Tier | What | Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| Below de-minimis | Ordinary gifts | Nothing — no record, no return |
| Above de-minimis, recipient below 80% of threshold | Mid-size gifts | Ledger entry via a two-minute online declaration; no tax |
| Recipient above 80% of threshold, or any receipt over £50k/$75k | Material transfers | Full Receipts Return per [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §5 |

Third-party feeds (banks, Land Registry, Companies House — see [gifts-and-timing.md](gifts-and-timing.md)) catch the undeclared middle tier; the taxpayer's incentive to declare is that *undeclared* receipts discovered later carry penalties while declared ones below threshold cost nothing.

## 5. Why an imperfect line beats no line

Every objection to this boundary already applies to boundaries the tax system polices routinely: employment income vs gifts (tips, testimonials — *Moorhouse v Dooland* and its line of cases), business vs personal expenditure, capital vs income, benefits-in-kind valuation, the existing s.11 family-maintenance exemption [2][5]. None of these lines is philosophically clean; all of them function, because (a) the bulk of cases sit far from the margin, (b) the margin is policed by disclosure duties plus audit, and (c) case law accretes workable sub-rules over time. The boundary proposed here is *less* fuzzy than the income/gift boundary that common-law courts have managed for a century, because it is anchored in observable market rates and registered transactions.

The honest residual: determined families will consume the de-minimis allowance systematically, host adult children generously below the imputation floor, and time weddings lavishly. The leakage is bounded — it is consumption-shaped, annual-capped, and cannot move *capital* — and capital, not consumption, is what builds dynasties ([`../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md`](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md)). A family that converts its fortune into twenty years of nice dinners has, from the proposal's standpoint, complied.

## Research gaps

- Distributional modelling of where the de-minimis level should sit (£3k inherited from current UK IHT practice; may be too low for a regime doing this much work — to verify against gift-survey data).
- Behavioural evidence on imputation compliance from the UK pre-owned-assets charge, which is widely regarded as under-enforced.
- Interaction of the age-25 support cliff with rising ages of financial independence.
