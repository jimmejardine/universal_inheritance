# Astroturf, Front Groups, and the Wealth-Defence Industry

**Executive summary.** Organised opposition will not arrive under its own name: the funders with eight-figure stakes will operate through front coalitions with populist branding, opaque think tanks, professional lobbyists, and the trust-and-estates profession acting as a standing institutional interest. Mapping these channels in advance — and legislating for their transparency — is a precondition for a fair fight, and their very existence is evidence for the capture thesis in [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md).

## The channel map

| Channel | UK form | US form | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Front coalitions | Sector bodies (farming, family business) fronting for the largest estates | "Family Business Coalition," 60 Plus Association, NFIB/Farm Bureau alliances (per the repeal campaign — [attack-playbook.md](attack-playbook.md)) | Put sympathetic faces on dynastic interests |
| Opaque think tanks | Free-market institutes with undisclosed funders; several UK bodies have historically received the lowest transparency ratings from funding-disclosure monitors (to verify against current Who Funds You? ratings) [1] | 501(c)(3) research arms producing repeal-friendly studies | Launder interest into "independent research" |
| Dark money vehicles | Unincorporated associations and shell-company donations flagged by Transparency International UK [2] | 501(c)(4) "social welfare" groups — political spending without donor disclosure | Move money without attribution |
| Professional lobbying | Consultant lobbyists outside the narrow UK register's scope; direct peer and MP cultivation | K Street retainers; revolving-door hires from tax-writing committees | Shape drafting, insert carve-outs |
| Wealth-defence professionals | STEP, the private-client bar, family offices | Same, plus the estate-planning bar and trust-jurisdiction bodies | Standing commercial interest in the tax's complexity and defeat |

## The wealth-defence industry as an organised interest

The deepest and most durable opponent is not any billionaire but the profession that services them. Jeffrey Winters coined the term "wealth defence industry" for the lawyers, accountants, and advisers whose business model is minimising taxes on large fortunes [3]; Brooke Harrington's *Capital without Borders* documented the trust-and-estates profession from the inside, including the Society of Trust and Estate Practitioners (STEP) as its credentialing and lobbying body [4].

Three properties make this industry a distinctive threat:

- **It outlives any campaign.** Front groups dissolve after a vote; the profession lobbies every finance bill, every year, forever. Its preferred outcome is not necessarily repeal — a complex, porous tax generates more fees than no tax. This aligns it with the carve-out-stuffing strategy in [sabotage-after-passage.md](sabotage-after-passage.md).
- **It writes the technical record.** Consultation responses on valuation, trusts, and reliefs are dominated by practitioners whose clients are the taxed. Legislators experience this as neutral expertise. See [../07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md](../07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md).
- **It is respectable.** Unlike a Super PAC, STEP presents as a professional standards body — which it also genuinely is. Attacking it clumsily looks like attacking lawyers for lawyering; the correct move is transparency, not demonisation.

## Dark-money routes, UK and US

- **US 501(c)(4)s** may engage in substantial political activity without disclosing donors; combined with LLC pass-throughs, the original funder of an anti-tax advertising blitz can be practically untraceable. Post-*Citizens United* outside spending data are surveyed in [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md).
- **UK think-tank opacity.** The UK has no obligation on charities or think tanks to disclose funders; monitoring projects (openDemocracy's and Who Funds You?'s ratings) have repeatedly graded several influential free-market institutes at the lowest transparency tier (to verify current ratings) [1]. Expect these institutes to supply the intellectual air cover: reports on capital flight ([../08-capital-flight/evidence.md](../08-capital-flight/evidence.md)), "double taxation," and administrative infeasibility.
- **UK lobbying register gaps.** The statutory register covers only consultant lobbyists meeting narrow criteria, not in-house lobbyists — a gap Transparency International UK has long flagged [2].
- **Donation concentration.** Both parties' funding bases include exactly the individuals with the largest stakes in this policy failing — the structural conflict documented in [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md) and central to party-incentive analysis in [../12-political-adoption/party-incentives.md](../12-political-adoption/party-incentives.md).

## How to read an opposition report

Think-tank and coalition publications opposing the tax will look like research. A standing checklist for spokespeople and journalists:

- **Who funded it?** If undisclosed, say so in every response before engaging the content.
- **Which tax is being modelled?** Reports will frequently model an estate-based tax without threshold or endowment — a different policy from [../02-proposal/README.md](../02-proposal/README.md).
- **What elasticities are assumed?** Capital-flight and savings-response assumptions drive most headline results; compare against the evidence base in [../08-capital-flight/evidence.md](../08-capital-flight/evidence.md) and [../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md](../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md).
- **Are hard cases counted or narrated?** A report that leads with vignettes rather than distributional tables is a campaign document.
- **Is the counterfactual stated?** "Revenue lost to flight" claims routinely omit what the same wealth pays under the status quo (often near zero — [../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md](../01-problem/wealth-concentration.md)).

## What to expect, in sequence

1. **Pre-announcement:** think-tank reports establishing "the evidence" against; polling commissioned with estate-framed questions.
2. **Announcement week:** a named coalition launches ("Families Against Confiscation" or similar), fronted by farmers and shopkeepers, funded invisibly.
3. **Committee stage:** technical consultation responses seeding carve-outs and valuation soft spots — the salami move in [attack-playbook.md](attack-playbook.md).
4. **Post-passage:** permanent professional lobbying for erosion — see [sabotage-after-passage.md](sabotage-after-passage.md) — plus litigation funding, see [legal-challenges.md](legal-challenges.md).

## Mitigations

1. **Legislate transparency alongside the tax.** Package lobbying-register reform (extend to in-house lobbyists), think-tank funder disclosure above a de-minimis threshold, and beneficial-ownership disclosure for campaign donors into the same legislative programme. Opponents then have to fight transparency in public in order to fight the tax in private.
2. **Fund the disclosure journalism.** Investigative capacity (openDemocracy, Bureau of Investigative Journalism, OpenSecrets-style databases) is the effective enforcement arm of transparency norms. Movement funding strategy in [../13-movement/funding.md](../13-movement/funding.md) should treat this as core infrastructure, not an adjunct.
3. **Name the industry, respectfully and relentlessly.** "The wealth-defence industry" should be standard vocabulary in all communications: it reframes technical objections from STEP and the private-client bar as submissions from an interested party, which they are. Every citation of an opposing report should carry its funding status ("funder undisclosed"). See [../14-communications/naming-and-language.md](../14-communications/naming-and-language.md).
4. **Prebunk the front-group launch.** Publish, before announcement, a plain-language explainer: "Within a month, a group with a name like X will appear, claiming to speak for family farms. Ask who funds it." Inoculation mechanics in [../14-communications/inoculation.md](../14-communications/inoculation.md); the 2001 US precedent gives the script — [attack-playbook.md](attack-playbook.md).
5. **Split the profession.** A recipient-based tax with a Central Register of Receipts ([../02-proposal/mechanism.md](../02-proposal/mechanism.md)) still needs valuers, executors, and compliance advisers. Engage the professional bodies on administrability early; practitioners who help design the regime acquire a stake in its workability. The German experience of practitioner-driven carve-out abuse ([../05-history/abolitions.md](../05-history/abolitions.md)) shows what happens when the profession's only role is adversarial.
6. **Symmetry audits.** Commission an independent standing tally of declared spending for and against the policy. The expected asymmetry — opponents outspending supporters by an order of magnitude (to verify once observable) — is itself a communicable fact that validates [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md): *the money fighting this tax is the money the tax is about.*
