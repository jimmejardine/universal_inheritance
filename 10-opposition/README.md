# 10 — Opposition: Who Will Fight This, How, and What We Do About It

**Executive summary.** This section maps the people and institutions that will oppose the proposal — dynastic wealth, its professional service industry, its media assets, and its political clients — together with their documented tactics and the counter-strategy for each. The premise throughout: opposition will be better funded, earlier organised, and more persistent than the campaign for the tax, and this asymmetry is not incidental — it is the political-capture problem of [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md) manifesting in real time, and should be argued as such.

## Scope note

This section is about *opponents and tactics* — who attacks, through what channel, and how each channel is neutralised. Rebuttals to the *arguments* themselves live in [../09-objections/](../09-objections/); the steelmanned substantive cases live in [../04-case-against/](../04-case-against/). Where an attack has an argumentative payload (e.g. "farms will be destroyed"), the files here address the delivery system and link to the rebuttal.

Severity reflects capacity to defeat or gut the policy, not volume of noise. The two critical-rated classes at top and bottom of the table bracket the campaign's life: the framing war decides whether the tax passes; the attrition war decides whether it survives.

Note also what is *absent* from the matrix: mass public opposition. Polling hostility to inheritance taxation ([../06-palatability/polling.md](../06-palatability/polling.md)) is real but manufactured and soft — it moves substantially under recipient-based and endowment framings. The threat model here treats the public as contested terrain, not as an attacker.

## The asymmetry is the argument

Be honest about the balance of forces. The opposition will command more money, more professional talent, more press, and more parliamentary access than the campaign — reliably and throughout. The US "death tax" repeal succeeded on precisely this asymmetry: concentrated eight-figure stakes against diffuse, unorganised beneficiaries ([attack-playbook.md](attack-playbook.md)). But this is a double-edged fact, and the campaign should wield the other edge: *the observable spending gap is a live demonstration of the thesis.* If a tax on eight-figure inheritances summons the best-funded lobbying operation of the decade, that is [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md) performed in public — dynastic wealth converting itself into political power to protect dynastic wealth. Every dark-money advert, every proprietor's front page, every funded lawsuit should be answered on substance *and* logged as evidence. The symmetry-audit proposal in [astroturf-and-lobbying.md](astroturf-and-lobbying.md) operationalises this.

## Recurring counter-strategies

Five mitigations recur across every file and constitute the section's core doctrine:

1. **Prebunk everything.** The playbook is public ([attack-playbook.md](attack-playbook.md)); publish it before opponents run it — [../14-communications/inoculation.md](../14-communications/inoculation.md).
2. **Design out the hard cases before launch.** No true victims for the vignette machine — [../02-proposal/carveouts.md](../02-proposal/carveouts.md).
3. **The endowment is the shield.** A named universal entitlement gives the tax the organised defender constituency every predecessor lacked — [../02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md).
4. **Transparency as flanking attack.** Funding disclosure, lobbying registers, ownership declarations — opponents must oppose openness in public to fight the tax in private.
5. **Institutions over individuals.** Statutory indexation, independent administration, and organisational (not personal) campaign fronting reduce every single point of failure.

## Mitigations

At section level, three commitments follow from the threat matrix:

1. **Sequence defence into design.** Every mitigation that depends on statute (indexation, hypothecation, relief hygiene, transparency measures) must be in the *first* bill, not promised for later; the window of maximum political capital is at passage — see [../15-transition/sequencing.md](../15-transition/sequencing.md).
2. **Build the permanent counter-organisation.** Rapid response, validator networks, harassment protocols, and post-passage monitoring are one standing capability, funded for decades — [../13-movement/organisational-forms.md](../13-movement/organisational-forms.md), [../13-movement/theory-of-change.md](../13-movement/theory-of-change.md).
3. **Convert every attack into evidence.** Maintain the public ledger of who is spending what against the policy, and cite it as the capture thesis in action — [../01-problem/political-capture.md](../01-problem/political-capture.md).

A final calibration note. Nothing in this section assumes bad faith in every opponent: many farmers, family-business owners, and even estate practitioners will oppose the proposal sincerely, and their concerns are engaged on the merits in [../04-case-against/](../04-case-against/) and [../09-objections/](../09-objections/). This section exists because the historical record — above all the US repeal campaign — shows that sincere concern is systematically *organised, funded, and amplified* by a much smaller group whose interest is not sincere concern but dynastic preservation. Distinguishing the two, publicly and consistently, is itself a counter-strategy.
