# Inbound Wealth: Arrivals, the Entry Ledger, and the "They'll Buy Up Everything" Objection

**Executive summary.** The flight literature asks who leaves; this file asks who arrives, and closes the accounting gap for them: a single worldwide lifetime ledger that never resets at the border, a Japan-style on-ramp before worldwide receipts bite, and ordinary credit relief for foreign tax. Quantitatively, the skilled-migrant inheritance-forfeiture objection is small: the £500k allowance sits at roughly the *90th percentile* of what native-born Britons inherit, and origin-country base rates of bequeathable wealth are 15–100× lower still, so the exposed cohort is low single-digit percent — a rounding error for most origins, concentrated in the urban-Chinese propertied class. On the political question — should a society *want* foreigners who have already inherited billions? — the design largely inverts the folk objection: an arriving dynast's wealth becomes a one-generation harvest, and for the skilled arrival's *children* the regime is arguably the best offer any destination makes: high-mobility second generations compete on human capital in the society that has just raised its relative price, with a universal endowment on the same residence terms as natives.

## Headline findings

- **One ledger, no border reset.** The lifetime receipts ledger (£500k/$750k, [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md)) is worldwide and continuous: pre-arrival receipts are declared at entry and consume headroom but are never themselves taxed. This mirrors the exit-side rule that tail years don't reset the ledger ([exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md)) — crossing the border, in either direction, neither taxes the past nor launders it.
- **A residence on-ramp before worldwide charge.** Japan — the only major regime taxing residents' worldwide receipts, as this proposal does — learned by trial that immediate worldwide scope repels skilled migrants; its "temporary foreigner" rule (worldwide charge only after >10 of the past 15 years' residence [1][2]) is the direct precedent for a symmetric design: 10 years in before worldwide receipts bite, 10 years of tail after leaving.
- **The exposed skilled-migrant cohort is small.** The allowance approximates the *native* P90 of per-heir parental wealth (top 10% of 1980s-born Britons: ≥£530k–£600k; median ~£136k–£180k [3][4]); only 4.62% of UK deaths paid any IHT in 2022–23 [5] and ~2% of US at-death transfers reach $1m [6]; millionaire base rates in sending countries run from ~0.5–0.6% of adults (China) to ~0.09% (India) against ~8–9% in the US [7]. Verified synthesis: low single-digit exposure overall, near-zero for Indian, Nigerian and most EU origins.
- **The flypaper thesis.** Inbound inherited wealth is not a leak in the regime but its intake valve: the arriving heir's own children (if resident) are in full charge, and every domestic asset the fortune buys enters the situs net permanently. A dynasty that immigrates is a dynasty that ends here, funding citizen endowments per the arithmetic of [`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md).
- **The second-generation offer.** Children of immigrants out-perform natives in upward mobility across nearly every sending country (US evidence over two centuries [10] — not re-verified here, flag retained), while wealth persistence runs through bequests, not education: Swedish registers attribute ≥50% of the parent–child wealth correlation to bequests and gifts versus ~25% to education and earnings combined [11]. A regime that blocks dynastic transmission and adds a universal endowment is structurally tilted *toward* the archetypal skilled-migrant family, whose asset is human capital.
- **The honest limits.** The flypaper binds at *transfer*: the holding decades (housing pressure, political money) are engaged by adjacent instruments ([`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md), [`../01-problem/political-capture.md`](../01-problem/political-capture.md)); a dynast who leaves before dying departs with the never-charged pre-arrival corpus; and the "your children rise on merit, not on your money" offer must be made honestly — it binds the migrant who succeeds here exactly as it binds natives.

## 1. The entry accounting: one ledger, no border reset

| Question | Rule | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Does the lifetime ledger reset on immigration? | **No.** Worldwide lifetime receipts are declared on becoming resident (an entry snapshot, analogous to rebasing declarations in existing expatriate regimes) and count against headroom | A fresh ledger would make "receive abroad, then move" a standing arbitrage; ledger continuity mirrors the return rule in [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md) |
| Are pre-arrival receipts taxed? | **Never.** They consume headroom only | No nexus existed when they were received; taxing them would be extraterritorial, retrospective in substance, and the single most effective way to deter every skilled migrant with a family business at home |
| When do worldwide receipts come into charge? | After a **residence on-ramp** (illustrative: 10 cumulative years of the past 15, mirroring the exit tail and the endowment residence test in [`../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md`](../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md) §5). Before that: UK-situs receipts only | The Japan precedent [1][2]; symmetry with the 10-year tail is easy to legislate and easy to defend |
| On-ramp anti-abuse | The on-ramp exempts only receipts from donors who were **never** UK-resident (or are beyond their own tail); receipts from a tailed emigrant remain in charge | Otherwise exit-and-gift-to-newly-arrived-heirs becomes the wash trade the return rule exists to kill |
| Foreign tax already paid | Ordinary **credit relief**, situs-state tax credited first, per the credit-ordering in [international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md) | Standard double-tax practice; the sparse estate-treaty network makes unilateral credit the workhorse |
| Endowment side | Separate test, already specified: entitlement at the later of 25 and ~10 years' residence ([`../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md`](../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md) §5) | The adult arrival never claims; their children raised here do — the offer that matters for skilled migration (§5) |

**Worked timeline — the arriving heir.** Year 0: a 35-year-old moves to the UK having inherited €40m abroad; entry declaration records it; ledger headroom fully consumed; nothing is taxed. Years 0–10: UK-situs receipts in charge; a further foreign inheritance from a never-resident parent is outside charge (on-ramp). Year 10+: worldwide receipts in charge above (exhausted) headroom. Year 25: she dies UK-resident; her estate — worldwide, including the surviving foreign portfolio — is in charge on the recipient axis: resident children taxed in full above their own headroom, non-resident heirs caught on everything UK-situs. Alternative year 20: she emigrates before dying — the exit charge reaches accrual that arose in-charge, and everything UK-situs stays in the net, but the never-charged pre-arrival corpus leaves with her. The flypaper holds what landed on it; no more.

## 2. Do we want inherited billions to immigrate? The flypaper thesis

The instinctive objection — *a society taxing inheritance at 100% should not attract people who have already inherited fortunes; they will just buy up everything* — gets the direction of the design backwards, in four steps:

1. **The wealth is taxed at the next transfer, and the next transfer happens here.** The arriving dynast is the last untaxed generation. Resident children are in full charge; even non-resident heirs are caught on every UK-situs asset. The regime cannot reach the world's existing dynastic wealth abroad, but every dynasty that immigrates volunteers its terminus.
2. **"Buying up everything" is how the situs trap closes.** Land, UK-registered shares, and domestic businesses bought with foreign inherited money become permanently chargeable regardless of anyone's later residence ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §5). The objection imagines the fortune escaping *into* assets; the dual nexus means domestic assets are where fortunes go to be caught.
3. **Meanwhile the money is spent and invested here.** Consumption, employment, VAT, capital formation — the standard fiscal case for wealthy in-migration — accrues during the holding decades, and the terminal charge accrues after them.
4. **The alternative is worse.** A rule *excluding* wealthy immigrants forfeits both the lifetime contribution and the terminal harvest, while advertising that the regime fears wealth rather than dynasty — conceding the frame that [retention-attraction.md](retention-attraction.md) exists to hold.

So stated as policy: the regime should be *indifferent-to-welcoming* toward inbound inherited wealth, hostile only to its onward transmission — the same stance it takes toward domestic fortunes.

## 3. The honest limits of the flypaper

Steelmanned, the objection is not about transfer at all, and the concession must be explicit:

- **The holding window is long and the tax is silent throughout it.** A dynast can arrive at 40, spend forty years exercising money-power — housing and trophy assets ([`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md)), media ownership, party finance ([`../01-problem/political-capture.md`](../01-problem/political-capture.md)) — and the transfer tax bites only at the end, if at all. These are *holding* externalities; the proposal's scope discipline ([`../07-implementation/human-capital.md`](../07-implementation/human-capital.md) makes the same move) is to name the adjacent instruments — non-resident property surcharges, party-finance law, media-plurality rules — without bundling them in.
- **Pre-death exit is a real hole.** The heir who leaves at 80 having realised nothing in-charge departs with the pre-arrival corpus intact. Bounded by the situs trap and by the empirical rarity of late-life emigration ([friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md)), but not zero.
- **Kleptocracy risk rhymes with the golden-visa record.** The UK's Tier 1 investor route was closed on 17 February 2022 on security grounds; the Home Office's January 2023 review of the 6,312 investors and adult dependants admitted between 2008 and 2015 found "a small minority" potentially at high risk of having obtained their wealth through corruption or other illicit financial activity, and/or of being engaged in serious and organised crime [12][13]; "Moscow-on-Thames" is the cautionary tale. The proposal must not be marketed as a wealth-attraction scheme; its AML posture rides on the beneficial-ownership and CRS stack in [international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md), and the entry snapshot (§1) is itself a disclosure event the golden-visa regimes never required.
- **Top-end skilled-migration friction exists but is now sized: small.** §4 quantifies it — roughly the top decile of even *native* families breach the per-recipient allowance, origin-country base rates are far lower, and skilled arrivals hold modest wealth. A residual few-percent cohort, concentrated among urban-Chinese-origin migrants, faces a real expected cost that the on-ramp defers but does not erase for career-length stays. The post-2025 UK residence-based IHT already runs the same worldwide logic at 40% [14], so the *marginal* deterrence versus current law is the rate, not the principle.

## 4. Sizing the exposed cohort

Adversarially verified benchmarks (each survived 3-0 refutation votes against primary sources; full audit trail in the research record):

| Benchmark | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Native per-heir parental wealth, England, 1980s-born | Median ~£136k (2017–18 prices) / ~£180k (2023 prices); top 25% ≥£300–360k; **top 10% ≥£530–600k** — the allowance ≈ native P90 | IFS [3][4] |
| Projected lifetime inheritance, 1980s-born top decile | "Almost £500,000" | IFS [3] |
| UK deaths incurring any IHT, 2022–23 | 4.62% (31,500 of 683,000); ~7% projected by 2032–33 | HMRC [5], IFS [4] |
| US transfers at death ≥$1m | ~2% of transfers (≈40% of dollars); >half of all transfers flow to the top wealth decile | Federal Reserve [6] |
| USD-millionaire share of adults, 2023 | US ~8–9%; China ~0.5–0.6%; **India ~0.09%** (868,671 of ~1bn adults); 56-market average 1.5% of adults holding 47.5% of wealth | UBS [7] |
| Arrival wealth of skilled (employment-visa) Indian/Chinese US immigrants | Median household net worth $30k / $10k at green-card grant vs ~$93k native median, despite strong parental-*education* selection (77%/53% of fathers with 12+ years' schooling) | Keister & Borelli [8] — 2003 cohort, venue caveat; treat as indicative |
| The exposed sub-cohort: urban China | Top-10% wealth share 40%→67% (1995→2015, near the US's 72%); >95% of housing privately held after privatisation | Piketty, Yang & Zucman [9] |

**Bottom line (a triangulated inference, not a measured statistic):** the share of skilled migrants with realistic expectancies above £500k *per recipient* is low single-digit percent — a rounding error for Indian, Nigerian and most EU origins, a genuine but bounded few percent concentrated among migrants from origin-country top deciles, above all urban-China property wealth. Three design features shrink it further: the allowance is per-recipient (siblings split estates), the spousal relief defers, and the on-ramp exempts the first decade entirely. Cutting the other way: the data are vintage (UK microdata 2014–18, US SCF to 2016; nominal wealth growth since raises exposure), lifetime *gifts* count against the allowance and are poorly measured everywhere, and education-selected migrants may be more wealth-selected today than the 2003 cohort was — the direct wealth-selectivity measurement does not exist and is flagged as a research gap.

**Deterrence economics.** The migration-elasticity literature reinforces the sizing: tax-induced mobility is real for foreigners but concentrated in a thin superstar tail — Denmark's preferential-scheme foreigner stock elasticity of ~1.6 was driven by >5× inflows in sports and entertainment against <2× elsewhere, and only top-1% inventors show elasticities above 3, with native/settled elasticities near zero across all studies [15]. A threshold tax that leaves ~95%+ of skilled migrants untouched deters, at most, the tail the regime can least retain anyway. Two honest caveats: that literature measures income tax on current earners, not inheritance expectations (extrapolation is directional, not quantitative), and foreigner elasticities exceeding domestic ones cuts both ways for a regime taxing long-term residents' worldwide receipts.

## 5. The second-generation offer: why the deal improves for the immigrant's children

The friction analysis above prices what the skilled arrival gives up. The other side of the ledger is what their *children* gain, and it is the stronger half of the recruitment case:

- **Immigrants' children are mobility outliers.** Across two centuries of US data, children of immigrants from nearly every sending country out-perform children of the native-born in upward income mobility [10]; UK evidence points the same way: second-generation ethnic minorities are more likely than the white majority to gain high qualifications, stay in education, and attend university — though the IFS notes this educational success does not yet translate into equal labour-market success [18]. The skilled-migrant family's engine is human capital.
- **What blocks their ascent is inherited capital, not talent.** Wealth persistence runs overwhelmingly through transfers: Swedish register data attribute at least 50% of the parent–child wealth correlation to bequests and gifts, against ~25% for education and earnings combined [11]; UK inherited wealth is rising from 9% of lifetime income for the 1960s-born to a projected 16% for the 1980s-born [3]. Every pound of dynastic transmission the regime blocks raises the relative return on what the immigrant family actually brings.
- **The endowment is claimable by their children on the same terms as natives'.** The ~10-year residence test ([`../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md`](../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md) §5) means the child who arrives at 8 or is born here draws the same capital floor at 25 as any native — a recruiting asset no competitor jurisdiction offers, and one that compounds with the nepotism-suppression effects in [`../07-implementation/nepotism-and-networks.md`](../07-implementation/nepotism-and-networks.md).
- **The recruitment framing writes itself, and honestly:** *"Bring your skills; your children will compete in the least dynastic market in the world — and start it with capital."* The typical skilled arrival (median net worth $10–30k [8]) gives up nothing binding and buys their children a flatter field; the pitch belongs in [`../14-communications/audience-messaging.md`](../14-communications/audience-messaging.md) and the political wedge in [`../12-political-adoption/wedge-dynamics.md`](../12-political-adoption/wedge-dynamics.md).
- **The honest limit:** no study yet tests *causally* whether second-generation mobility improves under lower dynastic concentration — the claim is a well-supported structural inference (high second-gen returns to human capital + transfer-dominated wealth persistence), not a measured treatment effect. And the offer is symmetric: the migrant who builds a fortune here faces the same terminus as every native founder. The regime sells a fair race, not a family exemption.

| "Haven for oligarchs — you tax our grandmothers and welcome sheikhs" | Left / populist | No preference exists: incomers face the identical transfer rules; pre-arrival wealth is untaxed for the same reason untransferred native wealth is — and it is disclosed at entry, which the status quo never achieves | §1, [`../14-communications/inoculation.md`](../14-communications/inoculation.md) |
| "Non-dom redux / forfait by the back door" | Tax-justice commentariat | The non-dom and forfait regimes gave resident foreigners a *better* deal than natives [14][16]; the on-ramp gives new arrivals time-limited protection only because natives had no pre-nexus life — publish the symmetry table before opponents publish the accusation | §1 |
| "They'll buy up our housing" | Populist, potent | True of holding, not transfer; the transfer tax converts domestic purchases into permanently trapped situs assets; housing-side instruments are adjacent policy, argued separately | §3, [`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md) |
| "Skilled migrants won't come — they'd forfeit family inheritances" | Business lobby, recruiters | Now sized: the allowance ≈ the *native* P90 [3][4]; origin-country base rates 15–100× lower [7]; exposed cohort low single-digit percent, mostly deferred a decade by the on-ramp; deterrence concentrated in a superstar tail [15]; and the second-generation offer (§5) runs the other way | §4–5 |
| "The exempt on-ramp is the new loophole" | Fiscal hawks | On-ramp applies only to never-resident donors' non-situs assets; tailed emigrants and returning residents are excluded; ledger continuity closes the wash trades | §1, [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md) |
| "You're importing the next generation of dynasts" | Egalitarian left | The imported generation is the *last* one: resident heirs are in full charge — importing a dynasty is importing its endpoint | §2 |

## 7. Design judgements

| Question | Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Ledger on entry | Worldwide continuity; entry snapshot; pre-arrival receipts consume headroom, never taxed | Kills receive-then-move arbitrage without extraterritorial reach |
| Worldwide-charge on-ramp | ~10 of past 15 years (parameter open) | Japan precedent [1][2]; symmetric with exit tail and endowment residence test — one number, three uses |
| Wealthy-immigrant posture | Neutral admission; no courting, no exclusion | Courting invites the golden-visa record [12]; exclusion concedes the anti-wealth frame |
| Skilled-migration posture | Lead with the second-generation offer, concede the sized tail | A quantified small cost plus a structural benefit beats denial; the pitch is honest and checkable (§4–5) |
| Holding externalities | Named, not bundled | A transfer-tax bill carrying housing and party-finance riders hands opponents [`../09-objections/slippery-slope.md`](../09-objections/slippery-slope.md) pre-written |

## Research gaps

- **Japan as natural experiment:** the rule is well documented [1][2], but no verified evidence was found on whether the pre-2017 worldwide regime measurably deterred or expelled skilled foreign residents — the single most on-point natural experiment for this design remains unquantified.
- **Wealth-selectivity (not education-selectivity) of post-2015 skilled emigrants** from India, China, Nigeria: what share of current H-1B/Skilled Worker families hold origin-country wealth above ~$750k? The 2003 NIS cohort [8] predates the Chinese/Indian wealth boom; no direct measurement exists.
- **Migrant-intent survey evidence** (OECD Indicators of Talent Attractiveness, industry relocation surveys) ranking tax considerations against career, wages, schools, rule of law — gathered but not verified in this pass.
- **Causal second-generation evidence:** does immigrant second-generation mobility demonstrably improve under lower dynastic concentration / stronger meritocratic access? Currently an inference from [10]+[11], not a measured effect.
- **Entry-snapshot administration:** valuation standards and privacy handling for declared pre-arrival wealth ([`../07-implementation/valuation.md`](../07-implementation/valuation.md)); the precedents are confirmed — Canada deems newcomers to have acquired their property at fair market value on becoming resident (ITA s.128.1(1)(b)) [19], and the UK's 2017 reform rebased personally held foreign assets of newly deemed-domiciled non-doms to their 5 April 2017 market value [20] — but the administrative design work remains.
