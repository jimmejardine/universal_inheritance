# Retention and Attraction: The Positive Case for Staying

**Executive summary.** Anti-flight policy is not only walls and tails: the UK and US retain the wealthy chiefly because they offer what no haven can — deep capital markets, talent pools, universities, culture, credible courts, and cities their children actually want to live in. The proposal's retention message is that it leaves the living entirely alone: "live as richly as you like; you just can't found a dynasty" — full consumption, honours, and a status-preserving philanthropic channel remain open, and can be deliberately engineered into a reputational covenant that makes compliant large estates a source of public standing rather than shame.

## Headline findings

- The wealthy are net *importers* to London and New York despite decades of higher taxes than the havens; the agglomeration goods they buy there are supply-constrained and non-replicable ([exit-options.md](exit-options.md)).
- The proposed tax is a tax on *transfer*, not on holding, earning, or spending: it binds only at the moment wealth attempts to become dynasty. Lifetime utility of the wealth-holder is essentially untouched — a sharper retention proposition than any wealth or income tax ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md)).
- The charitable exemption converts the blocked dynastic channel into an open status channel — the Carnegie pattern — making philanthropy the designed pressure-release valve (second-order effects in [`../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md`](../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md)).
- Status goods (honours, naming, boards) are state-influenced, cheap to supply, and jurisdiction-specific — an under-used retention instrument ([friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md) catalogues why they anchor).

## What the jurisdiction offers that havens cannot

| Retention asset | Why it binds wealth | Haven substitute? |
|---|---|---|
| Deep capital markets | Exit liquidity, IPO routes, private-equity ecosystems for the founder's next venture | Fragmentary (Switzerland partial; Dubai/Singapore growing but shallower) |
| Talent pools | The workforce that makes an operating business valuable | No — talent clusters in big open cities |
| Universities and schools | Children's education and networks; the elite's own institutional affiliations | No serious equivalent in any micro-haven |
| Culture and society | Theatre, sport, restaurants, friendship networks, the *scene* | Enclaves, not ecosystems |
| Rule of law and courts | English commercial law and Delaware/New York law are the world's contract defaults | Havens *borrow* UK/US law precisely because they lack their own depth |
| Healthcare at the frontier | Continuity and quality in the decades when it matters | Imported, discontinuous |
| Cities their children choose | The next generation's revealed preference is London/New York, not Monaco | Decisive: dynastic planning fails if the heirs won't live where the plan requires ([friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md)) |

This is why the empirical elasticities are low ([evidence.md](evidence.md)): the wealthy are not overpaying for sentiment; they are buying supply-constrained goods that only a handful of high-tax jurisdictions produce.

## The core message: no dynasty, everything else

The proposal taxes neither earning, nor holding, nor spending — only the attempted conversion of a fortune into an unearned position for specific individuals in the next generation. Everything the wealth-holder can do *for themselves* remains untouched:

- **Consumption:** unlimited, untaxed by this proposal. The yacht, the houses, the foundation-funded chair — all available.
- **Spousal transfers:** exempt ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md)).
- **Per-recipient thresholds:** each child can still receive £500k/$750k tax-free — several times median lifetime savings; wide distribution across many recipients is legal and encouraged by the design.
- **The business:** can be run, grown, and sold freely during life; succession-of-control (as distinct from succession-of-wealth) has the deferral/equity machinery of [`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md).
- **Philanthropy:** fully exempt, with anti-cycling rules ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md)).

The behavioural claim, to be tested against the bequest-motive literature in [`../04-case-against/bequest-motive.md`](../04-case-against/bequest-motive.md) and [`../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md`](../16-second-order/savings-consumption.md), is that a transfer-only tax gives the *living* wealth-holder less reason to emigrate than the wealth taxes that actually produced the Scandinavian exits: those taxed the owner annually, while alive, on assets they were still using. This proposal sends the owner no bill at all.

## The charitable channel as status-preserving exit

Carnegie's "The Gospel of Wealth" (1889) — surveyed in [`../05-history/carnegie-1889.md`](../05-history/carnegie-1889.md) — supplies the template: the man who argued *for* confiscatory estate taxation simultaneously demonstrated the alternative disposition, converting a fortune into libraries, universities, and a name that has outlasted every untaxed dynasty of his era. The design intent is identical here. Blocked from founding a dynasty, the large estate has one fully exempt, fully honoured outlet: the charitable foundation, subject to the anti-cycling and payout rules that prevent it becoming a dynasty in trust ([`../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md`](../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md) analyses the resulting surge and its governance risks). For retention purposes the key property is that the philanthropic channel is *place-bound*: the gallery wing, the college, the hospital carry the donor's name in the society the donor actually lived in. Emigrating to Monaco buys the heirs money; staying buys the family a permanent public monument. That is a trade many will take, and history says so.

## The reputational covenant

A deliberate policy layer, cheap to supply and unexploited: make visible compliance with the regime a source of public honour.

| Instrument | Design | Precedent |
|---|---|---|
| Naming rights | Statutory naming registers for endowment-funded institutions financed by identified estates | US university and hospital practice; Carnegie libraries |
| Honours | A recognised honours track (UK) / presidential recognition (US) for major compliant estates and lifetime givers | UK honours already reward philanthropy, unsystematically |
| Public accounting | An annual "Founders of the Endowment" publication linking large taxed estates to the citizen endowments they funded | War-bond honour rolls; charitable donor walls |
| Civic legacy instruments | Option to direct a share of above-threshold value to named public projects (parks, wings, scholarships) in lieu of anonymous revenue | French *dation en paiement* (art in lieu of tax); UK Acceptance in Lieu |

The covenant reframes the top of the distribution from *targets* to *founders* — each large compliant estate is publicly credited with the ~thousand citizen endowments it funds (arithmetic to verify against [`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md)). This inverts the political economy that killed prior regimes, where the only visible protagonists were victims ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)); the communications development of this frame belongs to [`../14-communications/narratives.md`](../14-communications/narratives.md) and the coalition logic to [`../06-palatability/framing.md`](../06-palatability/framing.md).

## Attraction: the inbound side

A jurisdiction adopting the proposal remains attractive to *incoming* wealth-builders: no wealth tax, no extra income tax, full consumption freedom, and a £500k-per-recipient allowance that exceeds what most arriving entrepreneurs expect to transfer anyway. The regime is arguably *more* attractive than the status quo to the self-made — it taxes the competitor's heirs, not the builder — a wedge developed in [`../12-political-adoption/wedge-dynamics.md`](../12-political-adoption/wedge-dynamics.md). The formation-location margin (founders choosing where to build from day one) is the genuine long-run exposure, and it is governed by the agglomeration table above, not by death-tax rates fifty years out ([game-theory.md](game-theory.md)).

## The graceful-exit principle

Retention policy should not shade into vindictiveness. Those who genuinely wish to leave should be able to do so on published, priced, dignified terms: the exit charge is assessed, collection is deferred to realisation, and the door back is open (with ledger continuity — [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md)). Three reasons. *Practically*, punitive exit regimes invite the legal challenges mapped in [`../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md`](../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md) and hand the opposition its martyrs. *Politically*, the regime's legitimacy rests on the claim that it constrains dynasty, not persons; a Berlin-Wall framing would concede the liberty objection of [`../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md`](../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md). *Strategically*, a calm, priced exit converts each departure from a scandal into a transaction — the abolition-era pattern of national-humiliation headlines ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)) fed on the drama of escape, and a regime that shrugs and cashes the exit charge starves that narrative.

## Measuring retention success

Indicators the regime should publish annually, so the flight debate runs on data rather than anecdote:

- Net migration of individuals above the threshold-relevant wealth bands (arrivals vs departures), against a pre-reform baseline.
- Exit-charge receipts and deferred-collection balances.
- Share of above-threshold estates electing the charitable and civic-legacy channels.
- New-business formation by founders in top wealth deciles (the formation-location margin).
- The counterpart erosion metrics committed to in [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md) §"How future policy can rescue this."

## Research gaps

- Survey/experimental evidence on whether honours and naming meaningfully substitute for bequest utility among HNWIs (the "status vs transfer" elasticity — largely unstudied).
- Estimate the charitable-giving response to a 100% marginal rate from US estate-tax charitable-deduction literature (elasticities to verify).
- Case study: Acceptance in Lieu and dation en paiement uptake as proof-of-concept for civic legacy instruments.
