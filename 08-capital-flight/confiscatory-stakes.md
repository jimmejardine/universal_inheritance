# Confiscatory Stakes: The Billionaire's Escape Decision, Taken Seriously

**Executive summary.** Every other file in this section reasons from measured elasticities; this one starts from the author's challenge that those elasticities cannot govern here, because a 100% rate above £500k is not a tax rise to be weighed against moving costs — it is the extinction of dynastic transmission, and for a £1bn family the compliant outcome is roughly £1.5m to the children against £750m+ for a successfully structured escape. At those odds this chapter assumes, as a planning posture, that **most billionaire-tier families attempt structured escape**, and asks the only question left: what does attempting achieve? The answer assembled below — from the escape-asset inventory, the absorption constraint on where escaped wealth can actually sit, and the recipient-axis permanence of the charge — is that full escape is achievable but costs a large fraction of the fortune, requires a dynasty-wide, decade-plus coordination project, parks the residue inside jurisdictions the tax coalition already reaches or in jurisdictions whose courts cannot be trusted with it, and forfeits the fortune's domestic life forever. The fiscal exposure, propagated honestly through the arithmetic, is real but bounded: because the mass of the taxable flow sits in the £500k–£10m band that cannot exile, even 70% top-tier escape removes single-digit £bn/yr from the mature billionaire-tier base — while the pessimistic compound of top-tier flight with high dispersal genuinely breaks fiscal self-sufficiency, and is stated as such.

## Headline findings

- **On money alone, the billionaire stays only if he is irrational — so assume he goes.** The worked model in §1 puts compliant dynastic transmission for a £1bn/3-heir family at ~£1.5m core plus single-digit £m of dispersal headroom, versus ~£650–800m net for full structured escape. No plausible attachment premium closes a ~100:1 gap. This chapter's planning assumption — labelled as such — is near-universal *attempted* escape at the billionaire tier, and the design case rests on what attempts deliver, not on hoping they are not made.
- **Escape means selling Britain, taking paper, and removing the people.** UK land, UK-registered shares, entity-wrapped UK assets, and UK operating businesses are situs-locked (§2); the only exit for their value is conversion — sale at market price, paying CGT at 24% (verified 2026 rates [1][2]) — after which the productive assets remain in Britain, repriced, under owners who paid full value. What leaves is a claim on foreign assets plus a family.
- **The escaped paper mostly lands inside the coalition's reach.** The US plus Europe hold ≈57% of global personal wealth (repo-verified, [game-theory.md](game-theory.md) [6]); US equity markets alone are nearly half of global market capitalisation [5]; China is closed by capital controls [6]; and — the under-appreciated fact — the US already levies estate tax at rates up to 40% on **non-resident aliens'** US-situs assets, including US-registered shares, above a **$60,000** exemption [3][4]. Situs follows the asset, not the account. The honest counterweight: foreign blocker corporations currently defeat that charge in practice [4], an unresolved race examined in §2b.
- **The tax's real lock is recipient-axis permanence: the line can never come home rich.** Under the §2801-analogue rule ([exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md)), receipts from the exiled line by any UK-resident descendant are charged without time limit — exile buys freedom for the branches that stay out, forever, and only for them (§4).
- **Fiscal exposure is bounded by composition, not by elasticity optimism.** Scenario analysis (§3, all figures labelled illustrative) puts billionaire-tier transmission at ~£3–6bn/yr of today's ~£31–42bn above-threshold gross; even 70% full-escape at that tier removes low-single-digit £bn/yr now and single-digit £bn/yr from the mature base. The pessimistic compound — 70% escape across both the billionaire and £100m tiers, on top of pessimistic dispersal — is also shown, and it means permanent co-funding of the endowment.
- **Britain has run the confiscatory-rate experiment once, with split results** (§6): the 83%/98% income-tax era produced a famous exile wave (the Rolling Stones 1971, Bowie 1976, Caine late-1970s — all verified [7][8][9][10]) drawn almost entirely from the portable-income superstar cohort; the landed and industrial dynasties under 80% estate duty overwhelmingly stayed and avoided domestically, because their wealth could not board a plane ([`../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md`](../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md)).
- **Two design gaps surfaced by this analysis are now fixed** (§5): the returning-heir look-back is pinned at 10 years in [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md), and the governing principle — **the unit of escape is the dynasty, not the donor** — is stated in [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §3 and cross-referenced across the section.

## 1. The billionaire's arithmetic, without flinching

The literature-based files ([evidence.md](evidence.md), [friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md)) argue that most wealthy people are anchored. At the billionaire tier, anchors must be priced against the largest financial incentive the tax system has ever created, and the pricing must be done honestly. Take a stylised family: £1bn fortune, founder aged 60, three adult children.

**STAY (full compliance).** What the dynasty transmits and retains:

| Channel | Value | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Core: 3 children × £500k headroom | £1.5m | [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) |
| Dispersal headroom: children's spouses, grandchildren, wider family (illustrative: 10–20 further recipients) | ~£5–10m | Per-recipient ledgers; the intended behaviour ([`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §2.2) |
| Surviving spouse | Unlimited, deferred to second death | Spousal exemption ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md) §1) |
| Lifetime family consumption — houses lived in, education, travel, the whole texture of a rich upbringing | Unlimited | Consumption is not a receipt; only transfers cumulate |
| Charity and a named family foundation, with governance roles and the family name on it | Unlimited (£990m+ if chosen) | Charitable exemption; control and prestige survive ([`../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md`](../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md)) |
| The founder's own life | Untouched | No tax in life on the fortune; "the owner never gets a bill" ([retention-attraction.md](retention-attraction.md)) |

**GO (full structured escape, fully compliant with the rules as designed).** The requirements, from [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md) and [`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §4–5:

1. The founder (and spouse) emigrate **at least 10 years before death** — at 60, a bet on the actuarial tables with the family's £750m as the stake.
2. **Every intended recipient** — children, their spouses if they are to receive, grandchildren — becomes and remains durably non-resident, each clearing their own tail before receipt, and no descendant who receives ever resumes UK residence within the look-back (§5a).
3. **Full divestment of UK situs**: the operating business sold (CGT at 24% on the gain — Business Asset Disposal Relief shelters only the first £1m of lifetime gains, at 18% from April 2026, a rounding error at this scale [1][2]; a liquidity/control discount on a private block sale, illustratively 10–30%; National Security and Investment Act screening on sensitive disposals — [`../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md`](../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md) [7]); land and property sold at 24% CGT; UK-registered securities liquidated or re-registered, visibly, under CRS reporting.
4. **Thirty years of destination risk**: the haven repricing cycle runs on roughly a decade scale in democracies ([poaching-and-haven-politics.md](poaching-and-haven-politics.md) §3.3), and the non-democratic havens carry the §2b rule-of-law discount.

**The prize, stated plainly.** On a founder fortune that is mostly accrued gain, conversion costs (CGT, discounts, transaction costs) plausibly consume £200–350m of the £1bn; the residue transmits intact to the exiled heirs. Net prize: **roughly £650–800m — call it £750m+ in favourable configurations — against ~£5–10m for staying.** That is not a margin an attachment discount closes. Grandchildren's schools, the founder's knighthood prospects, the family's boards and galas — the anchors of [friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md) are real and they are worth something, but they are not worth £740m, and it would be advocacy, not analysis, to pretend otherwise.

**The planning assumption this chapter adopts (labelled judgement):** at the billionaire tier, the design must assume that most families *attempt* structured escape, that they are advised by the best professionals money retains, and that the attempt begins on announcement day. The anchors argument survives for the £1m–£10m mass of the base, where the prize is small and the costs are the same; it is retired here for the top tier. What remains — the entire remainder of this file — is the question the folk model never asks: **what does attempting achieve?**

## 2. The escape-asset inventory

The attempt begins with a portfolio audit. What can a UK billionaire family actually own that is not nexused in?

| Asset class | Nexus status | Escape route | Conversion cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| UK land and buildings | **Situs-locked forever** — charged on gratuitous transfer whoever holds it, wherever they live | Sale only | 24% CGT on gains [1]; SDLT and transaction costs for the buyer; the asset stays in Britain |
| UK-registered company shares | **Situs-locked** — situs at the register | Sale, or re-domiciling the company (its own exit charges, shareholder votes, listing consequences) | 24% CGT [1]; re-domicile friction |
| Entity-wrapped UK assets (BVI holdco over Mayfair, foreign SPV over UK land or business) | **Looked through** — artificial chains re-sited to the underlying asset on substance tests; the UK has run exactly this look-through since 2017 (IHTA Sch A1) | None while the wrapper is artificial; genuine-substance foreign operating groups are the residual leak, conceded in [`../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md`](../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md) | Gifting the envelope is gifting the contents |
| UK operating business | **Locked until sold** — workforce, premises, PE all UK-situs | Sale (trade sale, IPO, PE) — the only conversion | 24% CGT above the £1m BADR band [1][2]; liquidity/control discount on private blocks; NSIA screening in 17 sectors; loss of the family's economic engine and identity |
| Foreign securities, foreign real estate, offshore cash | **Born portable — honestly stated** | Already out; escapes cleanly once every recipient is non-resident and all tails/look-backs have expired | None beyond the years of family exile the tails require |
| Crypto, art, gold, bearer assets | Portable; crypto sited at recipient residence by design ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §5) | Physical/self-custodied removal | CARF reporting and exchange-of-information for on-ramps; insurance, provenance, and audit cross-checks for art ([`../07-implementation/digital-and-novel-assets.md`](../07-implementation/digital-and-novel-assets.md)); concealment is evasion, not avoidance |

**The punchline, which is the chapter's central structural claim: escape = sell Britain, take paper, remove the people.** There is no configuration in which the Yorkshire estate, the London terraces, the family firm's factories, or the FTSE stake exit the charge while remaining themselves. They must be converted — sold at market price to buyers who pay full value and whose own eventual transfers are charged — and what the dynasty carries out is a portfolio of foreign claims plus its members. The tax cannot be escaped *in place*; it can only be escaped by liquidating the family's position in the country and physically relocating its bloodline. That is the transaction the design prices, and §4 prices it.

**Composition: how much of the top-tier base is in each row?** HMRC estate data show estates above £1m increasingly composed of securities rather than housing (repo-verified — [friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md) [5]), and at the billionaire tier the dominant asset is typically a controlling business stake — but no verified public decomposition of UK top-fortune composition by situs-mobility class exists (Sunday Times Rich List sector tables were not verified to citation standard in this pass). **Sizing the row-weights of this table at the £100m+ tiers is hereby named as a micro-simulation task** ([`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §5).

## 2b. The absorption constraint: there are not enough dodgy-jurisdiction assets

Suppose the conversion is done and the family holds £750m of paper. The author's own observation cuts in here, and it deserves to be developed at full strength, because it is the least-discussed structural fact in the flight debate: **escaped dynastic wealth has to be *held somewhere*, and the world's supply of safe, taxable-transfer-free asset domiciles is far smaller than the world's supply of resentment about tax.**

**(1) Safe-asset scarcity.** The US and Europe together hold ≈57% of global personal wealth (US 34.7%, Europe 22.3%, of USD 471tn at end-2024 — repo-verified, [game-theory.md](game-theory.md) [6]), and the concentration of *investable* assets is sharper still: US equity markets alone represent nearly half — ~49% — of the $126.7tn global equity market capitalisation (2024) [5], with Europe, Japan, and the other rule-of-law democracies making up most of the rest. China, the one non-aligned economy of comparable depth, is closed: its capital controls are a deliberate, persistent feature of its economic model, restricting both residents' outflows and foreigners' access, and no full liberalisation is in prospect [6]. What remains outside the coalition-plus-China set — Gulf property, microstate banking, crypto, gold, art — is a thin residual that cannot absorb dynastic wealth at the trillions scale without severe price, liquidity, and counterparty degradation. This is the asset-side twin of the haven-congestion argument ([game-theory.md](game-theory.md), small-coalition theorem; [exit-options.md](exit-options.md)): the *residences* ration by property prices and entry queues, and the *portfolios* ration by the sheer unavailability of deep markets that no floor coalition touches.

**(2) The rule-of-law paradox.** Property at the £750m scale is only secure where courts are independent, title is enforceable, and the state is credibly restrained — and the jurisdictions with those properties are precisely the ones that tax transfers, exchange information, and are candidates for the floor ([international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md)). The alternative is to hold the corpus under a strongman's protection, which trades a 100% tax on the *transmission slice* for expropriation, sanctions, and lawfare risk on the *entire corpus*: the Yukos prosecutions — ruled by an arbitral tribunal to be politically motivated expropriation, not bona fide tax collection (repo-verified — [`../10-opposition/extra-legal-playbook.md`](../10-opposition/extra-legal-playbook.md) [16]) — and the post-2022 freezing of Russian-linked wealth across every Western financial centre are the two live demonstrations. A dynasty fleeing a democratic transfer tax into autocratic asset protection has not de-risked its fortune; it has swapped a certain, rule-bound, one-generation charge for an uncapped, discretionary, any-day one.

**(3) Situs follows the asset, not the account — the killer fact.** The deepest error in the folk model is the belief that moving the *family* and the *brokerage account* moves the *wealth*. It does not, and the United States already proves it at scale: a non-resident alien with no US connection beyond owning US assets is subject to **US federal estate tax on those US-situs assets — explicitly including stock of US corporations, "even if the nonresident held the certificates abroad" — above an exemption of just $60,000**, at graduated rates reaching a maximum of 40% [3][4]. An exiled British family in Dubai holding the world's default portfolio — S&P 500 stocks — is holding assets the US taxes at their death regardless of where anyone lives or banks. Generalised (as the floor's UTPR-analogue would generalise it — [international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md)), the point is structural: **escaped paper wealth is overwhelmingly claims registered inside the coalition — its equities, its funds, its government debt, its land — and the coalition does not need the dynasty's residence to reach the dynasty's assets** (labelled argument: this is the design's logic plus the US precedent, not an operating multilateral regime today).

**The honest counterweight, at full strength.** In current practice, the US NRA estate tax is routinely defeated by interposing a foreign "blocker" corporation: shares of a foreign holding company are foreign-situs, so the nonresident dies owning non-US paper, and practitioner guidance openly describes the structure ("a foreign corporation can be used as a vehicle to hold the real estate without triggering a potential U.S. estate tax liability"), while noting its income-tax inefficiencies such as branch profits tax [4]. So the killer fact is today a fact about *architecture*, not *collection*: the situs hook exists in law and is blunted by wrappers. Three things keep the counterweight from dissolving the argument. First, wrapper look-through is the demonstrated direction of travel — the UK itself pierced offshore envelopes over residential property in 2017 (IHTA Sch A1) and the beneficial-ownership register stack (CRS, CARF, registers of overseas entities) makes the piercing administrable ([`../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md`](../16-second-order/foreign-ownership.md); [international-coordination.md](international-coordination.md)). Second, this proposal's own design looks through artificial chains on substance tests from day one ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md) §5). Third, blockers are not free: they cost annual income-tax efficiency for a contingent transfer-tax benefit, and they concentrate the fortune behind exactly the kind of opaque structure that transparency regimes are serially dismantling. But the race is genuinely unresolved, and §7 says so.

## 3. Stakes-adjusted flight scenarios: propagating model-risk into the numbers

**The method, stated first.** [`../04-case-against/model-risk.md`](../04-case-against/model-risk.md) §1 concedes that behaviour at a 0/100 kink "may be discontinuous in the rate" — that elasticities estimated at 40–60% rates carry no warrant at 100%. This chapter propagates that concession instead of arguing with it: **for the billionaire and £100m tiers, it rejects literature-derived elasticities entirely and replaces extrapolation with decision-model-based planning assumptions.** The §1 decision model says attempted escape approaches universality at the top; the open empirical question is the *completion rate* — what fraction of attempting families actually execute the full §2 conversion, the dynasty-wide decade of exile, and the no-return discipline of §5a. No data measures this and none will before an adopter runs it, so the honest instrument is scenario, not estimate: **30% / 50% / 70% of billionaire-tier and £100m-tier families achieving full structured escape over the transition generation.**

**Sizing the exposed tiers (every step labelled illustrative).** Zucman's assessment puts the UK billionaire class at ≈200 families each above £700m (repo-verified — [`../13-movement/closing-window.md`](../13-movement/closing-window.md) [1]); at an average of £1–1.5bn that is a stock of roughly £200–300bn. Translating stock to flow with a stated mortality assumption of ~2%/yr for ageing principals gives **~£3–6bn/yr of billionaire-tier transmission at the current stock** — an illustration, not a forecast (principal ages, spousal deferral, and inter-vivos timing all move it). The £100m–£700m tier is unmeasured; a placeholder of ~£6–9bn/yr (perhaps 1,000–2,000 families — **micro-sim task**, [`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §5) gives a combined top-tier flow of **~£9–15bn/yr against today's £31–42bn/yr above-threshold gross** — roughly a quarter to a third of the gross, in the hands of at most a few thousand families. By the mid-2040s (gross £70–92bn/yr), scale the tiers proportionately: billionaire ~£7–13bn/yr, combined ~£20–33bn/yr.

**What "full escape" removes.** Not the tier's whole flow: the situs slice stays charged until sold, conversion pays CGT into the same Treasury, and charitable diversion at the top is the highest of any band. Net of those recoveries, assume an escaped family removes ~60% of its transmission flow from all UK charge (illustrative).

**The scenario table** (net receipts recomputed from [`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §3 central case, with the undifferentiated 5–12% flight residual replaced by: tiered escape per scenario + a 3–5%-of-gross avoidance residual for the sub-£100m mass, which cannot exile but still leaks at the margins):

| Scenario: share of top-tier families achieving full structured escape | Revenue lost to top-tier escape, today (£bn/yr) | Revenue lost, mid-2040s (£bn/yr) | Net receipts, today (central otherwise) | Net receipts, mid-2040s (central otherwise) | Against the endowment cost (£35–45bn now; ~£35–50bn 2040s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **30%** | ~1.5–3 | ~3.5–6 | ~£11–15bn | ~£31–39bn | Indistinguishable from the existing central case: launch gap unchanged; maturity roughly at par |
| **50%** | ~2.5–4.5 | ~6–10 | ~£10–14bn | ~£27–35bn | Maturity funding slips to the bottom of the central band; the bridge lengthens ~2–4 years |
| **70%** | ~4–6.5 | ~8–14 | ~£9–13bn | ~£25–33bn | A persistent mature gap of ~£5–15bn/yr against the mid-cost line; endowment needs partial co-funding or a longer escalator |

*(All figures illustrative; billionaire-tier-only losses are the smaller half of each "lost" range — even 70% billionaire-tier escape alone removes ~£1.5–2.5bn/yr now and ~£3–5.5bn/yr from the mature base: single digits, because the flow's mass is the £500k–£10m band that cannot exile.)*

**The honest shape, both ways.** *For the design:* the tax's fiscal core survives even the worst top-tier scenario, because it never depended on billionaires staying — the £500k–£10m band supplies most of the base and its owners' assets and heirs are structurally immobile. *Against the design, without flinching:* the pessimistic compound — 70% top-tier escape **and** pessimistic dispersal/charity shares **and** slow early flows — lands mature net receipts near ~£15–20bn/yr against a ~£40bn cost: in that world the endowment is permanently co-funded from general taxation and the tax's fiscal promise has failed, even while its structural aim (no British dynasties) is being met. The policy must be defensible in that world, and [`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §3's pessimistic row already carries the same conclusion. The tiered residual is now recorded in arithmetic.md §2.3 (cross-linked edit), replacing the undifferentiated 5–12% with an explicit note that the residual is tiered per this file.

## 4. What escape costs the escaper — and what it delivers the policy

**The escaper's bill, itemised from §1–§2:**

1. **Conversion costs**: CGT at 24% on largely-gain fortunes, liquidity and control discounts on private blocks, NSIA friction — plausibly 20–35% of the fortune before it is portable. Paid to the UK, up front, certain — against a saving that is contingent on everything below.
2. **A decade-plus, dynasty-wide coordination project.** This is the named principle (§5b): **the unit of escape is the dynasty, not the donor.** One resident heir, one grandchild who marries in London, one death inside a tail, one branch that quietly prefers Britain — and that branch's receipts are fully charged. The founder is not making one decision; he is binding a family of independent adults for thirty years, several of whom did not build the fortune and one of whom will, statistically, defect.
3. **Destination risk on the poaching cycle.** The welcoming regime is a policy choice of the destination's current government; the record ([poaching-and-haven-politics.md](poaching-and-haven-politics.md)) is that democratic havens reprice or repeal on a roughly decade scale, and the non-democratic ones carry §2b's corpus risk. Thirty years is three repricing cycles.
4. **"The line can never come home rich."** The §2801-analogue recipient-axis rule — like its US model, **unlimited in time** ([exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md)) — is elevated here into a named consequence: any descendant of the exiled line, in any generation, who lives in Britain and receives from the exiled fortune is charged in full above headroom. The escape does not buy the family freedom *from* the tax; it buys freedom *conditional on perpetual exile of every enriched member*. The fortune can return only by being spent, given, or taxed. For a British family whose identity, language, and history are here, this is the term of the contract most likely to be underpriced at signing and most bitterly felt at generation three.
5. **The loss of the fortune's domestic life.** Status ecosystems are non-portable ([friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md)): the honours, the boards, the college and the wing with the family name, the political voice, the philanthropic seat at the national table. The exile keeps the money and forfeits what the money was for.

**What escape delivers the policy — the carefully-bounded partial-success argument.** State both halves, no spin. An exiled dynasty is a **fiscal loss**: its escaped flow funds no endowments, and §3 counts that honestly. It is also a **partial structural win**: the proposal's primary objective is structural — the dispersal of dynastic concentration — with revenue second ([`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §2.3; repo doctrine throughout). A family that has sold its UK land, businesses, and registered shares to arm's-length purchasers at market price, paid CGT on the way out, and physically removed itself has *removed its concentrated wealth-power from Britain*: its former assets are repriced under owners whose own transfers are in charge, and its capacity for domestic political capture, media ownership, and dynastic entrenchment has left with it. That is not the outcome the revenue model wants, but it is an outcome the political-economy model can absorb — with one standing exception: the **geopolitical steelman remains at full strength**. Where the departing fortune controls strategic capability or would have founded it, the loss is national power, not just revenue, and [`../04-case-against/capital-flight.md`](../04-case-against/capital-flight.md) §5 holds that account open; nothing in this file discounts it. And the partial-success argument must never be allowed to excuse revenue failure — §7 makes that a standing rule.

## 5. Two design gaps, fixed

**(a) The returning-heir look-back: pinned at 10 years (design decision).** [exit-tax-design.md](exit-tax-design.md)'s worked timeline previously invoked an undefined "look-back" for the non-resident heir who receives abroad and later returns. It is now specified, by edit to that file: **receipts received while non-resident are recomputed onto the recipient's lifetime ledger if UK residence resumes within 10 years of the receipt.** Rationale: symmetry with the 10-year exit tail and the 10-year inbound on-ramp ([inbound-wealth.md](inbound-wealth.md) §1) — one number, four uses, easy to legislate and defend; long enough that "receive offshore, wait, return" requires a genuine decade of exile per receipt rather than a gap year; short enough to survive proportionality review on the same footing as the tail. Beyond 10 years, the returning heir re-enters as any arrival does: the entry snapshot records pre-residence receipts against headroom without taxing them. The recompute-on-return rule and the §2801-analogue permanence operate on different axes: the look-back catches the *timing wash* (receive, then return); the recipient-axis rule catches *resident receipt from the exiled line* whenever it occurs, without limit.

**(b) The named principle: the unit of escape is the dynasty, not the donor.** Now stated in [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §3 and cross-referenced from [`../04-case-against/capital-flight.md`](../04-case-against/capital-flight.md) and [friction-and-anchors.md](friction-and-anchors.md). The one-paragraph form: a donor-based estate tax is escaped by one person moving once (the Kamprad pattern); a recipient-based, dual-nexus, tailed tax is escaped only when the donor, every recipient, and every asset have all cleared the charge and stay clear — so the escape decision is not an individual's but a whole bloodline's, sustained across decades and generations, and every analysis of flight in this repo prices the family system, not the founder.

## 6. Britain's own confiscatory-era natural experiment

Britain is the rare country that has already run confiscatory personal taxation on its richest citizens, and the record cuts both ways. It should be read with both edges showing.

**The exile wave was real.** In 1974 the top rate on earned income reached **83%**, and the 15% investment-income surcharge took the top rate on investment income to **98%** — the highest permanent rates since the war, unwound only from 1979 (top rate to 60%) with the surcharge abolished in 1985 [10]. The era's exiles are cultural landmarks, and each is verified: the **Rolling Stones** left for the south of France in spring 1971 — having, in their manager's accounting, already spent the money they owed in tax — with Keith Richards' rented villa Nellcôte at Villefranche-sur-Mer becoming the basement studio of *Exile on Main St.* [7][9]; **David Bowie** relocated to Blonay, Switzerland in 1976, deflecting the tax question with "in Switzerland, they leave me alone" [8][9]; **Michael Caine** moved to the US in the late 1970s to escape the Callaghan-era top rates and returned under Thatcher once they had fallen [9]. Confiscatory rates did produce a famous exile wave. Anyone deploying the low-elasticity literature against the flight objection must own this record; complacency is not available.

**The counter-observation is equally real.** Across the same decades, the landed aristocracy and the industrial dynasties faced **80% estate duty** (top rate from 1949; 85% marginal from 1969) — and they overwhelmingly *stayed*. Their response was domestic avoidance, not exile: gifts surviving the seven-year window, discretionary and offshore trusts (the Vestey settlement being the century's emblem), relief-harvesting on agricultural and business property — the full avoidance culture documented in [`../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md`](../05-history/uk-estate-duty.md). They stayed because their wealth was situs-locked land and mills that could not board a plane, and because their identity was territorial: the estate *was* the family.

**Reading the split (labelled interpretations).** *Interpretation one — against complacency:* confiscatory rates, sustained, generate exile at the top, complete with the era-defining imagery (a rock album literally named for it) that [`../10-opposition/media-narratives.md`](../10-opposition/media-narratives.md) warns any adopter will face. *Interpretation two — for the design:* the wave's composition is the tell. The 1970s exiles were portable-income superstars — young, internationally marketed, their earning power in their persons — precisely the high-elasticity cohort the modern literature flags ([evidence.md](evidence.md) §2), and they were fleeing an *annual* tax on *current* earnings that bit them personally every year. The landed and industrial dynasts, whose profile matches this proposal's target — old, illiquid, situs-anchored, the benefit of escape accruing to heirs rather than themselves — did not leave even at 80%; they lawyered. The design conclusion is the same one the rest of this section reaches from other directions: **situs holds what paper cannot, and the escape that matters is fought in the trust deed and the wrapper, not at the departure gate** — which is why this proposal's spine is gift integration, look-through, and recipient-axis permanence rather than border control. *Both interpretations are judgements from a two-edged record; neither is a measurement.*

## 7. Honest limits

- **No measured elasticity exists at confiscatory stakes, and none will until an adopter runs it.** §3's scenarios are decision-model-based planning assumptions, not estimates; the 30/50/70% completion rates are round numbers chosen to span ignorance. The first adopter is the experiment ([`../04-case-against/model-risk.md`](../04-case-against/model-risk.md)).
- **Tier-composition data is thin.** The billionaire-tier flow rests on one stock figure (Zucman's ~200 families) and an assumed mortality rate; the £100m-tier is a placeholder; the situs-mobility decomposition of top fortunes is unverified. All three are named micro-simulation inputs ([`../02-proposal/arithmetic.md`](../02-proposal/arithmetic.md) §5).
- **The partial-success argument must never excuse revenue failure.** "Flight is a structural win" is true only as a *partial* offset; a regime that lost its top-tier flow and its fiscal promise would have traded an endowment for an emigration statistic. The §3 pessimistic compound is the world in which advocates must still be able to defend the policy, and the defence there is structural-plus-co-funding, stated in advance, not discovered in office.
- **The announcement-era rush is the transition's problem, not this file's.** Everything above assumes the exit architecture (tails, exit charge, look-back) is in force before the rate; the window between announcement and commencement — the Norwegian error — is where a rush of §1 decisions would actually land, and it belongs to [`../15-transition/announcement-effects.md`](../15-transition/announcement-effects.md).
- **Blocker structures work today, and the look-through race is unresolved.** §2b's killer fact is presently defeated in US practice by foreign holding companies [4]; the argument that transparency and look-through will close the wrapper route is a direction-of-travel claim, not an accomplished fact, and if the race goes the other way, the absorption constraint weakens materially.
- **The 1970s analogy is imperfect in the design's favour and should not be overworked**: the era's exiles fled annual taxes on their own current income; this tax takes nothing from any living owner. But the same asymmetry means the 1970s record cannot *confirm* the design either — no historical episode taxes heirs at 100% with a tail, which returns to the first bullet.
