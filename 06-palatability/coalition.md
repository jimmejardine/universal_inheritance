# Coalition: Who Wins, Who Loses, Who Decides

**Executive summary.** Under the £500k-per-recipient / £50k-endowment design, the arithmetic winners are an overwhelming electoral majority — roughly everyone below the ~95th percentile of lifetime inheritance receipts — but arithmetic majorities do not vote their spreadsheet position; they vote their identification and their fears. This file profiles nine blocs, assigns each a strategy (mobilise, persuade, or neutralise), and identifies the two swing blocs on which passage actually turns.

## The stylised ledger

Material position under the proposal, before any framing (magnitudes from [`../02-proposal/thresholds.md`](../02-proposal/thresholds.md); all figures stylised, to verify by micro-simulation):

| Lifetime position | Approx. share of adults (UK, to verify) | Net effect of package |
|---|---|---|
| Inherits nothing | ~50% | Pure gain: +£50k endowment |
| Inherits below ~£50k (median receiver ~£11k) | ~30–35% | Strong gain: endowment exceeds inheritance |
| Inherits £50k–£500k | ~10–15% | Neutral-to-gain: inheritance untouched, plus endowment (and children's endowments) |
| Inherits above £500k | ~3–7% | Loses above the threshold; keeps £500k + endowment |
| Inherits multiples of £500k | ~1% | Principal loser; the policy's target |

The political problem is that the bottom four rows do not currently *know* they are in them ([polling.md](polling.md), Paradox 1), and the top row is organised, funded, and articulate ([`../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md`](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)).

## Bloc profiles

### 1. Priced-out young (roughly under-40 non-owners) — MOBILISE

- **Size/trend:** the largest growth bloc; UK owner-occupation among under-35s has roughly halved since the 1990s (to verify). Renting into the 40s is normalising.
- **Material interest:** unambiguous winners — the endowment is a deposit; most will inherit little and late (median inheritance arrives near age 60, to verify).
- **Values entry:** fairness of starting points; housing ([`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md)); "work doesn't pay, ownership does" ([`../01-problem/rentier-economy.md`](../01-problem/rentier-economy.md)).
- **Objections:** cynicism that it will ever happen; distrust of state delivery ([`../09-objections/practicality.md`](../09-objections/practicality.md)).
- **Messengers:** peers, personal-finance creators, not politicians ([`../13-movement/messengers.md`](../13-movement/messengers.md)).
- **Strategy:** mobilise — this is the base and the volunteer pool ([`../13-movement/recruitment.md`](../13-movement/recruitment.md)). The risk is turnout, not persuasion ([`../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md`](../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md)).

### 2. Older middle-class homeowners — PERSUADE (decisive swing bloc #1)

- **Size/trend:** the pivotal vote in UK and US elections; highest-turnout cohort; holds most housing wealth.
- **Material interest:** better than they believe. A £290k median home splits between two children at £145k each — 29% of each child's threshold. Even a £900k South-East home passing to two children sits fully inside the 0% band. Their grandchildren each collect £50k.
- **Values entry:** grandchildren's prospects; "we could buy a house at 25 on one salary — they can't."
- **Objections:** "it starts at £500k and ends at my bungalow" (slippery slope, [`../09-objections/slippery-slope.md`](../09-objections/slippery-slope.md)); indexation distrust — answered by the max(wage, house-price) rule in [`../02-proposal/thresholds.md`](../02-proposal/thresholds.md).
- **Messengers:** their own cohort — grandparents' groups, retired professionals; not the young base.
- **Strategy:** persuade, with the *grandchild dividend* as the lead message. This bloc's acquiescence, not enthusiasm, is the passage condition.

### 3. Small-business owners — NEUTRALISE→PERSUADE

- **Size/trend:** ~5.5m UK businesses, overwhelmingly micro (to verify); politically loud through federations (FSB, chambers).
- **Material interest:** mixed. Most businesses are worth under the threshold once split among heirs; the 30-year deferral at gilts+1% ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md)) means no forced sales. Owners *as founders* benefit from a labour market of endowed 25-year-olds who can take entrepreneurial risk.
- **Objections:** valuation fear, succession disruption ([`../09-objections/family.md`](../09-objections/family.md), [`../04-case-against/family-business.md`](../04-case-against/family-business.md)).
- **Messengers:** founders who built without inheriting; business-succession advisers.
- **Strategy:** neutralise the federations early with the deferral design; convert a visible minority of founder-patriots ("I want competitors' kids to earn it like I did").

### 4. Farmers — NEUTRALISE

- **Size/trend:** small (~100k UK farm holdings, to verify) but with symbolic power vastly exceeding numbers — proven again in 2024–25 ([case-studies.md](case-studies.md)).
- **Material interest:** land-rich, cash-poor; the archetypal hard case. Deferral plus optional state-equity route ([`../02-proposal/carveouts.md`](../02-proposal/carveouts.md)) is designed for exactly this profile; genuine working farms splitting among children mostly clear the threshold.
- **Objections:** forced land sales; distrust of Treasury promises; agri-land as identity, not asset.
- **Messengers:** tenant farmers and new entrants — the constituency currently *locked out* of land by inheritance-inflated prices ([`../07-implementation/hard-cases.md`](../07-implementation/hard-cases.md)).
- **Strategy:** neutralise. Never fight farmers on television. Publish the farm protocol before opponents write it for us ([`../14-communications/inoculation.md`](../14-communications/inoculation.md)).

### 5. Small-c conservatives — PERSUADE (decisive swing bloc #2)

- **Size/trend:** the moral centre of the right-of-centre vote; disproportionately older, provincial, high-turnout.
- **Material interest:** as bloc 2, mostly inside the 0% band.
- **Values entry:** proportionality and merit ([framing.md](framing.md) §5); anti-decadence — idle heirs offend them ([`../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md`](../03-case-for/heirs-burden.md)); the Carnegie/Roosevelt lineage shows this was once a *conservative* cause ([`../05-history/carnegie-1889.md`](../05-history/carnegie-1889.md), [`../05-history/roosevelt-1906.md`](../05-history/roosevelt-1906.md)).
- **Objections:** family autonomy, state overreach ([`../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md`](../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md), [`../09-objections/family.md`](../09-objections/family.md)).
- **Messengers:** right-coded voices only — see [`../12-political-adoption/right-parties.md`](../12-political-adoption/right-parties.md).
- **Strategy:** persuade a fraction, split the rest from active opposition. Without at least the acquiescence of this bloc, no UK majority exists.

### 6. Classical liberals / meritocrats — PERSUADE

- **Size/trend:** small numerically; large in commentary, economics, and policy elites.
- **Values entry:** equality of opportunity as the precondition of fair markets; Hayek's own discomfort with inherited advantage; the level-playing-field argument in [`../03-case-for/meritocratic-legitimacy.md`](../03-case-for/meritocratic-legitimacy.md).
- **Objections:** capital-flight and efficiency claims ([`../04-case-against/capital-flight.md`](../04-case-against/capital-flight.md), [`../09-objections/economic.md`](../09-objections/economic.md)) — answered on their own terms in [`../08-capital-flight/evidence.md`](../08-capital-flight/evidence.md) and [`../03-case-for/economic-efficiency.md`](../03-case-for/economic-efficiency.md).
- **Strategy:** persuade the intellectuals to *legitimise*, not to deliver votes. One prominent free-market convert is worth more than a march ([`../13-movement/messengers.md`](../13-movement/messengers.md)).

### 7. Progressives / left — MOBILISE (already aligned)

- **Material interest & values:** aligned on both; the base.
- **Risk:** over-ownership. If the policy codes as far-left, blocs 2 and 5 close. Discipline the base's language ([`../14-communications/naming-and-language.md`](../14-communications/naming-and-language.md)); route enthusiasm into organising ([`../13-movement/organisational-forms.md`](../13-movement/organisational-forms.md)).

### 8. Unions — MOBILISE

- **Size/trend:** declining density (~22% UK, to verify) but retains money, organisers, and conference influence.
- **Material interest:** members are overwhelmingly in the winner rows; endowed young workers have outside options, which raises wages.
- **Objections:** none structural; competing priorities. Endowment must not be framed as a substitute for wage bargaining or public services.
- **Strategy:** mobilise institutionally — affiliations, conference motions, funding ([`../13-movement/funding.md`](../13-movement/funding.md), [`../12-political-adoption/left-parties.md`](../12-political-adoption/left-parties.md)).

### 9. Faith communities — PERSUADE

- **Size/trend:** declining attendance but dense civic networks and moral authority with exactly the older swing blocs.
- **Values entry:** deep scriptural resources — Jubilee debt-and-land reset, "eye of the needle," stewardship over dynasty; charitable giving is fully exempt and the design *increases* charitable bequests ([`../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md`](../16-second-order/charitable-giving.md)).
- **Objections:** family sanctity; suspicion of state moral engineering.
- **Messengers:** clergy speaking to their own traditions ([`../13-movement/messengers.md`](../13-movement/messengers.md)).
- **Strategy:** persuade selectively; even neutrality from religious leaders deprives opponents of a moral chorus.

## Minimum-winning-coalition arithmetic

A UK general-election majority needs roughly 40–45% of the vote. Stylised composition (shares to verify against BES data):

- **Base (mobilise):** young non-owners + progressives + union households ≈ 25–30% of the electorate — necessary, insufficient, and turnout-fragile.
- **Bridge (persuade):** older middle homeowners and small-c conservatives overlap heavily; converting or neutralising even a third of this combined ~35–40% closes the gap.
- **Noise floor (neutralise):** farmers and small business decide the *media environment*, not the vote count. Every week spent on a farm story is a week the grandchild dividend goes unheard.

Hence the two decisive swing blocs are **older middle-class homeowners** and **small-c conservatives** — distinct in values entry (material-familial vs moral-proportional) but overlapping in demography, and both winnable *only* if the endowment is led, the threshold is believed, and the hard cases are pre-solved. Sequencing implications are drawn out in [`../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md`](../12-political-adoption/electoral-arithmetic.md) and [`../15-transition/sequencing.md`](../15-transition/sequencing.md); the bloc-message matrix is operationalised in [`../14-communications/audience-messaging.md`](../14-communications/audience-messaging.md).

All bloc sizes and shares are stylised from general knowledge and flagged **(to verify)** against ONS, British Election Study, FSB, Defra, and union-density statistics before manifesto use.
