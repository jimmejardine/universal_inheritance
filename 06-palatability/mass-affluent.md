# The £500k–£5m Family: Talking to the Mass Affluent

**Executive summary.** The loudest sceptics the movement's own members meet are not billionaires but peers — professional families holding £500k–£5m with children — and they deserve a precise answer, not a slogan. Run honestly, the arithmetic says: below roughly £500k *per intended recipient* nothing changes and the family gains (endowments, tax cuts); estates that spread across children, their partners, and grandchildren pass more to the family than under today's 40% IHT well into the millions; the genuine losers are estates of ~£2m+ stacked on one or two heirs — and that concentration, not the family's prosperity, is what the design deliberately prices. The perspective anchor throughout: £500k per recipient sits at approximately the 90th percentile of what native-born heirs of the 1980s cohort will *ever* receive.

## 1. Who this audience is

The top ~2–10% of the household wealth distribution: owners of a paid-off house in a good postcode, pensions, some investments, perhaps a business stake. Verified anchors (IFS, England, 1980s-born cohort): median per-heir parental wealth is ~£136k–£180k; the top 25% have ≥£300–360k per heir; only the **top 10%** have ≥£530–600k per heir [1][2]. So the £500k-per-recipient threshold does not graze this audience's midpoint — it *is* their upper boundary. Most people who feel addressed by this file will, when they divide their realistic estate by their number of children, find each share inside the 0% band. The first move in every conversation is to make them do that division ([framing.md](framing.md) §"Raise the threshold above the dream").

## 2. The ledger, run honestly

Illustrative couple estates, everything passing on second death; today's regime approximated as a couple allowance of ~£1m falling toward ~£650k for larger estates via the residence nil-rate-band taper (rates and tapers to verify [3]); proposal: £500k per recipient at 0%, 100% above, plus a £50k endowment to every child at 25 regardless.

| Estate | Recipients | Today: tax / family keeps | Proposal: tax / family keeps (incl. endowments) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| £800k | 2 children | £0 / £800k | £0 / £900k | **Better** |
| £1.5m | 3 children | ~£200k / £1.3m | £0 / £1.65m | **Better** |
| £1.5m | 2 children | ~£200k / £1.3m | £500k / £1.1m | Worse by ~£200k |
| £3m | 2 children | ~£940k / £2.06m | £2m / £1.1m | **Much worse** |
| £3m | 2 children + partners + 4 grandchildren (8 ledgers) | ~£940k / £2.06m | £0 / £3m + endowments | **Better** |
| £5m | 2 children only | ~£1.74m / £3.26m | £4m / £1.1m | **Much worse** |

Three honest readings. First, **the tax is on stacking, not on family prosperity**: the same £3m passes entirely untaxed when spread across the family's actual members, and is heavily taxed only when piled onto one or two heads — which is the design intent, stated plainly in [`../02-proposal/thresholds.md`](../02-proposal/thresholds.md) ("distributive estate planning is the intended response"). Second, **the widely-spread family beats today's regime** deep into this bracket, because today's 40% bites the *estate* while the proposal's 0% band multiplies with *recipients*. Third, **concentrated £2m–£5m estates pay genuinely more**, and no phrasing should hide it (§5).

## 3. Putting the bequest in perspective

For the peer who says "but I want to leave my kids real money":

- **£500k per child is P90.** Nine in ten heirs of their children's generation will never receive that much [1][2]. The allowance is not a token; it is more than triple the median family's *entire* per-heir wealth.
- **What it buys at 25–35**: an outright home in most of the UK, a debt-cleared education, seed capital for a business — several times median lifetime savings ([`../08-capital-flight/retention-attraction.md`](../08-capital-flight/retention-attraction.md)). Plus the £50k endowment on top, for every child and grandchild, forever.
- **Above £500k, the purchase changes.** The difference between leaving a child £500k and £2m is not opportunity — opportunity is saturated — it is *rank*: the ability to outbid other people's children for houses, positions, and safety margins. Naming this is uncomfortable and clarifying: the objection defends relative position, not provision ([`../03-case-for/equal-start.md`](../03-case-for/equal-start.md)).
- **Today's "certain" bequest is not certain.** Under the status quo their estate is already contingent on care costs (means-tested, uncapped in practice — to verify), longevity, markets, and their own late-life needs. The median expectation and the fantasy bequest should not be conflated; the proposal taxes the fantasy tail while *guaranteeing* the endowment floor.

## 4. What their family gains

The trade is not one-sided, and the gains land on their own descendants:

- **Endowments for every child and grandchild at 25**, unconditional, in perpetuity ([`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md)) — downside insurance this class quietly needs: one care-home decade, one divorce, one business failure separates the £2m family from the median, and the endowment does not care what happened to the family balance sheet.
- **The income-tax cuts** funded by the revenue ([`../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md`](../02-proposal/tax-interactions.md) §7) accrue across their working children's peak earning years.
- **A housing market with the dynastic bid removed**: their children currently compete against inherited deposits at the top of every chain ([`../01-problem/housing.md`](../01-problem/housing.md)); the mass-affluent family is a net *victim* of wealth stacked above it.
- **Their grandchildren compete on talent** in labour and education markets less distorted by trust-fund capital and nepotic placement ([`../07-implementation/nepotism-and-networks.md`](../07-implementation/nepotism-and-networks.md)). The mass affluent are meritocracy's actual constituency: prosperous enough to equip their children, nowhere near rich enough to exempt them from competing.

## 5. The honest losers

Estates of roughly £2m–£5m intended for one or two heirs pay materially more than under current law, even after endowments and tax cuts. Say so. The pitch to this band is a trade, not a denial: what they lose in bequest-stacking they gain in the items of §4, plus the system their grandchildren inherit — and the crossover table (§2) shows most of them a legal, honoured route (spreading across the family) that beats their current position. Some will refuse the trade; [coalition.md](coalition.md) already scores the top 1–5% as opponents, and the persuasion target is the far larger group *below* the crossover who currently believe, wrongly, that this tax is aimed at them. Correcting that belief — the division-by-recipients move of §1 — is worth more than converting any single £4m estate.

## 6. Conversation doctrine

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Ask "how much per child, realistically?" and let them do the division | Open with the 100% rate — it reads as total until the threshold lands |
| Concede the motive is love, immediately and sincerely | Moralise about their children's desert — that argument is for the £50m estate, not the £1.5m one ([framing.md](framing.md) "name the third party") |
| Show the spread-vs-stack rows of §2 | Imply the spread route is a loophole — it is the design working ([`../02-proposal/thresholds.md`](../02-proposal/thresholds.md) §7) |
| Use "you are closer to the endowment than to the dynasty" — their grandchildren's £50k is real money to this class | Pretend the £3m-two-kids case pays less; the audience can multiply |
| Anchor on P90 — "the allowance is more than 90% of heirs will ever see" [1] | Quote aggregate statistics about billionaires; they know they aren't billionaires and stop listening |

## Research gaps

- Commission the split-sample vignette polling of exactly this band ([polling.md](polling.md) P4: £250k/£500k/£1m with household vignettes) — the crossover table's persuasive power is testable.
- Verify current RNRB taper arithmetic and effective couple allowances used in §2 against HMRC guidance [3].

- Distribution of intended recipient counts (children, in-laws, grandchildren) among mass-affluent testators — determines how much of the band the spread route actually clears.
