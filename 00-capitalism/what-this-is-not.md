# What This Is Not

**Executive summary.** The reflex objection to a 100% inheritance tax is a category error: hearers file it with wealth taxes, income redistribution, and state ownership, when it is none of these — one rule change at one transaction, leaving every market mechanism and every living person's property untouched. This file is the calm statement of boundaries for the first-time reader; the debate-conditions version of the same material is [../09-objections/slippery-slope.md](../09-objections/slippery-slope.md).

## The table of distinctions

| This is NOT | Because | Where the design says so |
|---|---|---|
| **A wealth tax** | No annual levy on anyone's holdings. Nothing is assessed, valued, or taken from any living person's wealth at any point in their life. The tax event occurs only when capital passes to a new person, and only above that person's lifetime threshold | [../02-proposal/mechanism.md](../02-proposal/mechanism.md), [../03-case-for/wealth-tax-comparison.md](../03-case-for/wealth-tax-comparison.md) |
| **Income redistribution** | Market outcomes for the living stand in full. Earn a billion, keep a billion — every salary, capital gain, and founder's exit is governed by today's rules, not this proposal. The intervention is at the *start* of lives, not the results of them | [../03-case-for/equal-start.md](../03-case-for/equal-start.md) |
| **State ownership** | No asset passes into state hands. Estates above threshold are sold to private buyers or transferred in kind; proceeds flow through to citizens as the endowment — private capital, in private hands, privately deployed | [../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md](../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md), [../16-second-order/asset-prices.md](../16-second-order/asset-prices.md) |
| **Anti-success** | The founder keeps every penny for life — ownership, control, and use of everything built. Consumption at any scale, philanthropy at any scale, honours, reinvestment: all open. The one door that closes is dynastic succession above the threshold | [markets-vs-dynasties.md](markets-vs-dynasties.md) |
| **Anti-family** | Support, education, and £500k/$750k per child pass untaxed — more than most families who pay inheritance tax today can pass under current law, which is why the proposal is a tax *cut* for the typical taxpaying estate (to verify against [../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)) | [../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md), [../07-implementation/boundary-problem.md](../07-implementation/boundary-problem.md) |
| **Utopian central design** | One rule change at one transaction. No plan for the economy, no ministry of allocation, no new managed sector. The market — prices, competition, entry — does everything else, exactly as before | [what-capitalism-gets-right.md](what-capitalism-gets-right.md) |

## Why the reflex misfires

Each row shares a root: the objector assumes the tax operates on the *living economy*, when the design confines it to the *transmission between lives*. Every mechanism that makes capitalism productive — the price system, competitive entry, profit and loss, the founder's incentive — operates among living participants and is untouched by construction. The single transaction affected is the one that no market mechanism governs anyway: the unpriced, uncontested transfer to heirs ([markets-vs-dynasties.md](markets-vs-dynasties.md)). One cannot distort a market that was never there.

It is worth being equally plain about what the proposal *does* do, since candour is the currency of this project: above a generous threshold, it ends the ability to install one's children atop a great fortune. That is a real restriction on one use of property, and the honest liberty objection to it is steelmanned in [../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md](../04-case-against/liberty-autonomy.md) rather than defined away here. Our claim is not that the proposal costs nothing; it is that what it costs is the single least defensible transaction in the system, and what it buys is the system itself ([the-self-destruction-thesis.md](the-self-destruction-thesis.md)).

## And what it is — the same table, run forward

Boundary-setting invites the fair question: strip away the negatives and what remains? The affirmative counterpart of each row:

| This IS | In one sentence |
|---|---|
| A transfer tax at one event | 100% marginal rate on what any person *receives* above a £500k/$750k lifetime threshold — nothing else changes ([../02-proposal/mechanism.md](../02-proposal/mechanism.md)) |
| A universal inheritance | Every citizen receives £50k/$60k at 25, unconditional — the proceeds privatised to the young, not absorbed by the state ([../02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md)) |
| A tax cut for most current payers | Recipient thresholds above today's effective per-child allowances mean typical inheritance-taxpaying families pay less (to verify — [../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)) |
| Market-extending | It closes the last exemption from the contribution test and equips ~700,000 new contestants a year with starting capital (figure to verify — [markets-vs-dynasties.md](markets-vs-dynasties.md)) |
| Conservative in method | One rule, general and prospective, entrenched against caprice ([../02-proposal/constitutional-design.md](../02-proposal/constitutional-design.md)); no discretionary machinery, no ongoing programme to administer beyond collection and disbursement |

## The positive statement

Strip away what it is not, and what remains is this: **the completion of the liberal-capitalist settlement.**

Liberal capitalism has always justified unequal outcomes by appeal to a fair race — careers open to talents, rewards tracking contribution, each generation starting from the rule of law rather than the rule of birth. That justification quietly presupposes something the system has never delivered: an equal start. The presupposition is not incidental; it is what makes the rest of the argument work. Smith's system of natural liberty assumed contestants, not castes ([capitalist-supporters.md](capitalist-supporters.md)); the American founders abolished entail and primogeniture to prevent hereditary aristocracy ([../03-case-for/democratic-renewal.md](../03-case-for/democratic-renewal.md)); every defence of market inequality from Mill to Friedman leans on opportunity at the starting line ([../03-case-for/equal-start.md](../03-case-for/equal-start.md)).

The proposal supplies the missing piece. The recipient-based tax stops the compounding of unearned starting positions; the universal endowment gives every 25-year-old the capital stake the theory always imagined they had ([../02-proposal/endowment.md](../02-proposal/endowment.md)). Market outcomes then proceed exactly as before — but now with the legitimacy the system has been borrowing against for two centuries. This is not capitalism amended; it is capitalism *as advertised*.

## First-contact questions, briefly

Four questions arrive within minutes of any first hearing; the one-line answers, with their deep treatments:

- **"So I can't leave my house to my children?"** You can — a £500k threshold *per recipient* means an ordinary family home passes untaxed to one child, and a couple's estate splits across children and grandchildren well beyond most families' wealth ([../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)).
- **"What happens to the family firm?"** Deferral and instalment machinery keeps going concerns intact; no forced fire sales ([../02-proposal/carveouts.md](../02-proposal/carveouts.md), [../04-case-against/family-business.md](../04-case-against/family-business.md)).
- **"Won't everyone just give it away before death?"** Lifetime gifts count toward the same recipient ledger — the design is gift-integrated from the first clause, the lesson of every historical failure ([../02-proposal/avoidance.md](../02-proposal/avoidance.md), [../05-history/abolitions.md](../05-history/abolitions.md)).
- **"Where does the money go?"** Not into general government spending: hypothecated to the universal endowment, a design choice made precisely so the answer to this question is never "the Treasury" ([../02-proposal/revenue-model.md](../02-proposal/revenue-model.md), [../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md](../02-proposal/endowment-delivery.md)).

## The one-sentence positioning

For the movement, the line is:

> **The most capitalist policy never tried.**

Every element earns its word. *Capitalist*: it extends market discipline to the last untested transaction and equips every citizen to compete ([what-capitalism-gets-right.md](what-capitalism-gets-right.md)). *Never tried*: the historical high-rate regimes were estate-based, gift-leaky, and endowment-free — the design errors are catalogued in [../05-history/abolitions.md](../05-history/abolitions.md), and this proposal is engineered against each ([../02-proposal/avoidance.md](../02-proposal/avoidance.md)). *Most*: no other available policy removes hereditary allocation while leaving every price, wage, profit, and property right of the living untouched. The communications development of this positioning is owned by [../14-communications/naming-and-language.md](../14-communications/naming-and-language.md); the psychology of why the boundary-setting must come *before* the argument is in [../06-palatability/framing.md](../06-palatability/framing.md).

## Verification checklist

- The "tax cut for typical taxpaying families" claim: model current UK IHT incidence (nil-rate bands, residence band, spousal transfers) against per-recipient £500k thresholds — calculation owned by [../02-proposal/thresholds.md](../02-proposal/thresholds.md).
- The ~700,000 UK 25-year-olds per cohort figure (ONS population estimates).
- Confirmation that no element of the design as specified in [../02-proposal/](../02-proposal/README.md) involves state retention of assets beyond the disposal window.
