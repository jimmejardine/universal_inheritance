# 11 — Geographies: Who Adopts First, and Why

**Executive summary.** Scoring twenty-odd rich democracies against the ten criteria in [adoption-criteria.md](adoption-criteria.md) yields a clear headline: the **UK is the strongest single-country candidate** (majoritarian institutions, live inheritance-tax infrastructure, acute young-generation housing grievance, an adjacent-proposal think-tank ecosystem), with **Korea** the most probable rate-maximalist convergence, **Ireland** the standing proof that the recipient-based lifetime mechanism works, and the **Netherlands** the underrated continental dark horse. The section's governing paradox is that the most egalitarian societies — Sweden, Norway, Canada, Australia, New Zealand — are precisely the ones that abolished or never rebuilt their transfer taxes: institutions and infrastructure predict adoption; values do not.

## Headline findings

- **Institutions beat values.** The five criteria that best separate the top from the bottom of the table are administrative capacity, existing transfer-tax infrastructure, majoritarian legislative power, constitutional openness, and national-level assignment — not trust or egalitarianism. Weight campaigns accordingly ([adoption-criteria.md](adoption-criteria.md)).
- **The egalitarian paradox is a design lesson, not a mystery.** Sweden and Norway abolished badly designed taxes under exit fear that a multilateral floor would remove ([nordics.md](nordics.md), [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md), [`../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md`](../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md)).
- **Every mechanism in the proposal is running somewhere today.** Recipient basis and lifetime cumulation in Ireland; 50–55% top rates in Korea and Japan; death as a taxable event in Canada; per-heir computation in Japan; control-premium surcharges in Korea. Adoption is assembly, not invention.
- **No plausible candidate adopts the full design in one act.** The realistic path everywhere is stepping-stone convergence — receipts-basis reform, endowment pilots, rate escalation — timed to a crisis window ([crisis-windows.md](crisis-windows.md), [`../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md`](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md)).
- **Coordination is the multiplier.** Half the table (Ireland, the Nordics, Canada, NZ, the Benelux) moves only behind a UK/EU/OECD floor; the design's international architecture is therefore as adoption-critical as its domestic one ([`../08-capital-flight/game-theory.md`](../08-capital-flight/game-theory.md)).
