# 15 — Transition

**Executive summary.** The transition from today's estate-side taxes to a recipient-based 100% regime is where the policy is most vulnerable: a pre-announced change triggers a historic gift rush, grandfathering fights can hollow out the base for fifty years, and endowment outlays arrive before mature revenue does. Each of these risks is an engineering problem with a known answer — commencement-by-announcement, deemed-receipt on post-commencement trust distributions, and a phased revenue bridge — and this section is the policy engineer's plan for getting from current law to the full scheme in [`../02-proposal/`](../02-proposal/README.md) without dying en route.

## Why the transition is the danger zone

The abolition record ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)) shows inheritance taxes dying by erosion over decades. A *new* regime faces the mirror-image risk: dying in its first five years, from three specific timing failures.

| Failure mode | Mechanism | Engineering answer | File |
|---|---|---|---|
| Announcement rush | Gap between announcement and commencement invites mass forestalling gifts, gutting the early base and the narrative | Commencement-by-announcement; lookback aggregation; lifetime cumulation itself | [announcement-effects.md](announcement-effects.md) |
| Grandfathering capture | "Settled expectations" arguments exempt pre-commencement trusts and gifts, creating a multi-decade hole | Distributions after commencement are receipts regardless of settlement date | [grandfathering.md](grandfathering.md) |
| Revenue-before-liability gap | Endowment outlays start at commencement; 100%-rate receipts mature only as deaths occur over decades | Phased endowment, hypothecated borrowing, transitional revenue | [revenue-bridge.md](revenue-bridge.md) |

A fourth, slower failure — trying to legislate the whole scheme in one act, in one parliament — is answered by sequencing: the reform is a ladder of separately defensible steps, not a single leap ([sequencing.md](sequencing.md)).

## Scope of this section

This section covers *policy* sequencing and *legislative* mechanics. It deliberately excludes:

## The one-sentence position

The transition is survivable if — and only if — it is designed *before* announcement: every anti-forestalling rule, grandfathering boundary, and revenue-bridge instrument must be in the first published draft, because retrofitting them after the avoidance industry has moved is twice as hard and looks like panic.
