# Sequencing: The Reform Ladder

**Executive summary.** The full scheme cannot and should not be legislated in one act: it is a five-phase ladder in which each rung is independently defensible, builds the administrative capacity the next rung requires, and — critically — makes the eventual 100% rate a parameter change rather than a new tax. Honest estimate of full transit: 10–20 years, with the first two phases deliverable inside a single parliament.

This is the *policy* sequence — what must be built in what order for the machine to work. The *political* cover for each step (why a government would take it, what it can be sold as on its own terms) is treated in [`../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md`](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md); the two documents describe the same ladder from different sides.

## The ladder at a glance

| Phase | Content | Depends on | Indicative duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Infrastructure: receipts register, beneficial-ownership completion, valuation capacity | Nothing — can start now | 2–4 years, overlapping Phase 1 |
| 1 | Basis switch: estate-based IHT → recipient-based receipts tax (Ireland model), revenue-neutral | Phase 0 register at least legislated | 1 parliament |
| 2 | Threshold-and-rate reshaping: 0% below £500k lifetime, rising schedule above | Phase 1 in operation, one full data cycle | 2–5 years after Phase 1 |
| 3 | Rate → 100% above threshold; endowment launch | Phase 2 revenue data; endowment delivery machinery | 3–8 years after Phase 2 |
| 4 | International floor: multilateral coordination on transfer taxation | Phase 3 domestically stable; willing partners | Open-ended; pursued in parallel from Phase 2 |

## Phase 0 — Infrastructure

**What it is.** Three capacities that the end-state regime consumes but that are valuable under *any* tax policy, and can therefore be built without announcing the destination:

1. **Central Register of Receipts** — the lifetime ledger specified in [`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §6, initially populated from probate, Land Registry, and Companies House feeds. Can be launched as a compliance tool for the *existing* IHT gift rules (the current seven-year lookback already requires exactly this information; it is simply not collected systematically).
2. **Beneficial-ownership completion** — closing the gaps in the register of overseas entities holding UK property, trust registration, and nominee disclosure. Already the direction of travel post-2022; see [`../07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md`](../07-implementation/trusts-and-vehicles.md).
3. **Valuation capacity** — HMRC's Valuation Office and its US analogues staffed and proceduralised for volume appraisal of unlisted business interests and real property; statutory appraisal methodologies published. See [`../07-implementation/valuation.md`](../07-implementation/valuation.md).

**What breaks if skipped.** Everything downstream. A recipient-based tax without a receipts ledger is unadministrable; the Austrian abolition ([`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)) shows a valuation regime failing can kill the tax by judicial route. Phase 0 is also the cheapest phase to do badly and the most expensive to redo.

**Duration.** 2–4 years to operational; register back-population is incremental thereafter.

## Phase 1 — The basis switch

**What it is.** Replace estate-side IHT with a recipient-based tax on receipts, at rates and thresholds calibrated to be approximately **revenue-neutral** — deliberately *not* a tax rise. Ireland's Capital Acquisitions Tax is the operating model; the Mirrlees Review's lifetime-receipts recommendation is the intellectual authority. Existing reliefs are translated, not yet reformed.

**Why this rung matters most.** The basis switch is the load-bearing move of the entire programme. Once tax follows the *recipient*, three things become true that were false before: (i) splitting an estate among many recipients reduces tax, aligning incentives with dispersal; (ii) the "death tax on the deceased" framing collapses — see [`../14-communications/naming-and-language.md`](../14-communications/naming-and-language.md); (iii) rate and threshold become per-person parameters that later phases can move without structural legislation.

**What breaks if skipped.** Attempting to jump straight to a 100% *estate* tax repeats every design error catalogued in [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md) — the framing, the incidence problems, the lot. There is no viable path that skips this rung.

**Duration.** One Finance-Bill cycle to legislate (see [legislative-path-uk.md](legislative-path-uk.md)); 2–3 years to bed in.

## Phase 2 — Threshold-and-rate reshaping

**What it is.** Move to the end-state *shape*: a 0% band up to the £500k/$750k lifetime threshold, then a rising schedule above (illustratively 20% → 45% → 60% by tranche), with automatic indexation locked in ([`../02-proposal/thresholds.md`](../02-proposal/thresholds.md)).

**The under-appreciated fact.** At a £500k per-recipient threshold, Phase 2 is a **tax cut for the large majority of people who currently pay UK IHT** — estates around the current £325k–£1m nil-rate-band boundary, typically split among two or more children, fall entirely into the 0% band (share to verify by micro-simulation). The phase can be presented, accurately, as "taking ordinary families out of inheritance tax for good, paid for by those inheriting millions." This is the single best political rung on the ladder, which is why the political treatment in [`../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md`](../12-political-adoption/stepping-stones.md) centres on it.

**What breaks if skipped.** Jumping from Phase 1 rates straight to 100% concentrates the entire distributional shock in one announcement, maximising the forestalling rush ([announcement-effects.md](announcement-effects.md)) and the opposition's mobilisation window ([`../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md`](../10-opposition/attack-playbook.md)). The rising schedule also generates the revenue and behavioural data needed to calibrate Phase 3.

**Duration.** 2–5 years after Phase 1, including one full assessment cycle of Phase-1 data.

## Phase 3 — 100% and the endowment

**What it is.** The top marginal rate moves to 100%; simultaneously — not later — the universal endowment launches ([`../02-proposal/endowment.md`](../02-proposal/endowment.md)). The pairing is non-negotiable: the endowment is the structural defence against the abolition pattern, and launching the confiscatory rate without its visible beneficiary recreates the exact political economy that killed the twentieth-century taxes.

**What it needs.** Endowment delivery machinery (a DWP/Treasury payment channel and the fund vehicle in [revenue-bridge.md](revenue-bridge.md)) built and tested — ideally piloted ([pilots.md](pilots.md)) — before the rate change; the anti-forestalling package in [announcement-effects.md](announcement-effects.md) enacted *with* the rate change; the grandfathering boundary ([grandfathering.md](grandfathering.md)) settled in the same act.

**What breaks if skipped.** Nothing — this *is* the destination. The failure mode is doing it too early: before the register can cumulate lifetime gifts, a 100% rate is a sieve.

**Duration.** Legislated in one act; revenue matures over the following two decades ([revenue-bridge.md](revenue-bridge.md)).

## Phase 4 — The international floor

**What it is.** A multilateral minimum on transfer taxation, analogous to the 2021 corporate global-minimum-tax agreement, to blunt the exile option. Pursued in parallel from Phase 2 onward, not as a precondition — the domestic exit-tax and situs rules ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md), [`../08-capital-flight/exit-tax-design.md`](../08-capital-flight/exit-tax-design.md)) are designed to hold unilaterally. See [`../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md`](../08-capital-flight/international-coordination.md).

**What breaks if skipped.** The regime survives but leaks at the top end; the Kamprad/Rausing narrative risk documented in the Swedish abolition remains live.

## Honest timing

Adding the phases: **10–20 years from first infrastructure spend to a mature 100% regime**, spanning three to five parliaments. This is not a weakness to be hidden; it is the normal tempo of structural tax reform (the UK's shift from estate duty through CTT to IHT took from 1974 to 1986 merely to *rearrange* the old architecture). Two consequences follow. First, each rung must be worth having even if the ladder stops there — Phases 0–2 all pass that test. Second, the programme must survive changes of government, which is a design constraint on framing and coalition, treated in [`../12-political-adoption/`](../12-political-adoption/README.md) and [`../13-movement/theory-of-change.md`](../13-movement/theory-of-change.md).
