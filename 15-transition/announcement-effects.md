# Announcement Effects: The Forestalling Problem

**Executive summary.** Any gap between announcing the regime and commencing it invites a historic rush of inter-vivos gifts and restructurings designed to move wealth outside the base before the door closes — potentially the largest voluntary wealth transfer in national history, compressed into months. The toolkit is well established — commencement-by-announcement, lookback aggregation, transitional rates — and, less obviously, the lifetime-cumulation design itself defuses most timing games before they start.

## Why forestalling is the first attack

A confiscatory rate above a threshold creates, for every household above that threshold, an enormous one-off return to acting *before* commencement. Unlike ongoing avoidance ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md)), forestalling requires no cleverness: an outright, legally unimpeachable gift made the day before commencement escapes entirely under a naive design. The behavioural response to *pre-announced* tax changes is among the best-documented phenomena in public finance:

- **Pre-announced CGT changes (UK).** Rate changes announced in advance of a tax-year boundary have repeatedly produced large disposal spikes in the intervening window, visible in HMRC receipts data (to verify against specific episodes, e.g. 1988 rebasing announcement and the 2010 emergency-Budget rate rise, where the *avoidance* of a gap — the 2010 change took effect at midnight — was itself the lesson).
- **Pre-announced IHT/estate-duty changes.** The introduction of the UK's Capital Transfer Tax was announced in 1974 with lifetime gifts brought into charge *from the announcement White Paper*, precisely because the government anticipated a gift rush (to verify against the 1974 Finance Act record).
- **Australia pre-abolition.** The mirror image: after Queensland announced abolition of death duties (effective 1977), deaths and asset transfers were reportedly timed and structured around the effective dates, and interstate migration of the elderly wealthy accelerated (to verify; see [`../05-history/abolitions.md`](../05-history/abolitions.md)). Behaviour responds to *any* pre-announced discontinuity, in either direction.
- **US estate-tax sunset (2010).** The one-year repeal window created by the 2001 Act generated documented estate-planning gymnastics and a macabre literature on death-timing elasticity (to verify against Kopczuk–Slemrod line of work). See [legislative-path-us.md](legislative-path-us.md).

The conclusion: assume the rush, size it as historic, and design for it in the first published draft.

## The anti-forestalling toolkit

| Instrument | What it does | Cost / objection |
|---|---|---|
| Commencement-by-announcement | Tax applies to transfers made on or after announcement day (Budget day), with legislation following | Standard UK practice via Budget resolutions; taxes on trust and legal advice available same-day |
| Lookback aggregation | Gifts made in the N years before commencement count against the recipient's lifetime threshold (not taxed, but consume headroom) | The retrospectivity-ethics question — see below |
| Transitional rate on rushed gifts | Gifts between announcement and commencement taxed at an intermediate rate rather than 0% or 100% | Blunts but does not eliminate the incentive; adds complexity |
| Deemed-receipt backstop | Assets given pre-commencement but with retained benefit (donor still occupies the house, controls the company) treated as received at commencement | Extends existing gift-with-reservation doctrine; litigable boundary |

### Commencement-by-announcement

The UK already operates this machinery routinely: anti-avoidance measures take effect from the moment of the ministerial statement, under the Provisional Collection of Taxes Act 1968 and Budget-resolution practice (see [legislative-path-uk.md](legislative-path-uk.md)). For the Phase 3 rate change ([sequencing.md](sequencing.md)), the rule is simple: **transfers on or after announcement day are within the new regime.** This closes the front door. It does not close the window *before* announcement, which leaks whenever the policy is foreseeable — a manifesto commitment ([legislative-path-uk.md](legislative-path-uk.md), Salisbury discussion) is itself an announcement to the avoidance industry.

### Lookback aggregation

Therefore: gifts received in the **N years before commencement** (proposal: N = 7, matching the existing IHT potentially-exempt-transfer lookback so that no one can claim the horizon was unforeseeable) are entered on the recipient's ledger and **consume threshold headroom**, though they are not themselves taxed. A recipient who took £2m in pre-commencement gifts starts the regime with zero headroom; the gift stands, but its shelter value is spent.

**The retrospectivity-ethics discussion.** Is lookback aggregation retrospective taxation? The defensible line: it does not impose tax on a completed past transaction (the pre-commencement gift bears no charge); it uses past receipts as *information* in computing a future liability — exactly as the current IHT seven-year rule reaches back to gifts made before a death, and as means-testing reaches back to past income. Legitimate-expectation and ECHR Article 1 Protocol 1 analysis is developed in [grandfathering.md](grandfathering.md) and [`../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md`](../10-opposition/legal-challenges.md); the short version is that headroom-consumption is far more robust than any attempt to tax the past transfers themselves, and the design should stay on the robust side of that line.

### Transitional rates

If political circumstances force a announced-but-delayed commencement (e.g. systems readiness), a transitional schedule — gifts in the gap taxed at, say, the Phase 2 top rate rather than 0% — converts the cliff into a slope. This is a second-best tool: it concedes the gap exists. The first-best is not to have a gap.

## Why the design itself defuses most timing games

The deeper answer is structural, and it is the reason this section is shorter than opponents expect. Under **lifetime cumulation** ([`../02-proposal/mechanism.md`](../02-proposal/mechanism.md) §2):

1. **There is no annual clock to game.** Classical gift taxes with annual exemptions and n-year survival rules reward *timing*; a lifetime ledger is indifferent to when a receipt arrives. A gift at 25 and a bequest at 60 draw on the same stock of headroom.
2. **Rushing merely re-times, it does not reduce.** Once the lookback rule is in force, a pre-commencement rush consumes the same headroom the eventual bequest would have. The net saving from rushing approaches zero for anyone inside the lookback window.
3. **Dispersal, the one remaining strategy, is the intended behaviour.** The only durable way to reduce total tax is to spread wealth across more recipients, each with their own £500k threshold. A forestalling rush that scatters a £10m fortune across twenty recipients is not avoidance; it is the policy working early. See [`../03-case-for/ethical.md`](../03-case-for/ethical.md).

What survives is the pre-announcement window before the lookback horizon — transfers made 8+ years before a foreseeable commencement. That leakage is real, bounded, one-generation-only, and shrinking: it cannot recur once the regime exists, and the recipients of rushed gifts carry spent headroom into the rest of their lives.

## Residual risks

- **Trust settlement rush.** Assets moved into discretionary trusts pre-commencement, hoping to distribute never or later. Answered by the deemed-receipt-on-distribution rule in [grandfathering.md](grandfathering.md), which is the single most important transition provision in the entire scheme.
- **Emigration rush.** Donors or recipients leaving before commencement. Answered by the exit tax and 10-year tail ([`../02-proposal/avoidance.md`](../02-proposal/avoidance.md)); evidence on how much actually moves is in [`../08-capital-flight/evidence.md`](../08-capital-flight/evidence.md).
- **Valuation-date gaming.** Suppressing appraised values during the transition window; treated in [`../07-implementation/valuation.md`](../07-implementation/valuation.md) and [`../07-implementation/gifts-and-timing.md`](../07-implementation/gifts-and-timing.md).
- **Narrative risk.** The rush itself becomes the story — "capital flees before confiscation." Inoculation strategy in [`../14-communications/inoculation.md`](../14-communications/inoculation.md): pre-announce the *expectation* of a rush and publish the lookback rule with the first draft, so the rush reads as anticipated and priced in, not as the plan unravelling.
